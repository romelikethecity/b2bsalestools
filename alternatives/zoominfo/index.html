Best ZoomInfo Alternatives (2026)

ZoomInfo's $15K+ annual contracts and auto-renewal policies push many teams to find alternatives.

Last updated: 2026-05-06

Best ZoomInfo Alternatives

The top ZoomInfo alternatives are Apollo.io, Cognism, Lusha, RocketReach. Teams switch from ZoomInfo due to pricing, feature gaps, or workflow fit.

Why Teams Leave ZoomInfo

ZoomInfo is the dominant B2B contact database, and its pricing reflects that position. Annual contracts start at $15K for smaller teams and climb well past $40K for mid-market packages. Enterprise deals regularly exceed $100K per year. These are annual commitments with auto-renewal clauses that kick in 30-60 days before the contract end date. Teams that miss the cancellation window get locked in for another year at a higher price. The sales process itself is opaque: no public pricing page, mandatory demos, and quotes that vary wildly based on perceived willingness to pay. Multiple customers report receiving quotes 2-3x apart for similar seat counts, depending on how the sales conversation went.

Data quality is strong for North American enterprise contacts, but coverage drops off for SMB targets, international markets, and newer companies. Teams prospecting into small businesses or companies outside the US often find that ZoomInfo's database has significant gaps in these segments. Email bounce rates for SMB contacts can run 15-25%, compared to 5-10% for enterprise records. At $15K+/year, those gaps sting. Direct dial accuracy is a similar story: ZoomInfo's phone numbers are reliable for VP-level and above at mid-market and enterprise companies, but coverage thins out for individual contributors and smaller organizations.

The platform has expanded aggressively into sales engagement, conversation intelligence, and marketing features. ZoomInfo acquired Chorus for conversation intelligence and built out SalesOS, MarketingOS, and TalentOS as separate product lines. This bundling means you may be paying for capabilities you already have in other tools. Teams that only need contact data end up subsidizing features they never touch. The product has become a platform play, and that platform tax shows up in every invoice.

Contract negotiations with ZoomInfo follow a predictable pattern. Initial quotes come in high. Pushing back yields discounts of 20-40%, but only if you negotiate firmly and have competing quotes in hand. Multi-year deals lock in pricing but reduce your flexibility to switch. The renewal process is where most teams feel the squeeze: ZoomInfo's standard renewal includes a 5-15% price increase, and the auto-renewal clause means silence equals consent. Teams that want to negotiate need to start the conversation 90+ days before the renewal date.

Recent changes to ZoomInfo's credit system have also frustrated users. Export credits, which control how many contacts you can pull from the database, are limited by tier. Teams that hit their credit ceiling mid-quarter face a choice: stop prospecting or pay for additional credits at premium rates. The credit structure creates an artificial scarcity that pushes teams toward higher-priced plans even when their seat count stays the same.

Who should stay with ZoomInfo? Organizations with 50+ reps targeting North American enterprise accounts, where data depth (technographics, org charts, intent signals) directly impacts deal velocity. Teams running account-based marketing programs that depend on ZoomInfo's intent data and company-level insights also get outsized value. If your average deal size is $50K+ and ZoomInfo helps you close even one additional deal per quarter, the ROI math works despite the high price.

Who should leave? Teams under 20 reps, companies targeting SMB or international markets, and organizations that primarily need email addresses and phone numbers without the platform extras. If your ZoomInfo usage report shows that 80% of your activity is basic contact lookups, you are dramatically overpaying for a feature set you do not use.

Apollo.io offers a comparable contact database with 270M+ contacts and built-in email sequencing at a fraction of ZoomInfo's cost. The free tier includes 10,000 monthly export credits. Paid plans start at $49/user/month, making it accessible to individual reps and small teams who cannot justify a $15K annual contract. Data accuracy trails ZoomInfo slightly for enterprise contacts but matches or exceeds it for SMB and startup segments. Apollo's strength is the all-in-one approach: you get contact data, email verification, sequencing, and a basic CRM in a single platform. For teams that want to start prospecting within an hour of signing up, Apollo's zero-configuration approach is a stark contrast to ZoomInfo's mandatory demo and multi-week procurement process. The pricing difference is dramatic. A 10-person team on Apollo pays roughly $6,000/year versus $15K-$25K on ZoomInfo. That savings funds other tools or simply drops to the bottom line. Apollo's database is particularly strong for tech companies, SaaS, and digital-first businesses. Where it falls short is traditional industries (manufacturing, healthcare, finance) and verified direct dials for senior executives. Teams that rely heavily on phone outreach to C-suite contacts will notice the gap. Apollo also includes email sequencing that eliminates the need for a separate engagement tool like Outreach or Salesloft. For teams running straightforward multi-step email campaigns, this consolidation saves both money and complexity. The sequencing features lack the governance controls and A/B testing depth of enterprise platforms, but for teams under 30 reps, the simplicity is a net positive.

Cognism is the strongest ZoomInfo alternative for European and international prospecting. Their phone-verified mobile number database is a standout feature, with direct dials verified by a human research team through what they call Diamond Data. GDPR compliance is built into the platform at every level, which matters for teams selling into the EU where data privacy regulations carry real enforcement risk. Pricing is enterprise-level, typically $15K-$30K/year depending on seat count and data access, but the international data quality justifies the investment for teams with global territories. The Diamond Data verification process is what separates Cognism from every other provider. A human researcher calls the phone number to confirm it reaches the right person before marking it as verified. This produces connect rates of 80-95% on verified numbers, compared to 20-40% on unverified numbers from most databases. For phone-heavy outbound teams, this verification alone can justify the cost. If your SDRs spend half their day dialing wrong numbers, Cognism's verified data reclaims that time. Cognism also partners with Bombora for intent data, giving you buying signals alongside contact information. The platform integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, and Salesloft. The main limitation compared to ZoomInfo is North American coverage depth: ZoomInfo has more US contacts, deeper technographic data, and more comprehensive org charts for domestic enterprise accounts. Teams that sell primarily in North America get more value from ZoomInfo. Teams with any meaningful international territory get more value from Cognism.

Lusha is a lightweight, affordable option for teams that need contact data without a platform. The browser extension pulls emails and direct dials from LinkedIn profiles in seconds. Pricing starts at $36/user/month with a usable free tier that includes 5 credits per month. The Pro plan at $36/user/month includes 40 credits, and the Premium plan at $59/user/month bumps that to 80 credits. For reps who prospect directly from LinkedIn, Lusha's workflow is hard to beat: find a prospect, click the extension, get contact info, push to CRM. Lusha lacks the company-level firmographic data, intent signals, and technographic information that ZoomInfo provides. There are no org charts, no buying committee maps, and no account-level intelligence. For teams whose primary need is email addresses and phone numbers for individual contacts, Lusha delivers 80% of the value at 10% of the cost. The simplicity is the product: no training required, no complex search filters, no credit negotiations. The data quality is solid for email addresses, with reported accuracy rates around 80-85%. Phone number coverage is more limited than ZoomInfo or Cognism, particularly for mobile numbers. Lusha works best as a complement to LinkedIn Sales Navigator rather than a standalone prospecting database. Teams that already have a system for finding prospects (LinkedIn, conferences, referrals) and just need contact details find Lusha is the most efficient path from "I know who I want to reach" to "I have their email and phone number."

RocketReach focuses on email finding with a database of 700M+ profiles. It excels at finding personal email addresses and direct dials for hard-to-reach contacts, including professionals who are not active on LinkedIn or who work at companies with limited web presence. Plans start at $53/month for individual users, with team plans offering bulk lookups and API access. The interface is simple and search-focused without the platform overhead that comes with ZoomInfo's expanding product suite. RocketReach's database spans a wider range of industries and geographies than many competitors. The 700M+ profile count includes professionals outside the typical B2B tech bubble, which makes it valuable for teams targeting healthcare, education, government, and non-profit sectors. Email accuracy runs around 85-90% for business emails, with personal email addresses available as a fallback when business emails are unavailable. This personal email option is useful for reaching founders, consultants, and executives at small companies that do not have standardized corporate email. The main limitation is the lack of engagement features. RocketReach finds contact information and that is the entire product. No sequencing, no CRM, no intent data, no company intelligence. Teams that need accurate contact info for targeted outreach rather than bulk list-building find RocketReach effective and affordable. Pair it with a CRM and an engagement tool, and the total stack cost often comes in well under ZoomInfo while providing comparable contact accuracy for targeted searches. RocketReach also offers a well-documented API for technical teams that want to build enrichment into custom workflows or internal tools.

Migration Tips

Switching from ZoomInfo starts with understanding your actual usage patterns. Pull your ZoomInfo usage reports for the past 6 months. Look at how many contacts you exported, which search filters you used most, and how many users actually logged in regularly. Many teams discover that only 30-50% of their licensed seats are active users. This data informs both the tool you switch to and the number of seats you actually need.

Export your ZoomInfo search history, saved lists, and any custom data enrichment before your contract ends. ZoomInfo does not make it easy to extract data after your access is cut off. Download saved searches as CSV files. Export your target account lists, contact lists, and any custom segments you have built. This data represents months of prospecting work that has value regardless of which tool you use next.

Time your transition around your contract renewal. ZoomInfo's auto-renewal clauses mean you need to send written cancellation notice 60+ days before the end date. Some contracts require 90 days. Read the fine print. Send cancellation via email to your account rep and their contracts team, and request written confirmation of receipt. Do not rely on a verbal acknowledgment. If the cancellation window passes without written notice, you are locked in for another year.

Start evaluating alternatives 3-4 months before renewal. Run parallel trials with your top 2-3 candidates, testing data accuracy against your actual target accounts. Have reps search for the same 50-100 contacts across tools and compare hit rates, email accuracy, and phone number quality. Real testing beats vendor demos every time. Build a simple spreadsheet: contact name, company, ZoomInfo result, alternative tool result, verified accuracy. This gives you hard data for the buying decision.

During the transition, plan for a 2-4 week overlap period where your team has access to both ZoomInfo and the new tool. This parallel run catches gaps early. Some reps will find contacts that exist in ZoomInfo but not in the new tool. Track these misses to understand whether the gap is a deal-breaker or an acceptable trade-off for the cost savings. Most teams find that 85-95% of their daily lookups work fine in the new tool, and the remaining 5-15% can be handled with a secondary data source or manual research. Document these misses to build a case for or against adding a supplementary data tool later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which ZoomInfo alternative has the best data quality?

It depends on your target market. For North American enterprise contacts, Cognism and Apollo come closest to ZoomInfo's accuracy. For European markets, Cognism is stronger due to GDPR-compliant data collection and phone verification. For SMB and startup contacts, Apollo often has better coverage than ZoomInfo. For hard-to-reach individuals across industries, RocketReach's 700M+ profile database casts a wider net. Test with your actual target accounts rather than relying on vendor-reported database sizes. Database size numbers are marketing metrics, not quality indicators. A smaller, more accurate database outperforms a larger one filled with outdated records.

Can I get ZoomInfo-quality data for under $5K/year?

Yes. Apollo's paid plans start at $49/user/month ($588/year) and include a large contact database with email sequencing. Lusha starts at $36/user/month. Neither matches ZoomInfo's full feature set (intent data, org charts, technographics), but for core contact lookup and list building, these tools deliver strong results at a fraction of the price. A 5-person team on Apollo costs roughly $3,000/year versus $15K+ on ZoomInfo.

How do I cancel my ZoomInfo contract?

Check your contract for the auto-renewal clause and cancellation notice period. Most ZoomInfo contracts require written notice 30-60 days before the renewal date, though some require 90 days. Send cancellation via email to your account rep and their contracts team. Copy your finance or legal department on the email. Request written confirmation that your cancellation was received and processed. Save all correspondence. Missing the window means another full year at a potentially higher rate. If you are past the cancellation window, consult your legal team about options.

Is Apollo.io good enough to replace ZoomInfo?

For teams under 50 reps doing outbound prospecting, Apollo replaces 80-90% of ZoomInfo's daily-use functionality. You get contact search, email verification, and sequencing in one tool. Where Apollo falls short is enterprise-level intent data, advanced org charts, and the depth of technographic data. If those features drive your workflow, evaluate Cognism or 6sense alongside Apollo. If your reps primarily search for contacts, grab emails, and start outreach, Apollo covers that workflow at roughly one-fifth the cost.

Do any ZoomInfo alternatives include intent data?

Cognism offers intent data powered by Bombora, integrated directly into the contact search workflow. Apollo has a basic intent signal feature on higher tiers. For dedicated intent data comparable to ZoomInfo's, most teams pair a contact data tool (Apollo, Lusha) with a standalone intent platform (Bombora, 6sense, or Demandbase). This unbundled approach often provides better intent coverage at a lower total cost than ZoomInfo's bundled offering.

How long does it take to switch from ZoomInfo?

Plan for 4-6 weeks total. Weeks 1-2: run parallel trials with 2-3 alternatives, testing data accuracy on your target accounts. Week 3: make a decision and begin team onboarding. Weeks 4-5: parallel run with both tools active. Week 6: full cutover. The data migration itself is fast (CSV exports and imports). The time is mostly spent validating data quality and building team confidence in the new tool.

What do ZoomInfo customers complain about most?

Three issues dominate customer complaints. First, pricing and renewal tactics: opaque quotes, aggressive auto-renewal clauses, and annual price increases of 5-15%. Second, credit limitations that force teams to upgrade plans mid-contract. Third, data accuracy for SMB contacts and international markets, where bounce rates and wrong numbers are noticeably higher than for enterprise US contacts. Support response times have also drawn criticism, particularly for teams on lower-tier plans.

Can I use multiple data providers instead of ZoomInfo?

Yes, and many teams find this approach provides better coverage at lower cost. A common stack is Apollo for primary contact search ($49/user/month), Lusha for LinkedIn-based lookups ($36/user/month for targeted searches), and a tool like Clay or FullEnrich for waterfall enrichment on hard-to-find contacts. The combined cost for a 10-person team runs $6K-$10K/year versus $15K-$40K for ZoomInfo, with comparable or better coverage across segments.

Does ZoomInfo own my data if I export it?

ZoomInfo's terms of service restrict how you can use exported data. You can use it for your own sales outreach, but you cannot resell it, share it with third parties, or use it to build a competing database. Once you cancel your contract, you retain the data you already exported, but you lose access to the platform for new searches and updates. Export everything you need before your contract ends. The exported data degrades over time as contacts change jobs and emails become invalid, so do not treat a ZoomInfo export as a permanent database. Plan to re-enrich contacts through your new provider every 6-12 months. B2B contact data decays at roughly 30% per year due to job changes, company moves, and email address updates.

Are ZoomInfo's direct dials accurate?

ZoomInfo's direct dial accuracy runs 60-70% for enterprise contacts (VP and above at mid-market and large companies). Accuracy drops to 40-50% for individual contributors and SMB contacts. Cognism's Diamond Data verified numbers outperform ZoomInfo on connect rates, but at a comparable price point. If phone outreach is central to your strategy, test dial accuracy on 50+ numbers in your target segment before committing to any provider.
