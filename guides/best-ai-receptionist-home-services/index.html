What to Look For

Six criteria matter for AI receptionist selection.

**Voice quality on real customer calls.** Test the AI by calling as a customer with complex scenarios. Bad AI is a customer-experience disaster.

**FSM integration depth.** AI that does not write call data into your FSM creates extra office-staff work. Verify integration before buying.

**Multi-trade vocabulary handling.** Trade-specific terms (HVAC: heat pump, condenser; plumbing: traps, p-trap, water heater; electrical: panel, breaker, GFCI) matter for natural conversation. Trade-trained AI handles these better.

**After-hours coverage strategy.** Most home services operations adopt AI for after-hours and overflow first, then expand to business-hours coverage. Vendors that handle this graduated rollout cleanly fit the typical adoption pattern.

**Spanish-language support.** 18% of US households speak Spanish. Higher in TX, FL, CA, AZ.

**Pricing model fit.** Subscription is predictable; usage-based scales with call volume. For seasonal trades (HVAC, lawn care), usage-based can either reduce off-season cost or spike during peak.

Pricing Scenarios

**Solo to 3-tech home services:** Trillet $49/mo, Rosie $49-$99/mo, or Goodcall Starter $59/mo. All-in: $600-$1,200/year.

**5-15 person operation:** Goodcall Growth $99/mo or Rosie higher tier $149/mo or Hatch SMB tier $500-$1,000/mo. All-in: $1,200-$12,000/year.

**Mid-large operation on ServiceTitan:** Avoca custom usage-based $2,000-$5,000+/mo. All-in: $25,000-$60,000+/year.

What to Avoid

**Generic consumer AI for customer-facing calls.** Voice quality and trade-specific knowledge matter.

**Skipping the voice quality test.** Always call the AI as a customer before buying.

**Underestimating integration importance.** AI without FSM integration creates more office-staff work, not less.

**Buying based on lowest price alone.** $49/mo Trillet is fine for very small operations but voice quality and integration depth differences matter as the operation grows.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which AI receptionist is best for SMB home services?

Goodcall ($59-$199/mo) for configurability and SMB pricing, Rosie ($49-$149/mo) for trade-trained voice quality. Both fit the typical 1-15 person home services operation on Jobber, Housecall Pro, or FieldEdge. Pick based on whether you value configurability (Goodcall) or trade-specific voice quality (Rosie).

When does Avoca's premium pricing pay back?

For ServiceTitan-integrated operations doing $3M+ revenue. The deeper integration writes call data into ServiceTitan natively (no manual entry), voice quality is highest in category, and the per-call recovered-revenue math compounds at higher operation sizes. Below $3M revenue, Goodcall or Rosie deliver most of the value at much lower cost.

How does AI receptionist compare to a third-party answering service?

AI receptionist typically wins on cost and consistency. Third-party answering services (Ruby Receptionists, similar) charge $300-$800+/month for human answering with variable quality and inconsistent availability. AI receptionist services run cheaper, never call in sick, scale better during peak season, and increasingly match human voice quality. The trade-off: AI is less flexible on complex customer questions, so escalation logic matters.

Will my customers prefer AI or a human voice?

Most customers do not notice the difference at modern voice quality, especially for routine calls (scheduling, basic questions, service requests). Older customers and customers with complex emergency situations sometimes prefer human escalation. Strong vendors (Avoca, Goodcall, Hatch, Rosie) all handle graceful escalation when AI is not the right fit for the call.

Can AI receptionist handle Spanish-speaking customers?

Quality varies by vendor. Avoca, Goodcall, and Hatch all support Spanish with reasonable quality. Lower-tier vendors are less polished in Spanish than English. For trades operations in markets with significant Spanish-speaking customer bases (TX, FL, CA, AZ, Southwest generally), test Spanish-language quality specifically before buying.
