Best Sales Tools for Email Warmup & Deliverability Tools (2026)

You bought 10 new domains, set up DKIM, SPF, and DMARC, and you're ready to send cold email. Not so fast. New domains have zero sender reputation, and sending cold email from a fresh inbox is the fastest way to land in spam. Email warmup tools fix this by automatically sending and replying to emails from your new accounts, building sender reputation over 2-3 weeks before you start real outreach. Some cold email platforms include warmup for free. Others charge extra. And standalone warmup tools exist for teams using engagement platforms that don't include it. Here's what works.

Best Sales Tools for Email Warmup & Deliverability Tools

The best sales tools for email warmup & deliverability tools span 2 categories, from prospecting and outreach to deal management and analytics. Tool selection depends on your sales motion, team size, and budget.

We reviewed 5 tools across 2 categories to build this guide. Every recommendation is based on hands-on testing, published pricing data, and feedback from teams that actually use these tools daily. No vendor paid for placement.

This guide covers Built-In, and Standalone. Each section includes our top picks with scores, pricing, and honest takes on where each tool falls short. The goal isn't to list every option. It's to narrow the field to the tools worth your time.

Pricing in this space changes constantly. We verify rates quarterly, but always confirm directly with vendors before making a purchase decision. Enterprise pricing is almost always negotiable.

If you're building a stack from scratch, start with one tool per category and add complexity only when you've outgrown the basics. The most expensive mistake isn't picking the wrong tool. It's buying five tools when you needed two.

How These Tools Work Together

The typical workflow moves through these stages: Built-In → Standalone. Most teams don't need a separate tool for every stage on day one. Start with the highest-impact gap in your current process and work outward from there.

Integration matters more than individual features. A tool that connects cleanly with your CRM and email provider will save more time than one with a longer feature list that lives on its own island. Check integration depth before committing, not just whether an integration exists.

Budget Guidance

Tools in this guide range from affordable entry points to enterprise contracts over $15K/year. For teams under 10 reps, you can build a functional stack for under $200/month per rep. Growth-stage teams (10-50 reps) typically spend $300-$600/month per rep on tooling.

The biggest budget trap is paying for annual contracts on tools your team hasn't fully adopted. Start with monthly billing even if it costs more per month. Switch to annual only after 90 days of consistent usage. The discount isn't worth it if half your team stops logging in after the first week.

Built-In

If you're already using a cold email platform, check whether warmup is included before paying for a separate tool. Instantly has the largest warmup network in the industry with 200K+ real accounts exchanging emails. It's included with every plan and it's the main reason Instantly's deliverability numbers are consistently higher than competitors. Saleshandy includes warmup in its plans too, though the network is smaller. For most cold email senders, the built-in warmup from Instantly or Saleshandy is more than enough.

Standalone

Standalone warmup tools make sense if your main sending platform doesn't include warmup, or if you want a second warmup layer for extra inbox health. Warmbox is the best standalone option at $15/month. Clean interface, solid deliverability analytics, and it works with any email provider. Mailwarm is simpler and more expensive. It works, but at $69/month for what Warmbox does for $15, it's hard to recommend unless you're already using it. Lemwarm is Lemlist's warmup tool, included free with Lemlist subscriptions. If you use Lemlist, you've already got it. Standalone, it's $29/month.

How We Evaluated

Every tool in this guide was scored on four criteria: data quality or core capability (does it actually do what it claims?), pricing transparency (can you find the real cost without a sales call?), ease of setup (how long until your team is productive?), and integration depth (does it connect cleanly with your existing stack?).

Scores range from 1 to 10. A 7+ means we'd recommend it to most teams. Below 7 means it has a specific niche where it works well, but isn't a default recommendation. We don't accept payment for placement, and vendors can't influence their scores.

The Bottom Line

If you're a SDR/BDR building your stack in 2026, start with Instantly (8.7/10, starts at $30/mo). The runner-up is Saleshandy at $25/mo. Both are solid choices that won't lock you into a bad contract.

Don't overthink the decision. Pick one tool from each category you need, run it for 30 days, and evaluate based on actual team adoption, not feature lists. The best tool is the one your team actually uses.
