The Bottom Line

Gong Forecast has a legitimately unique advantage: conversation data as a forecasting signal. Knowing what prospects said on calls gives deal scoring an information layer that purely CRM-based tools can't match. For organizations where phone and video calls are central to the sales process, this signal makes forecasts smarter and risk detection earlier.

The catch is the cost structure. Gong Forecast only exists as an add-on to Gong's conversation intelligence platform. If you're already a Gong customer, adding Forecast is a straightforward decision with incremental cost. If you're starting from scratch, you're buying a $200K+ platform to get a forecasting module. That's a hard sell when Clari provides deeper forecasting for less total investment.

Buy Gong Forecast if you're already a Gong customer and want forecasting in the same platform. Buy Clari if forecasting accuracy is your primary need and you want the most mature, purpose-built solution. Don't buy the full Gong platform just to get the Forecast add-on. The economics only make sense if you need conversation intelligence too.

Gong Forecast Overview

Gong Forecast is the forecasting module built on top of Gong's conversation intelligence platform. The unique angle is compelling: instead of forecasting based solely on CRM data and activity signals, Gong Forecast incorporates what was said on sales calls. When a champion mentions budget approval timelines, competitor evaluations, or internal resistance during a recorded call, Gong Forecast factors that into the deal score. No other forecasting tool has access to this signal layer.

The product launched in 2022 as Gong moved beyond conversation intelligence into the broader revenue platform category. It includes deal boards, pipeline analytics, AI-generated deal scores, and forecast rollups. Managers see which deals have strong verbal commitments versus which have gone quiet. The integration with Gong's call library means you can click from a forecast view directly into the conversation where a risk signal was detected.

Gong Forecast works best for organizations already running Gong for conversation intelligence. The call data is what makes the forecasting unique. Without it, you're paying premium prices for a forecasting tool that competes with Clari and BoostUp at a disadvantage. The add-on pricing model (on top of an existing Gong subscription that already costs $100-$150 per user per month) makes the total investment substantial.

For existing Gong customers, the Forecast module is a natural expansion that unlocks forecasting capabilities with minimal additional implementation. For companies evaluating forecasting tools from scratch, the requirement to buy the full Gong platform first is a significant barrier. You're buying a conversation intelligence platform with a forecasting add-on, not a standalone forecasting solution.

Pros & Cons

  • Conversation data creates a unique forecasting signalGong Forecast analyzes what prospects say on calls to score deals. A prospect mentioning 'We need to get legal involved next week' is a stronger buying signal than a close date in Salesforce. This signal layer gives Gong Forecast information that purely CRM-based tools like Clari and BoostUp can't access. Call sentiment analysis adds another dimension to deal scoring.
  • Single platform for CI and forecastingTeams already using Gong for call recording, coaching, and deal intelligence get forecasting in the same interface. No additional tool to configure, no separate login, no data sync between platforms. Reps see deal boards, forecast data, and call history in one view. Managers coach and forecast from the same dashboard.
  • AI deal scoring improves manager focusGong's AI scores every deal based on engagement patterns, call sentiment, and CRM data. Managers can sort their pipeline by AI score and spend their time on deals that need intervention. The scores update in real-time as new calls happen and new signals emerge. This is faster than reading through rep notes or hoping managers ask the right questions in pipeline reviews.
  • Requires a full Gong subscriptionGong Forecast isn't available standalone. You need the Gong platform first ($100-$150 per user per month), then add Forecast on top. For companies evaluating only forecasting tools, this means buying a $200K+ conversation intelligence platform to get a forecasting module. The math only works if you already use Gong or plan to.
  • Less mature than Clari for pure forecastingClari has been building forecasting since 2012. Gong Forecast launched in 2022. The gap shows in forecast customization, historical trend analysis, and executive reporting depth. Clari's AI models have had more training time on more datasets. If forecasting is your primary need (not conversation intelligence), Clari delivers more.
  • Expensive total cost of ownershipGong platform ($100-$150/user/month) plus the Forecast add-on pushes total costs to $150-$200 per user per month. For a 100-person sales org, that's $180K-$240K annually. Clari at $75K-$150K per year plus a separate CI tool might be cheaper in total, depending on team size and which CI tool you choose.
  • Call coverage gaps weaken the forecasting advantageThe conversation signal only works for deals where calls are recorded. Deals managed primarily through email, in-person meetings, or channel partners won't have call data. If your sales motion is 60% email-driven, Gong Forecast's unique advantage is reduced. The forecasting reverts to CRM-based signals for uncovered deals.

Use Cases

Existing Gong Customer Adding Forecasting

A $80M ARR company already pays $180K per year for Gong across their 120-person sales org. Their VP Sales wants to replace the spreadsheet forecasting process but doesn't want to buy a separate tool. Adding Gong Forecast means the team stays in one platform. The Forecast module is configured in 2 weeks since the data connections already exist. Within the first quarter, the VP identifies that deals where prospects mention competitors on calls close at 35% lower rates, allowing him to adjust forecasts for competitive deals downward.

Manager Using Call Signals for Pipeline Reviews

A frontline manager runs weekly pipeline reviews for her 8-person AE team. In Gong Forecast's deal board, she sorts by AI deal score and focuses on 12 deals scoring below 60 (at-risk). For each deal, she clicks into the most recent call recording to understand why the score dropped. Three deals had calls where the prospect mentioned budget freezes. Two had calls where the champion went silent after previously being engaged. She coaches reps on specific recovery strategies based on what was said, not on guesses about what happened.

CRO Correlating Conversation Patterns with Win Rates

A CRO uses Gong Forecast's analytics to identify which conversation patterns predict closed-won deals. The analysis reveals that deals where the prospect asks about implementation timelines in the second call close at 2.4x the rate of deals where implementation isn't discussed until the fourth call. He instructs AEs to drive implementation conversations earlier in the sales cycle. The team's win rate improves from 22% to 27% over two quarters, adding $4.2M in incremental ARR.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I buy Gong Forecast without the full Gong platform?

No. Gong Forecast requires an active Gong subscription. The forecasting module depends on Gong's conversation data, activity capture, and deal intelligence infrastructure. It's designed as an expansion of the Gong platform, not a standalone product. If you only need forecasting, evaluate Clari or BoostUp instead.

How is Gong Forecast different from Clari?

The biggest difference is conversation data. Gong Forecast analyzes what prospects say on recorded calls and factors that into deal scores and forecasts. Clari relies on CRM data, email engagement, and calendar signals but doesn't analyze call content. Clari has more mature forecasting features and deeper executive dashboards. Gong Forecast has a unique signal layer that no CRM-based tool can replicate.

Does Gong Forecast work with HubSpot?

Gong integrates with HubSpot for contact and deal sync. The Forecast module uses this data alongside conversation signals. The HubSpot integration is solid but the Salesforce integration is more mature. HubSpot customers can run Gong Forecast effectively, though some advanced pipeline analytics features are optimized for Salesforce data structures.

How accurate is Gong Forecast compared to Clari?

Both platforms claim forecast accuracy within 5-15% of actual results after sufficient training. Gong Forecast's conversation signals can provide earlier warning of deal risk, which may improve accuracy for specific deals. Clari's models have been training longer on more customer data. Head-to-head accuracy comparisons depend heavily on your specific sales motion and data quality.

Is Gong Forecast worth the add-on cost?

If you're already paying for Gong, adding Forecast at $30-$50 per user per month is cheaper than buying Clari ($50K+ annually) as a separate tool. The incremental cost is modest relative to the base Gong investment. If you're not a Gong customer yet, the total cost (Gong + Forecast) may exceed what Clari charges for equivalent forecasting capabilities.
