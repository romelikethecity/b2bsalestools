The Bottom Line

Conga CPQ exists for a specific type of buyer: the enterprise with product and pricing complexity that exceeds what other CPQ tools can handle. If your catalog has thousands of SKUs with multi-level dependencies, if your pricing involves algorithmic calculations based on dozens of variables, if your proposals run 40+ pages with dynamic content, Conga can model it. The platform's ceiling is higher than any competitor in the market.

The cost of that power is significant and non-negotiable. Implementation runs six figures. Administration requires dedicated expertise. The user experience prioritizes capability over simplicity. Organizations that buy Conga for 'future complexity' they don't have yet are overpaying for infrastructure they won't use. This is software you buy when you've outgrown everything else, not when you're anticipating growth.

Buy Conga CPQ if you have enterprise-scale configuration complexity, the budget for proper implementation ($100K+), and the internal resources to maintain it. Buy Salesforce CPQ if you need strong CRM integration with significant but not extreme complexity. Buy DealHub if you want CPQ power with mid-market implementation speed. The CPQ market segments clearly by complexity tolerance, and Conga owns the high end.

Conga CPQ Overview

Conga CPQ, formerly Apttus, is built for enterprises where the word 'simple' doesn't apply to pricing. Manufacturing companies with 10,000-SKU catalogs. Telecom providers with nested service bundles that change by region. Global organizations where the same product has different pricing, compliance requirements, and contract terms across 30 countries. When other CPQ tools hit their complexity ceiling, Conga is usually where these organizations land. The platform handles configuration depth that would break lighter tools.

The product configuration engine supports multi-level hierarchies, constraint-based rules, and algorithmic pricing that can model virtually any business logic. A telecom company configuring a bundle with voice, data, hardware, installation, and support components, each with their own pricing tiers, dependencies, and regional variations, can build that entire model in Conga. The rule engine processes thousands of configuration combinations in real-time, validating feasibility and calculating pricing as reps make selections.

Conga's document generation capabilities are a strength that sets it apart from most CPQ competitors. The platform creates complex proposals, contracts, and SOWs from templates with dynamic content blocks that adapt based on the configured deal. A 40-page enterprise proposal with product specifications, pricing tables, terms and conditions, and appendices generates automatically from the quote data. For organizations where proposal quality directly impacts win rates, this document automation layer saves days of manual assembly per deal.

The cost of entry is the elephant in the room. Conga CPQ implementations are measured in months and six figures. The platform requires skilled administrators, often Conga-certified consultants, and the learning curve for end users is steeper than DealHub or HubSpot. Organizations that buy Conga without the internal resources to maintain it end up with an expensive tool that nobody uses correctly. This is software for companies that know they need enterprise CPQ and have the budget and team to support it.

Pros & Cons

  • Handles the most complex configurations on the marketMulti-level product hierarchies with constraint-based rules, algorithmic pricing, and conditional logic support catalogs with 10,000+ SKUs. Manufacturing BOMs, telecom service bundles, and multi-component enterprise solutions configure accurately within the rule engine. Companies that evaluated three other CPQ tools and found them insufficient for their complexity typically find that Conga can model what others can't.
  • Enterprise document generation is best in classConga Composer generates proposals, contracts, and SOWs with dynamic content that adapts to deal specifics. Templates pull in pricing tables, product specifications, legal clauses, and custom sections based on the configured quote. A proposal that takes a solutions engineer 8 hours to assemble manually generates in minutes. For companies where proposal quality is a competitive differentiator, this automation is transformative.
  • Contract lifecycle management integrationConga's broader platform includes CLM (contract lifecycle management), which means the quote-to-contract-to-renewal lifecycle lives in a single system. Negotiated terms in the contract feed back into renewal pricing. Amendment workflows handle mid-contract changes. This end-to-end coverage eliminates the handoff gaps between quoting and legal that create revenue leakage.
  • Global pricing and compliance supportMulti-currency, multi-language, and region-specific pricing rules handle the complexity of global sales operations. Tax calculations, regulatory compliance flags, and country-specific terms and conditions adapt automatically based on the deal's geography. Organizations selling in 20+ countries get compliant quoting without manual checks for each region.
  • Implementation costs are enterprise-gradeConga CPQ implementations typically run $100K-$500K+ with timelines of 4-9 months. The platform's power comes with configuration complexity that requires certified consultants. Underbudgeting implementation is the most common mistake new customers make. Plan for 2x what you initially estimate and you'll be closer to reality.
  • Steep learning curve for administrators and usersThe admin interface has depth that takes months to master. End users face a more complex quoting experience than DealHub or HubSpot. Training requirements are significant, and ongoing education is needed as the platform evolves. Organizations without a dedicated admin (or at least a power user with 20% of their time allocated) will see adoption suffer.
  • Post-Apttus transition created some growing painsThe acquisition and rebranding from Apttus to Conga consolidated products but also created integration overlap and product roadmap uncertainty for some customers. Long-time Apttus customers have reported feature changes and support transitions during the merger period. The platform is stabilizing, but the transition history is worth understanding during evaluation.
  • Overkill for most mid-market companiesIf your product catalog has under 200 SKUs and your pricing fits a standard tier structure, Conga's complexity works against you. You'll pay for enterprise infrastructure and spend months implementing a system that DealHub could deliver in weeks. The platform is purpose-built for extreme complexity, and bringing it to a simple problem creates unnecessary overhead.

Use Cases

Global Telecom Provider Configuring Multi-Component Service Bundles

A telecom company sells bundled services (voice, data, cloud, security, hardware) across 25 countries. Each country has different product availability, pricing, regulatory requirements, and tax rules. A single enterprise deal might include 40+ line items with interdependent pricing and volume commitments. Conga CPQ's multi-level configuration engine handles the full bundle complexity. Region-specific pricing rules apply the correct rates and compliance requirements automatically. Proposals generate in the customer's local language with country-specific terms. The sales team closes deals 35% faster because reps no longer wait for pricing specialists to validate complex configurations manually.

Industrial Manufacturer Quoting Engineered-to-Order Products

A manufacturer sells configurable industrial equipment where each order involves hundreds of specification choices. Materials, dimensions, motor specifications, safety certifications, and mounting options create thousands of valid combinations and millions of invalid ones. Conga's constraint-based rules validate configurations in real-time, preventing orders that can't be manufactured. Pricing algorithms calculate costs based on material weights, labor hours, and component selections. Detailed specification documents generate alongside the quote, giving the engineering team a validated build sheet. Order errors drop from 12% to under 2%, saving $1.2M annually in rework and returns.

Enterprise Software Company Managing Complex Renewal Portfolios

A software company with 2,000 enterprise customers manages a renewal book where each customer has a unique combination of products, custom pricing, negotiated terms, and multi-year ramp schedules. Conga CPQ integrates with CLM to pull existing contract terms into renewal quotes automatically. Amendment workflows handle mid-term product additions with co-termination. The renewal team processes 500+ renewals per quarter with accurate pricing that reflects each customer's negotiated rates and volume commitments. Revenue leakage from manual renewal pricing errors drops from 4% to under 0.5%, recovering $800K annually.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Conga CPQ the same as Apttus?

Conga acquired Apttus in 2020 and merged the products under the Conga brand. The CPQ technology is the evolution of Apttus CPQ, which was one of the original enterprise CPQ platforms. Existing Apttus customers were migrated to Conga's platform. The core CPQ engine retains Apttus's configuration power with ongoing enhancements from Conga's development team.

Does Conga CPQ work with Salesforce?

Yes. Conga CPQ integrates with Salesforce, and historically Apttus was built as a Salesforce-native application. Conga also supports Microsoft Dynamics and can operate as a standalone platform. The Salesforce integration is the most mature, with deep object-level connectivity. Organizations on Salesforce evaluating Conga vs. Salesforce CPQ should compare based on configuration complexity needs.

How does Conga CPQ compare to Salesforce CPQ?

Conga handles more extreme configuration complexity with deeper multi-level hierarchies and algorithmic pricing. Salesforce CPQ has tighter native CRM integration and a larger admin talent pool. Choose Conga when your product catalog exceeds 500 SKUs with nested dependencies that strain Salesforce CPQ's rule engine. Choose Salesforce CPQ when native Salesforce integration and ecosystem access matter more than maximum configuration depth.

What industries use Conga CPQ most?

Telecommunications, manufacturing, high-tech, and enterprise software are the primary verticals. These industries share a pattern: large product catalogs, complex configuration rules, multi-component pricing, and global operations. Companies in these verticals typically have pricing complexity that simpler CPQ tools can't model. Professional services and healthcare are growing verticals as well.

Can a small team implement Conga CPQ without consultants?

This is not recommended. Conga CPQ's configuration depth requires expertise that takes months to develop. Most successful implementations involve Conga's professional services team or certified partners. Attempting a self-service implementation with a complex product catalog typically leads to suboptimal configuration, user frustration, and a second engagement with consultants to fix the problems. Budget for professional help from the start.
