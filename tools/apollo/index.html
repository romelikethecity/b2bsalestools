The Bottom Line

Apollo.io is the best value play in B2B sales tools. No other platform combines a 270M+ contact database, email sequencing, a dialer, and LinkedIn integration at $49/month. For startups, SMBs, and growth-stage teams that need to do more with less, it's the obvious first choice.

The trade-offs are real. Data accuracy lags behind ZoomInfo, especially for phone numbers and enterprise contacts. The sequencing engine works for most use cases but won't satisfy teams running complex multi-channel campaigns at scale. And the sheer breadth of features can make the UI feel cluttered.

Buy Apollo if you're a team under 50 reps that values workflow integration and cost efficiency over having the absolute best data. Outgrow it when you need intent data, best-in-class phone numbers, or enterprise-grade sequencing. Most teams won't outgrow it for at least 2-3 years.

Apollo.io Overview

Apollo.io has become the default prospecting tool for startups and growth-stage sales teams. The platform combines a 270M+ contact database with built-in email sequencing, a dialer, and LinkedIn integration, all starting at $49/month. No other tool in the market bundles this much functionality at this price point.

The company raised $100M in Series D funding in 2023 and has been growing rapidly, claiming over 3 million users. Their aggressive freemium model (10,000 email credits/month for free) has driven adoption across the entire sales stack, from solo founders doing their own outreach to 200-person SDR floors running multi-channel sequences.

Apollo's data quality sits in the middle of the pack. Email accuracy runs 91-93% for business emails, which is solid but below ZoomInfo's 95%+. Phone numbers are the weakest point, with connect rates around 50-60% for direct dials. European data coverage has improved but still has gaps in Southern and Eastern Europe.

The platform's real competitive advantage is the workflow layer built on top of the data. You can find a prospect, enrich their profile, add them to a sequence, and track engagement without leaving Apollo. That eliminates the need for separate tools for data, sequencing, and basic analytics, which makes the total cost of ownership dramatically lower than a ZoomInfo + Outreach stack.

Pros & Cons

  • Best value in B2B sales toolsApollo bundles contact data (270M+), email sequencing, a dialer, and LinkedIn integration starting at $49/month. A comparable stack using ZoomInfo + Outreach + a standalone dialer would cost $25,000-$40,000/year. For budget-conscious teams, the math is straightforward.
  • Generous free tier for early-stage teamsThe free plan includes 10,000 email credits/month, basic sequencing, and LinkedIn extension access. Solo founders and early-stage SDRs can run meaningful outbound campaigns without spending anything. The free tier is fully usable, not a crippled demo.
  • All-in-one prospecting workflowFind contacts, enrich profiles, add to sequences, make calls, and track opens/clicks without switching tools. The integrated workflow eliminates data sync issues between separate platforms and reduces the number of tabs your reps juggle daily.
  • Fast onboarding and low learning curveMost teams are running sequences within 2-3 days of signup. The UI is intuitive for anyone who's used a modern SaaS product. Compare that to ZoomInfo (2-4 weeks) or Outreach (1-2 weeks) and the productivity ramp is significantly faster.
  • Email accuracy below ZoomInfo for enterprise targetsApollo's 91-93% email accuracy is good but not great. When prospecting into Fortune 500 companies with complex email routing and security, bounce rates climb. If you're selling to enterprise, verify emails with a secondary tool like NeverBounce before sending high-volume campaigns.
  • Phone numbers are unreliableDirect dial accuracy runs 50-60%, meaning nearly half your calls go nowhere. Mobile numbers are worse. SDR teams relying heavily on phone outreach should supplement Apollo data with a dedicated phone data provider or use a parallel dialer that can handle high miss rates.
  • UI can feel overwhelmingApollo packs a lot of features into one interface. New users sometimes struggle to find specific settings or understand the difference between sequences, plays, and workflows. The breadth is a strength for power users but can slow down onboarding for less technical reps.
  • Sequencing features lag behind dedicated platformsApollo's sequencing is good enough for most use cases, but it lacks the advanced branching, A/B testing depth, and deliverability management of Outreach or Salesloft. Teams running sophisticated multi-channel campaigns at scale may outgrow Apollo's sequencing before they outgrow its data.

Use Cases

Startup Founder Running Solo Outbound

A SaaS founder uses Apollo's free tier to build prospect lists of VP-level buyers at companies with 50-500 employees. They create a 5-step email sequence targeting 200 prospects per week. With 10,000 free credits per month, they can sustain this volume indefinitely. The built-in tracking shows which emails get opened and clicked, letting them focus follow-ups on engaged prospects.

10-Person SDR Team at Series B Company

A growing SDR team uses Apollo Professional ($79/seat/month) to run multi-channel outbound. Each rep builds daily prospect lists filtered by industry, title, and company size, then adds contacts to sequences combining email and LinkedIn touchpoints. The team books 15-20 meetings per rep per month. Apollo's built-in dialer handles call tasks directly from the sequence workflow.

RevOps Lead Enriching CRM Data

A RevOps manager uses Apollo's API and CRM integration to automatically enrich new leads entering HubSpot. When a contact fills out a form, Apollo fills in missing fields like company size, industry, revenue range, and direct phone number. This eliminates manual research and ensures routing rules have the data they need to assign leads correctly.

Pricing Analysis

Apollo's pricing is transparent and published on their website. The free tier includes 10,000 email credits/month with basic sequencing. Basic ($49/user/month) adds unlimited email credits, advanced filters, and job change alerts. Professional ($79/user/month) adds the dialer, A/B testing, and advanced reports. Organization ($119/user/month, minimum 3 seats) adds intent data, call transcription, and API access.

Annual billing saves roughly 20% across all plans. For teams of 10+, Apollo offers custom pricing that can bring per-seat costs down further. There's no multi-year lock-in like ZoomInfo, and you can upgrade, downgrade, or cancel monthly on the Basic and Professional plans.

The credit system is where costs can creep up. Mobile phone number exports cost more credits than email exports. Teams doing heavy phone prospecting should model their credit usage before committing to a plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Apollo.io free?

Apollo offers a fully usable free tier with 10,000 email credits/month, basic sequencing, and LinkedIn extension access. You can run meaningful outbound campaigns without paying anything. Paid plans start at $49/user/month for unlimited email credits, advanced filters, and additional features.

How accurate is Apollo data?

Apollo's email accuracy sits around 91-93% for verified business emails. Phone number accuracy is lower, with direct dials connecting about 50-60% of the time. Email accuracy is competitive with most providers but below ZoomInfo's 95%+. For high-stakes outreach, consider verifying emails through a secondary service.

Is Apollo better than ZoomInfo?

For teams under 20 reps with budgets under $30K/year, Apollo is the better choice. It provides 80% of ZoomInfo's core data functionality at 5% of the cost, plus built-in sequencing. ZoomInfo wins on data accuracy (especially phone numbers), intent data depth, and enterprise features. The right choice depends on budget, team size, and how much you value phone data.

Does Apollo integrate with Salesforce?

Yes. Apollo offers bi-directional Salesforce integration that syncs contacts, accounts, and activity data. The integration supports automated enrichment, lead routing, and sequence enrollment from within Salesforce. HubSpot, Pipedrive, and other major CRMs are also supported.

Can Apollo replace Outreach or Salesloft?

For most teams, yes. Apollo's sequencing handles multi-step email campaigns, call tasks, and LinkedIn steps. It lacks some of Outreach's advanced features like complex branching logic and enterprise-grade deliverability management. Teams running fewer than 500 active sequences typically find Apollo's built-in sequencing sufficient.
