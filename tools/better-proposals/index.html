The Bottom Line

Better Proposals is the best proposal tool for freelancers and small agencies. The per-account pricing (not per user) makes it dramatically cheaper than competitors at small team sizes. The web-based proposal format looks professional. E-signatures and payment collection are included. Live chat keeps deals moving. For independent operators and small teams sending $3K-$50K proposals, Better Proposals delivers a professional buying experience at a price that makes sense.

The limitations become apparent as teams grow. User management is basic. CRM integrations are lightweight. Approval workflows are minimal. Content governance for larger teams doesn't exist. These gaps matter at 10+ users but are irrelevant for the tool's target market. Better Proposals isn't trying to compete with PandaDoc at scale, and that focus on the small end of the market is a strength.

Buy Better Proposals if you're a freelancer, solo consultant, or small agency (1-10 people) that wants professional proposals with integrated signing and payments at the lowest possible cost. Upgrade to PandaDoc when you grow beyond 10 people and need deeper CRM integration, approval workflows, and content governance. Buy Qwilr if you want interactive pricing in a similar web-based format. Better Proposals wins on value for small teams, and for most of its target market, that's the only factor that matters.

Better Proposals Overview

Better Proposals is the proposal tool built for freelancers, agencies, and small businesses that don't need enterprise complexity. Where PandaDoc and Proposify target mid-market and enterprise teams with per-user pricing and team governance features, Better Proposals focuses on the smaller end of the market with a simpler product at a price that works for independent operators. The result is a tool that does fewer things but does them well and doesn't charge per user on the entry plan.

The proposal builder uses a web-based format similar to Qwilr, where proposals display as scrolling web pages rather than downloadable documents. Templates are well-designed and cover common proposal types for agencies, freelancers, and service businesses. The editor is straightforward: add content blocks, customize text and images, insert a pricing table, and include a digital signature field. A proposal takes 10-15 minutes to create from a template, which is fast enough that even small deals get a professional proposal.

Better Proposals includes e-signatures, payment integration (Stripe, PayPal, GoCardless), and proposal analytics in all paid plans. The analytics show when buyers open, read, and forward proposals. Live chat within the proposal lets buyers ask questions without switching to email or scheduling a call. These features combine to create a smooth proposal-to-close experience for smaller deals where speed matters more than process formality.

Starting at $19/month (not per user), Better Proposals is significantly cheaper than per-user-priced competitors for solo operators and small teams. A freelancer pays $19/month total versus $19/user/month with PandaDoc or Proposify. For a 3-person agency, Better Proposals at $29/month (Premium plan) costs less than a single user on most competitor plans. The savings are dramatic at small team sizes. The trade-off is clear: enterprise features like approval workflows, advanced CRM integration, and content governance are minimal or missing entirely.

Pros & Cons

  • Priced for freelancers and small teams, per accountThe Starter plan at $19/month includes 10 proposals per month, e-signatures, and payment integration. The Premium plan at $29/month adds unlimited proposals and custom branding. These prices cover the account, not individual users. A 3-person agency pays $29/month total versus $57/month on PandaDoc Essentials or $57/month on Proposify Team. The math is clear.
  • Web-based proposals that look professionalProposals display as modern, scrolling web pages that look polished on any device. The templates are clean and well-designed for service business proposals. The web format is similar to Qwilr's approach but at a fraction of the cost. Buyers receive a link and the experience feels current and professional.
  • Built-in signatures and payment collectionE-signatures and payment integration (Stripe, PayPal, GoCardless) are included in all paid plans. Buyers can sign the proposal and pay a deposit in one flow. For freelancers who previously sent proposals by email and chased payments separately, the integrated workflow eliminates days of follow-up and awkward payment reminder emails.
  • Live chat inside the proposalBuyers can ask questions directly within the proposal using built-in chat. Questions appear as notifications to the seller. This feature is simple but effective for small deals where a quick clarification prevents the buyer from abandoning the proposal. The real-time communication keeps momentum alive at the moment the buyer is most engaged.
  • Limited scalability for growing teamsBetter Proposals works well for 1-5 person teams but lacks the user management, role-based permissions, content governance, and approval workflows that growing teams need. Agencies scaling beyond 10 people will outgrow the platform and need to migrate to PandaDoc or Proposify. Plan for that transition when it comes.
  • CRM integrations are basicBetter Proposals integrates with common CRMs (HubSpot, Pipedrive, Zoho) but the integrations are lightweight compared to PandaDoc's or Proposify's Salesforce integration. Teams with complex CRM workflows that require deep bi-directional sync may find the integration capabilities insufficient for their needs.
  • Template library is smaller than competitorsBetter Proposals has fewer templates than PandaDoc's extensive library. The templates that exist are well-designed for agency and freelancer use cases, but teams in specialized industries may need to build templates from scratch. The editor makes this straightforward, but it's additional setup time that competitors avoid.
  • Proposal limits on lower plansThe Starter plan limits users to 10 proposals per month. Agencies sending more than 10 proposals monthly need the Premium ($29/month) or Enterprise ($49/month) plan. The proposal cap is unusual in a category where most tools offer unlimited documents on paid plans.

Use Cases

Freelance Web Developer Sending Proposals to Clients

A freelance developer sends 8-10 proposals per month for website projects ranging from $3,000 to $15,000. Each proposal includes project scope, timeline, pricing with optional add-ons (SEO setup, maintenance plan, hosting), and a digital signature field. When the client signs, a Stripe payment link for the 50% deposit activates automatically. The freelancer tracks which proposals are viewed and follows up when a proposal has been opened but not signed. Total monthly cost: $19. Previous process: Google Docs proposal emailed as PDF, separate invoice for deposit, manual follow-up. Time saved per proposal: 30+ minutes.

A 4-person marketing agency creates templates for their three service packages: SEO retainers, content marketing programs, and social media management. Each template includes the relevant case studies, team bios, service details, and pricing with optional add-on services. The agency sends 15-20 proposals per month. Live chat in proposals lets prospects ask questions about scope without scheduling another call. Payment integration collects a $500 setup fee at signing. Monthly cost on the Premium plan: $29 total for the entire agency. Proposal close rate improved from 24% to 38% after switching from email PDFs.

Consulting Firm Testing Proposals Before Scaling

A 2-person consulting firm uses Better Proposals to professionalize their proposal process before investing in a more expensive platform. They create 5 templates for different engagement types and send proposals with integrated signing and payment. The founders plan to migrate to PandaDoc when the firm grows to 8-10 people and needs approval workflows, deeper CRM integration, and content governance. For now, Better Proposals at $29/month delivers 80% of what they need at 20% of PandaDoc's cost. The low commitment lets them focus budget on growth instead of tools.

Pricing Analysis

Better Proposals has three plans. The Starter plan at $19/month includes 10 proposals per month, e-signatures, payment integration, and analytics. The Premium plan at $29/month adds unlimited proposals, custom branding, and a content library. The Enterprise plan at $49/month includes team features, approval workflows, and Salesforce integration.

All prices are per account, not per user. This pricing model is dramatically cheaper for small teams. A 3-person team on Premium pays $29/month total. The same team on PandaDoc Essentials pays $57/month (3 x $19).

Annual billing is available with modest discounts. Free trials let teams test the platform before committing. The 14-day trial is enough time to send a few real proposals and evaluate the workflow with actual buyer reactions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Better Proposals good for agencies?

Yes, for small to mid-size agencies (1-10 people). The template system, web-based proposals, and payment integration work well for agency sales workflows. Larger agencies with complex approval chains, deep CRM requirements, or enterprise client procurement processes will likely need PandaDoc or Proposify as they scale.

How does Better Proposals compare to PandaDoc?

Better Proposals is simpler and cheaper for small teams (per account vs per user pricing). PandaDoc is more powerful with deeper CRM integrations, more templates, and better scalability. A freelancer saves money with Better Proposals. A 20-person sales team needs PandaDoc's team features and CRM depth. The crossover point is around 5-8 people.

Can I collect payments through Better Proposals?

Yes. Stripe, PayPal, and GoCardless integrations are included in all paid plans. Buyers can pay a deposit or full amount alongside their signature. The payment flow is integrated into the proposal experience, so signing and paying happen in one step without redirecting to a separate payment page.

Is there a free plan?

Better Proposals doesn't offer a permanent free plan, but they provide a 14-day free trial of any paid plan. For free e-signatures without a time limit, PandaDoc's free plan is the better option. Better Proposals' value is in the proposal creation experience with integrated payments, not just the signing step.

Can I use Better Proposals with a team?

Yes. The Enterprise plan at $49/month supports team collaboration with shared templates, team management features, and basic approval workflows. However, the team features are limited compared to PandaDoc or Proposify. Teams above 5-10 people should evaluate whether Better Proposals' team capabilities meet their collaboration and governance needs before committing.
