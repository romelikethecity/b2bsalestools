Clari Overview

Clari is the revenue intelligence platform that CROs at high-growth companies build their forecasting processes around. The product ingests data from your CRM, email, calendar, and conversation tools, then applies machine learning to give you a forecast you can trust more than whatever your reps manually submitted in Salesforce. Founded in 2012, Clari has raised over $500M and counts companies like Okta, Qualtrics, and Adobe among its customers. The company has established itself as the category leader for a reason: the forecasting accuracy is unmatched.

The core value proposition centers on pipeline inspection and deal health scoring. Clari analyzes engagement patterns, deal velocity, and historical win rates to surface which deals are on track and which are at risk. Reps see deal-level health scores. Managers see rollup forecasts with AI-generated confidence intervals. CROs see board-ready revenue projections that don't rely on gut feel. The exec dashboard alone justifies the cost for many organizations.

Activity capture runs in the background, syncing emails, meetings, and contacts to Salesforce without reps lifting a finger. This solves the biggest CRM data quality problem in B2B sales: reps don't log activities. With Clari capturing everything automatically, the AI models have clean input data, which makes the forecasts more accurate over time. The system gets smarter with every closed deal.

Pricing starts at $50K per year for most deployments, which puts Clari firmly in the enterprise category. Smaller companies with under $10M in ARR will struggle to justify the investment. But for organizations running $20M+ pipelines where a 5% improvement in forecast accuracy translates to millions in better planning decisions, Clari pays for itself. Clari works. The question is whether your organization is big enough to need it.

Use Cases

CRO Preparing a Board-Ready Forecast

A CRO at a $150M ARR SaaS company uses Clari to prepare quarterly forecasts for the board. Instead of spending 3 days collecting manager submissions and reconciling conflicting pipeline estimates, she opens Clari's executive dashboard 30 minutes before the board meeting. The AI-generated forecast shows $41.2M projected for Q3, with a confidence range of $39.8M to $43.1M. She drills into the top 20 deals by value, checks their health scores, and identifies 4 deals flagged as at-risk that account for $3.2M of the forecast. She briefs her VPs on those deals before the meeting. The board gets a forecast they trust because it's data-driven.

VP Sales Running Weekly Pipeline Reviews

A VP Sales managing 6 frontline managers runs Monday pipeline reviews using Clari's pipeline inspection view. Each manager's pipeline is broken down by deal health: green (on track), yellow (needs attention), and red (at risk). The VP focuses the 30-minute meeting on the 15-20 red and yellow deals across the organization. Clari shows which deals lost engagement in the past week (fewer emails, no meetings scheduled) and which have new risk signals (delayed close dates, missing stakeholders). Before Clari, these reviews took 90 minutes and relied on manager opinions. Now they're data-driven and half the length.

RevOps Building a Forecasting Process from Scratch

A RevOps leader at a Series D company replacing spreadsheet forecasting implements Clari across a 120-person sales org. Phase one (weeks 1-4) connects Clari to Salesforce, Gmail, and Gong. Phase two (weeks 5-8) configures forecast categories, pipeline stages, and deal scoring weights. Phase three (months 3-6) trains the team on Clari's interface and compares AI forecasts against manual submissions. By month 6, the AI forecast is consistently within 7% of actuals while manual submissions average 18% variance. The company standardizes on Clari's number for all planning.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is Clari's forecasting?

Most customers report AI-generated forecasts within 5-10% of actual quarterly results after 2-3 quarters of training data. The accuracy improves over time as the models learn your specific sales patterns, deal velocities, and seasonal trends. First-quarter accuracy is typically lower (15-20% variance) while the models calibrate. Organizations with clean CRM data and consistent sales processes see the best results.

Does Clari work without Salesforce?

Clari's primary CRM integration is Salesforce, and most customers run on Salesforce. HubSpot integration is available but less mature. If you're on a different CRM (Dynamics, Pipedrive, etc.), Clari's functionality will be limited. The platform is designed around Salesforce data structures, and the deepest features assume Salesforce as the source of truth.

How long does Clari implementation take?

Standard implementation takes 4-8 weeks for CRM connection, data sync, and basic configuration. Full deployment with custom forecast categories, trained AI models, and organizational adoption takes 3-6 months. The AI models need at least 2 quarters of historical data to produce accurate forecasts. Plan for a dedicated RevOps resource spending 10-15 hours per week during the first month.

Can Clari replace my manual forecasting process?

Yes, and that's the primary use case. Clari's AI forecast replaces the bottom-up, manager-submitted forecast that most sales orgs run. The shift typically happens in stages: quarter one runs both processes in parallel, quarter two uses Clari as the primary forecast with manual as backup, and by quarter three most organizations rely on Clari's number for planning and board reporting.

Is Clari worth $50K+ per year?

For companies with $20M+ in ARR and 50+ person sales teams, yes. A 5% improvement in forecast accuracy at that scale directly impacts hiring plans, marketing spend, and cash flow management. For companies under $10M ARR, the ROI is harder to justify. Consider BoostUp or InsightSquared as more affordable alternatives until your pipeline volume warrants Clari's price tag.
