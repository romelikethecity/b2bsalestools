Leadfeeder (Dealfront) Overview

Leadfeeder, now part of the Dealfront platform, is the veteran of website visitor identification. The company has been identifying anonymous B2B visitors since 2012, which gives it one of the largest proprietary IP-to-company databases in the market. When someone visits your site, Leadfeeder resolves their IP address against this database and tells you which company they work for, which pages they visited, and how long they spent on each page. It operates at the company level, not the person level, but the company identification is among the most accurate available.

The Dealfront merger in 2023 expanded Leadfeeder's capabilities significantly, especially for European markets. Dealfront brings GDPR-compliant data enrichment, European company databases, and compliance infrastructure that US-centric tools lack. For B2B companies selling into Europe, this combination of visitor identification and compliant data handling is a meaningful advantage. No other visitor ID tool comes close to Leadfeeder's European coverage.

Leadfeeder's interface organizes visitors into a feed sorted by quality. Custom filters let you surface visitors by company size, industry, visit frequency, or pages viewed. The integration layer connects to HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, and dozens of other CRMs to push identified companies directly into your sales workflow. The filtering and routing capabilities are more mature than newer entrants like RB2B, reflecting over a decade of product development.

The free plan (Leadfeeder Lite) identifies up to 100 companies per week with 7 days of data retention. The paid plan starts at $99/month and unlocks unlimited identification, 12 months of data history, CRM integrations, and custom feeds. Pricing scales with the number of identified companies, which means high-traffic sites may pay significantly more. For companies with moderate traffic that want reliable company-level identification with strong CRM integration, Leadfeeder remains a solid default choice.

Pros & Cons

  • Strongest European visitor identificationThe Dealfront merger gave Leadfeeder access to European company databases and GDPR-compliant data processing infrastructure that US-built tools don't have. For companies selling into the UK, Germany, France, or Nordics, Leadfeeder identifies visitors that tools like RB2B and Clearbit miss entirely. If any portion of your target market is European, this alone can justify the choice.
  • Mature filtering and lead scoringCustom feeds let you create filtered views based on company size, industry, pages visited, visit duration, and return frequency. A VP of Sales can see only enterprise visitors who viewed pricing. An SDR can see mid-market visitors who returned three times this week. The filtering depth reflects years of product iteration and helps teams focus on the highest-quality visitors instead of scrolling through raw traffic.
  • Deep CRM integrationsLeadfeeder's integrations with HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, and other CRMs are among the tightest in the category. Identified companies and visit data sync automatically to account records. Visit activity appears in CRM timelines. Sales reps see visitor intelligence without leaving their CRM. These integrations took years to build and test, and they work more reliably than what newer tools offer.
  • Free tier is useful for small sitesLeadfeeder Lite identifies up to 100 companies per week for free, with basic visit data and 7-day history. For early-stage companies or sites with under 1,000 monthly visitors, the free plan provides enough intelligence to identify warm accounts without spending a dollar. It's a genuine free tool, not a trial with an expiration date.
  • Company-level only, no person identificationLeadfeeder tells you that someone from Acme Corp visited your site. It doesn't tell you which person. For large companies with hundreds of employees, the sales team still has to research who the likely visitor was. RB2B and Warmly offer person-level identification that eliminates this gap. The company-level limitation is the biggest functional difference between Leadfeeder and newer competitors.
  • Pricing scales with traffic volumeThe paid plan starts at $99/month but increases based on the number of unique companies identified. High-traffic sites (20,000+ monthly visitors) can see monthly costs climb to $300-$500 or more. This variable pricing makes budgeting harder and can surprise teams whose traffic spikes from a viral post or ad campaign. Fixed-price tools like RB2B are more predictable.
  • Dealfront branding creates confusionThe Leadfeeder-to-Dealfront transition has created brand confusion. Some features live in the Leadfeeder product, others in the broader Dealfront platform. Documentation and support resources reference both names inconsistently. New users may struggle to understand what they're buying and which features are included in which product.
  • Limited automation capabilitiesLeadfeeder identifies visitors and sends alerts, but the built-in automation is basic compared to Warmly. There's no AI chatbot, no automated outreach sequences, no meeting booking from visitor signals. Teams wanting to automate the path from identification to engagement need additional tools or a platform like Warmly that bundles everything together.

Use Cases

European B2B SaaS Company Identifying Cross-Border Visitors

A SaaS company headquartered in Berlin sells across the EU and UK. They install Leadfeeder on their marketing site and identify 600 companies per month across 12 European countries. The GDPR-compliant identification is critical for their legal team's requirements. Custom feeds filter visitors by country, showing the German sales team only DACH visitors and the UK team only British companies. CRM sync pushes identified companies directly into Salesforce with visit data attached to account records. The sales team prioritizes outreach to companies that visited the pricing page and returned within 7 days, generating 15-20 qualified meetings per month from visitor intelligence alone.

B2B Marketing Team Measuring Content Effectiveness

A content marketing team publishes 8 blog posts per month and wants to understand which content attracts ICP-fit companies. They use Leadfeeder's custom feeds to filter identified visitors by company size (500+ employees) and view which content each company consumed. Over 90 days, the data reveals that technical comparison posts attract 3x more enterprise visitors than thought leadership articles. The team shifts their content calendar toward comparison content, and enterprise visitor volume increases 40% in the next quarter. The $99/month investment directly informs a content strategy that drives higher-quality traffic.

Sales Team Building Account-Based Prospecting Lists

A 6-person sales team at a mid-market company uses Leadfeeder as a source of warm accounts for outbound prospecting. Each week, the team reviews the Leadfeeder feed for companies that visited key pages (pricing, case studies, product pages) and had more than 3 page views. This weekly review surfaces 30-40 companies showing buying signals. Reps research contacts at these companies on LinkedIn, craft personalized outreach referencing specific content the company viewed, and achieve 25% response rates on their outreach, compared to 8% on cold prospecting. Leadfeeder turns anonymous website traffic into a warm prospecting list.

Pricing Analysis

Leadfeeder Lite is free forever and identifies up to 100 companies per week with 7 days of data retention. You get basic company information and page visit data. No CRM integration, no custom feeds, no email notifications on the free plan.

The paid plan starts at $99/month (billed annually) or $165/month (billed monthly) and includes unlimited company identification, 12 months of data history, CRM integrations, custom feeds, and email/Slack notifications. Pricing scales based on the number of unique companies identified per month. Sites identifying 200-300 companies stay near the base price. High-traffic sites identifying 1,000+ companies may see pricing increase to $200-$500/month.

Dealfront offers additional products (sales intelligence, compliance tools) that can be bundled with Leadfeeder at combined pricing. Enterprise agreements with custom terms are available for larger organizations. Annual billing saves approximately 40% compared to monthly billing, making it one of the more aggressive annual discount structures in the category.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Leadfeeder and Dealfront?

Leadfeeder is the visitor identification product that's now part of the Dealfront platform. Dealfront is the parent company that also offers sales intelligence, lead generation, and compliance tools focused on European markets. When you buy Leadfeeder, you're buying the visitor identification functionality. The broader Dealfront platform adds additional capabilities that can be purchased separately or bundled.

Can Leadfeeder identify individual people?

Leadfeeder primarily identifies companies, not individuals. When someone visits your site, you'll see the company name, pages visited, and visit duration, but not the specific person's name. Some plans include contact suggestions (likely contacts at the identified company based on job title matching), but this is an estimate rather than a confirmed identification. For person-level identification, consider RB2B.

How does Leadfeeder handle GDPR compliance?

The Dealfront merger specifically strengthened GDPR compliance. Leadfeeder processes visitor data through European infrastructure, offers data processing agreements, and provides tools for managing consent and data subject requests. For European companies or companies selling into Europe, this compliance infrastructure is more developed than what US-built alternatives offer. The company positions GDPR compliance as a core feature rather than an afterthought.

Is Leadfeeder worth it vs the free plan?

The paid plan adds three things that matter: unlimited identification (vs 100/week), 12 months of data history (vs 7 days), and CRM integration. If your site identifies more than 100 companies per week and your sales team uses a CRM, the paid plan is worth the upgrade. The 12-month history is particularly valuable for tracking which accounts return over time, which is a strong buying signal. Start with the free plan and upgrade when you hit the 100-company weekly limit.

How accurate is Leadfeeder's company identification?

Leadfeeder's IP-to-company resolution is among the most accurate in the category, with the company claiming 80%+ accuracy on identified visitors. The resolution works best for visitors on corporate networks and VPNs that route through identifiable IP ranges. Remote workers on residential ISPs are harder to identify and account for most missed identifications. The accuracy rate has been validated by multiple independent reviews over the years.
