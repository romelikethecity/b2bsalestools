The Bottom Line

Clari Copilot occupies a specific niche in conversation intelligence: it's the best option for teams that want AI coaching during live calls. The real-time cue cards providing battle cards, objection responses, and discovery prompts while reps are mid-conversation is a capability no other major CI tool matches. For teams with newer reps, complex product lines, or extensive competitive landscapes, this in-the-moment guidance drives immediate call quality improvements.

The trade-off is clear. Copilot's post-call analytics, deal intelligence, and coaching workflows trail Gong. Organizations whose primary CI use case is retrospective analysis, pipeline risk scoring, and systematic manager coaching will get more from Gong. The decision comes down to where you want the AI to intervene: during the call or after it.

Buy Clari Copilot if you're already using Clari for revenue management and want CI integrated into that ecosystem, or if real-time coaching for your reps is the primary use case. Buy Gong if post-call analytics and deal intelligence are your top priorities. Consider lighter tools like Sybill or Fireflies if you want call transcription and CRM updates without the enterprise CI price tag.

Clari Copilot Overview

Clari Copilot, formerly known as Wingman, is conversation intelligence with a twist: real-time coaching during live calls. While Gong and Chorus analyze calls after they happen, Clari Copilot puts AI-powered cue cards on screen while reps are still talking to prospects. Competitor mentioned? A battle card appears. Pricing objection raised? Handling suggestions pop up. Discovery question missed? A prompt nudges the rep. This live coaching capability is what separates Clari Copilot from post-call analytics tools.

Clari acquired Wingman in 2023 and rebranded it as Clari Copilot, integrating it into Clari's broader revenue platform. The integration matters because Clari's forecasting and pipeline management tools now receive conversation signals directly from Copilot. Deal risk scoring becomes more accurate when it incorporates what was said on calls, not just CRM stage data. The Clari ecosystem creates a feedback loop between conversations, deals, and forecasts that standalone CI tools can't replicate.

The post-call analytics are capable but don't match Gong's depth. Call summaries, action items, topic tracking, and searchable transcripts cover the standard CI feature set. Coaching scorecards and team dashboards provide manager visibility. The analytics work well for day-to-day coaching and call review, but organizations wanting the deepest possible conversation analysis and custom reporting will find Gong more flexible.

Pricing is custom and typically falls between $80-$120/user/month depending on whether you're buying Copilot standalone or as part of the broader Clari revenue platform. The value calculation depends on how much you weight real-time coaching versus post-call analytics. If your reps are in live sales conversations and would benefit from in-the-moment guidance, Clari Copilot offers something Gong and Chorus don't. If post-call analysis and deal intelligence are your priorities, Gong has a stronger offering.

Pros & Cons

  • Real-time coaching during live callsAI-powered cue cards surface battle cards, objection responses, and discovery prompts while reps are on active calls. This in-the-moment guidance helps junior reps handle situations they haven't encountered before. For teams with newer sellers or reps selling complex products with extensive competitor landscapes, the live coaching directly impacts call quality in real time.
  • Integration with Clari's revenue platformConversation signals from Copilot feed into Clari's forecasting and pipeline management. Deal health scores incorporate what was said on calls, improving forecast accuracy. For organizations already using Clari for revenue management, adding Copilot creates a unified view of pipeline health that combines quantitative CRM data with qualitative conversation signals.
  • Strong competitor and topic trackingClari Copilot excels at detecting competitor mentions, pricing discussions, and buying signals in real time and post-call. The tracking is tuned for sales conversations and catches nuanced references that simpler keyword matching misses. Competitive intelligence reports aggregate these signals across all team calls.
  • Automated call summaries and CRM updatesPost-call, Copilot generates structured summaries with action items, next steps, and key discussion topics. These summaries can push directly to Salesforce, reducing the time reps spend on call documentation. The summary quality is solid, capturing the important elements of the conversation without requiring manual editing.
  • Real-time cue cards can distract experienced repsVeteran salespeople with strong product knowledge and objection handling skills often find the on-screen cue cards more distracting than helpful. Pop-up prompts during a natural conversation flow can break the rep's focus. Most teams end up configuring cue cards differently for junior versus senior reps, which adds administration overhead.
  • Post-call analytics trail GongWhile the real-time coaching is differentiated, the post-call analysis features are a step behind Gong's offering. Deal intelligence is less sophisticated, custom reporting is more limited, and coaching workflows lack Gong's polish. Teams prioritizing deep post-call analytics over real-time coaching will find Gong more capable.
  • Smaller market share means fewer integrationsGong and Chorus have larger customer bases and broader integration ecosystems. Some niche tools in your stack may integrate with Gong but not Clari Copilot. Check your specific tech stack compatibility before committing. The core integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoom, Teams) are all supported.
  • Best value within the Clari ecosystemClari Copilot's strongest differentiator beyond real-time coaching is its integration with Clari's revenue platform. Organizations not using Clari for forecasting and pipeline management lose a significant part of the value proposition. As a standalone CI tool, the case for Copilot over Gong is harder to make without the real-time coaching use case.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does real-time coaching work during calls?

Clari Copilot displays an overlay panel during live calls (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet). When the AI detects specific triggers like competitor mentions, pricing questions, or objections, it surfaces relevant cue cards with suggested responses, talking points, or data. Reps see the cards on their screen while the prospect sees nothing. The experience is similar to having a coach whispering in your ear.

Is Clari Copilot the same as Wingman?

Yes. Clari acquired Wingman in 2023 and rebranded it as Clari Copilot. The core real-time coaching functionality carried over, with additional integration into Clari's revenue platform for forecasting and pipeline management. Existing Wingman customers were migrated to the Clari Copilot product.

Do I need Clari's other products to use Copilot?

No. Copilot works as a standalone CI tool with real-time coaching. The integration with Clari's forecasting and pipeline management is valuable but not required. Standalone buyers get call recording, transcription, real-time cue cards, and post-call analytics without other Clari products.

Can I customize the cue cards?

Yes. Admins configure cue cards with custom battle cards, objection responses, product information, and process prompts. Trigger conditions determine when each card appears based on keywords, topics, or conversation patterns. Most teams spend 2-3 hours setting up their initial cue card library and refine it over the first month based on rep feedback.

How does Clari Copilot compare to Gong?

Copilot's unique advantage is real-time coaching during live calls. Gong's strengths are deeper post-call analytics, more sophisticated deal intelligence, and more polished coaching workflows. Choose Copilot if in-the-moment guidance for reps is your priority. Choose Gong if post-call analysis, pattern recognition across thousands of calls, and deal risk scoring matter more.
