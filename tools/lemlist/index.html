The Bottom Line

Lemlist is the best cold email tool for teams that invest in creative, personalized outreach. The image and video personalization features are unmatched, the multi-channel sequences cover email, LinkedIn, and phone, and the built-in lead database eliminates the need for a separate data tool. If your outbound strategy relies on standing out visually and coordinating touches across channels, Lemlist delivers.

The economics are the sticking point. At $39-$159 per user per month, Lemlist costs 2-5x what Instantly charges for cold email. That premium buys personalization and multi-channel. Teams that end up sending plain text emails through Lemlist are paying a personalization tax they never use. Be honest about whether your team will invest the effort to create custom image templates and run multi-channel campaigns.

Buy Lemlist if personalization is central to your outbound strategy, if multi-channel coordination matters, and if your team will create and iterate on custom visual content. Buy Instantly if you need volume-first cold email at the lowest cost. Buy Reply.io if you want multi-channel with AI writing instead of visual personalization.

Lemlist Overview

Lemlist pioneered personalized cold email with image and video personalization features that no competitor had in 2018. The concept is straightforward: embed a prospect's name, company logo, or headshot into a custom image inside the email, making each message feel individually crafted. When Lemlist launched this feature, reply rates for personalized image emails ran 2-3x higher than plain text. Competitors have since added similar capabilities, but Lemlist's implementation remains the most mature.

The platform has expanded well beyond its personalization roots into a full multi-channel outreach tool. Campaigns can include email steps, LinkedIn actions (profile visits, connection requests, messages), and phone call tasks. The built-in lead database (450M+ contacts) added prospecting to the workflow. Lemlist now covers the full outbound cycle from finding prospects to engaging them across channels.

Lemlist's multi-channel approach puts it in an interesting competitive position. For pure cold email volume, Instantly and Smartlead win on deliverability infrastructure and pricing. For full sales engagement, Outreach and Salesloft are more powerful. Lemlist sits between these categories, offering better personalization than the cold email tools and simpler multi-channel than the enterprise platforms. That middle ground works well for teams running creative, relationship-focused outbound.

Pricing starts at $39/month for email-only campaigns and scales to $159/month for the full multi-channel suite with the lead database. The per-user pricing model (vs Instantly's flat rate) means costs climb faster as teams grow. A 5-person team on Lemlist's Multichannel Expert plan pays $495/month, compared to $30/month total for Instantly's Growth plan. The price premium only makes sense if you're using the personalization and multi-channel features that justify it.

Pros & Cons

  • Best image and video personalization in cold emailLemlist's dynamic image personalization embeds prospect-specific variables (name, company, logo, website screenshot) into custom image templates. Video personalization adds a similar concept to embedded videos. These features make emails visually distinctive in crowded inboxes. While competitors have added basic image personalization, Lemlist's template library and customization options remain the deepest in the category.
  • Native multi-channel sequencesCampaigns combine email, LinkedIn (visits, connections, messages), and phone tasks in a single sequence builder. The LinkedIn integration automates profile visits and connection requests directly from the campaign. This eliminates the need for a separate LinkedIn automation tool for basic social selling actions.
  • Built-in lead databaseLemlist's database includes 450M+ contacts with email and LinkedIn data. The quality is comparable to Apollo for basic prospecting. Having prospecting built into the outreach tool eliminates the export/import step between separate data and engagement platforms. Email verification is included to reduce bounces.
  • Active community and contentLemlist has built one of the most active cold outreach communities. Their YouTube channel, blog, and community forums share campaign templates, benchmarks, and tactics. New users benefit from a library of proven templates and sequence structures. The community creates a learning ecosystem around the tool.
  • Deliverability infrastructure lags Instantly and SmartleadLemlist added warmup and inbox rotation, but the deliverability tooling isn't as sophisticated as Instantly's 200,000+ inbox warmup network or Smartlead's sending optimization. High-volume senders (500+ emails/day) may see better inbox placement rates on dedicated cold email platforms.
  • Per-user pricing gets expensive at scaleAt $39-$159 per user per month, a 10-person team pays $390-$1,590/month. Instantly charges $30/month flat regardless of team size. The math only works if every user is actively leveraging personalization and multi-channel features. Teams that end up sending mostly plain text emails from Lemlist are overpaying.
  • Personalization features require effort to use wellDynamic image templates need to be designed. Variable mapping needs to be configured. Logo and headshot scraping doesn't always find the right assets. Teams that invest 2-3 hours creating custom image templates see strong results. Teams that use default templates see marginal improvement over plain text. The feature rewards effort, and not every team has the bandwidth.
  • LinkedIn automation has limitationsLemlist's LinkedIn features cover visits, connection requests, and messages but lack the depth of dedicated LinkedIn tools like Expandi or Dripify. Daily action limits are conservative to protect accounts. Teams running aggressive LinkedIn outbound alongside email may still need a dedicated LinkedIn tool.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Lemlist's image personalization work?

Yes, when implemented well. Campaigns with custom image templates showing prospect-specific variables (name, company logo, website screenshot) consistently outperform plain text emails on reply rates. The key is investing time in designing templates that look professional and feel relevant. Default or generic templates produce marginal uplift. Well-designed personalization drives 2-3x improvement in reply rates based on Lemlist's published benchmarks and user reports.

How does Lemlist compare to Instantly for cold email?

Instantly is better for pure cold email volume: larger warmup network, unlimited accounts, and $30/month flat pricing. Lemlist is better for personalized, multi-channel outreach: image/video personalization, LinkedIn integration, and phone steps. Choose Instantly if email volume is the priority. Choose Lemlist if creative personalization and multi-channel coordination matter more than raw volume.

Can Lemlist do LinkedIn automation?

Lemlist includes LinkedIn profile visits, connection requests, and messaging as native campaign steps. The integration works through a Chrome extension. Daily action limits are conservative to protect your LinkedIn account from restrictions. For basic LinkedIn touches alongside email, Lemlist covers it. For aggressive LinkedIn outbound (100+ actions/day), consider a dedicated tool like Expandi.

Is Lemlist worth the price vs Instantly?

Only if you use the personalization and multi-channel features. A single user sending personalized image emails with LinkedIn steps gets clear value from Lemlist's $99/month Multichannel plan. A team sending plain text emails should use Instantly at $30/month. The pricing difference ($69+/month per user) is the cost of personalization and multi-channel capabilities.

Does Lemlist have a lead database?

Yes. Lemlist's built-in database includes 450M+ contacts with email addresses and LinkedIn profiles. The data quality is comparable to Apollo for mid-market prospecting. Lead finder credits are included in paid plans (varying by tier). The database covers the basics but doesn't match ZoomInfo or Cognism on data depth, intent signals, or technographic information.
