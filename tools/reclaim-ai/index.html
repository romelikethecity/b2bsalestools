The Bottom Line

If your problem is back-to-back meetings with no time for prep, follow-up, or focused work, Reclaim solves it intelligently. The AI scheduling adapts throughout the week without requiring constant manual adjustment.

For sales professionals specifically, Reclaim pairs well with an external scheduling tool like Calendly or Chili Piper. Use Calendly to let prospects book meetings. Skip it if your calendar is manageable or if your primary need is external prospect scheduling. The free plan is a legitimate starting point with real utility, which makes trying Reclaim a low-risk decision.

Reclaim.ai Overview

Reclaim.ai solves a different problem than Calendly or Chili Piper. Instead of external scheduling (getting prospects to book meetings), Reclaim focuses on internal calendar management. It uses AI to automatically schedule and protect time for habits, tasks, breaks, and focus work around your meetings. Think of it as an AI assistant that manages your calendar so you don't spend Sunday night playing calendar Tetris for the week ahead.

The product connects to Google Calendar (Outlook support added in 2024) and creates smart, flexible time blocks. You tell Reclaim you need 2 hours of focus time daily, a 30-minute lunch, and time for your weekly 1:1 prep. Reclaim finds optimal slots, marks them as busy to others, and automatically reschedules if conflicts arise. The blocks look like regular calendar events to colleagues, so there's no stigma about blocking time.

For sales professionals, Reclaim is useful in a specific way: protecting preparation and follow-up time around prospect meetings. AEs who spend all day in back-to-back calls with no time for research, proposals, or CRM updates can use Reclaim to automatically buffer meeting prep and follow-up time. It won't help you book meetings faster, but it will help you show up prepared and follow through after.

Pricing is reasonable. The free plan covers basic smart scheduling with 3 habits. The Starter plan at $8/user/month unlocks unlimited habits, task integration, buffer time between meetings, and priority scheduling. Business ($12/user/month) adds team-level features and analytics. For individuals and small teams, the free plan provides real value. The paid plans are worth it for anyone whose calendar regularly spirals into unmanageable territory.

Pros & Cons

  • AI scheduling that adapts to your weekReclaim's core AI figures out where to place your recurring time blocks based on your meeting load, priorities, and preferences. When a new meeting gets added, Reclaim automatically reschedules your flexible blocks around it. This happens continuously throughout the week without manual intervention. It's the closest thing to having a human assistant manage your calendar.
  • Protects focus time and prevents calendar overloadSales reps and managers often have calendars packed with meetings and zero time for the work between meetings. Reclaim creates defender events that block off time for deep work, prep, and admin tasks. These blocks appear as busy to colleagues but can flex when high-priority meetings need to be scheduled. The result is a sustainable calendar instead of constant burnout.
  • Free plan is surprisingly capableReclaim's free tier includes smart scheduling for up to 3 habits, scheduling links, and calendar sync. That's enough for individuals to experience the core value. Most free scheduling tools (Calendly included) don't offer anything like Reclaim's AI calendar management at no cost.
  • Task integration with project management toolsReclaim syncs with Todoist, Linear, Jira, Asana, ClickUp, and Google Tasks to automatically schedule time for your task list. When you add a task with a deadline, Reclaim finds time on your calendar to work on it. For sales reps juggling pipeline tasks, proposal deadlines, and CRM updates, this keeps work visible on the calendar instead of buried in a task app.
  • Doesn't solve external scheduling problemsReclaim solves a different problem than Calendly or Chili Piper for prospect meeting booking. It doesn't offer form routing, lead distribution, or CRM-integrated scheduling. If your primary need is getting prospects to book meetings, Reclaim isn't the answer. It's a complement to external scheduling tools, not a replacement.
  • Google Calendar dependency until recentlyReclaim was Google Calendar-only for years, which excluded Microsoft 365 organizations. Outlook support launched in 2024 but is still maturing. Teams on Microsoft 365 should verify that the Outlook integration covers their needs, particularly around shared calendars and room bookings.
  • Learning curve for optimal configurationGetting Reclaim to work well requires thoughtful setup. You need to define your habits, set priorities, configure flexibility windows, and calibrate how aggressively Reclaim defends your time. The defaults are reasonable, but the product delivers its best results after 1-2 weeks of tuning based on how your calendar behaves in practice.
  • Team features are still developingReclaim's team features (smart 1:1 scheduling, team analytics, no-meeting days) are functional but less mature than its individual features. Teams wanting organization-wide calendar optimization should evaluate whether the team tier's capabilities match their needs. The product's strength remains individual calendar management.

Use Cases

Enterprise AE Protecting Prep and Follow-Up Time

An enterprise AE running 6-8 prospect meetings per day uses Reclaim to automatically schedule 15-minute prep blocks before each meeting and 15-minute follow-up blocks after. Reclaim reads the calendar, identifies upcoming meetings, and inserts prep/follow-up buffers in the surrounding time slots. When a new meeting gets added, the buffers automatically adjust. The AE stopped going into meetings cold and started sending follow-up emails within 30 minutes of every call. Win rate improved from 22% to 28% over one quarter. The AE credits having prep and follow-up time built into the workflow instead of relying on willpower.

Sales Manager Maintaining Coaching and Strategy Time

A frontline sales manager with 10 direct reports uses Reclaim to protect 2 hours daily for coaching prep, pipeline review, and strategic thinking. Without Reclaim, those hours disappeared into ad-hoc meetings and Slack requests. Reclaim marks the time as busy so team members schedule around it, but the blocks flex automatically when a VP requests an urgent pipeline call. The manager also uses Reclaim's smart 1:1 feature to find optimal times for weekly coaching sessions with each rep, saving 30 minutes of scheduling logistics per week.

SDR Team Preventing Calendar Burnout

A 15-person SDR team uses Reclaim on the Business plan to enforce no-meeting blocks for prospecting. Each SDR has 3 hours of protected calling time daily that Reclaim schedules around their existing meetings, team stand-ups, and training sessions. The calling blocks appear as busy time on shared calendars so internal meetings don't encroach. The SDR manager uses Reclaim's team analytics to see how much protected time each rep gets per week and correlates it with activity numbers. Reps with consistent prospecting blocks book 35% more meetings than those who let their calendars fill up.

Pricing Analysis

Reclaim.ai offers four tiers. The Free plan includes up to 3 habits (recurring smart blocks), scheduling links, calendar sync, and basic smart scheduling. Starter is $8/user/month and adds unlimited habits, task integration (Todoist, Linear, Jira, Asana), buffer time between meetings, priority-based scheduling, and custom working hours.

Business is $12/user/month with team analytics, smart 1:1 scheduling, no-meeting days, and team-wide scheduling policies. Enterprise pricing is custom and includes SSO, advanced admin controls, and dedicated support.

All paid plans offer a 14-day free trial. Annual billing saves approximately 20% compared to monthly. For individual users, the free plan covers the core value. Teams that want buffer time, task integration, and team coordination will get meaningful value from the Starter or Business tiers at a price point well below most sales tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Reclaim.ai a Calendly alternative?

They solve different problems. Calendly handles external scheduling (letting others book time with you). Reclaim manages your internal calendar (automatically scheduling focus time, tasks, and habits around your meetings). Most people benefit from using both: Calendly for prospect-facing scheduling, Reclaim for personal calendar management.

Does Reclaim.ai work with Outlook?

Yes. Reclaim added Outlook/Microsoft 365 support in 2024. The integration covers calendar sync, smart scheduling, and habit blocking. Google Calendar integration is more mature since it was available first, but Outlook support covers the core functionality. Check the latest feature parity documentation if you're on Microsoft 365.

How does Reclaim.ai protect focus time?

You create habits for recurring needs like 'Deep Work' (2 hours daily) or 'Lunch' (30 minutes). Reclaim automatically finds time slots on your calendar, marks them busy so colleagues can't book over them, and reschedules them if conflicts arise. The blocks flex based on priority, so a high-priority habit is harder for meetings to displace than a low-priority one.

Can Reclaim.ai schedule tasks from Jira or Asana?

Yes. Reclaim integrates with Todoist, Linear, Jira, Asana, ClickUp, and Google Tasks. Tasks sync into Reclaim and get scheduled as calendar blocks based on due date, estimated duration, and priority. When deadlines shift or new tasks appear, Reclaim reschedules automatically. It turns your task backlog into calendar commitments.

Is Reclaim.ai worth paying for?

The free plan is worth trying for anyone who struggles with calendar management. The Starter plan ($8/month) is worth paying for if you need task integration, meeting buffers, or more than 3 habits. The Business plan ($12/month) adds value for teams wanting shared scheduling policies. Compared to the hourly cost of manual calendar management, even paid plans pay for themselves quickly.
