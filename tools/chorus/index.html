The Bottom Line

Chorus is the conversation intelligence tool you should buy if you're already in the ZoomInfo ecosystem. The bundled pricing makes the economics dramatically more favorable than adding a separate Gong contract, and the core CI capabilities (recording, transcription, analytics, coaching) cover what most sales teams need. The ZoomInfo data enrichment on call participants adds context that standalone CI tools can't match.

The honest assessment: Chorus trails Gong in analytics sophistication, coaching workflow maturity, and overall user experience. Teams that treat conversation intelligence as a strategic weapon and want the absolute best tool should buy Gong. Teams that want solid CI capability integrated with their existing ZoomInfo investment should buy Chorus and save the budget difference for other tools.

The worst decision is buying both ZoomInfo and Gong when Chorus would have been sufficient. Evaluate whether you need Gong's premium analytics or whether Chorus's 80-85% capability bundled at a fraction of the cost serves your actual workflow. Most teams under 50 reps won't exploit the features that separate Gong from Chorus.

Chorus (ZoomInfo) Overview

Chorus was one of the original conversation intelligence platforms before ZoomInfo acquired it in 2021 for $575M. The acquisition made strategic sense: ZoomInfo had the best B2B contact database, and Chorus added the ability to analyze what happens when sales teams talk to those contacts. The combined offering gives ZoomInfo customers CI capability without buying a separate Gong license.

The core CI functionality is solid. Chorus records and transcribes sales calls, identifies key moments (pricing discussions, competitor mentions, next steps), and provides searchable conversation libraries. Call analytics track talk ratios, question frequency, and topic distribution. Team dashboards show coaching opportunities across reps. The fundamentals work and have improved since the ZoomInfo acquisition added engineering resources.

Where Chorus falls compared to Gong is in the details. Gong's deal intelligence is more sophisticated, with better pipeline risk scoring and multi-threading analysis. Gong's coaching workflows (scorecards, clip libraries, comment threads) are more polished. Gong's analytics dashboards offer more customization. These differences matter most for large organizations that need deep CI capability. For teams using CI primarily for call review and basic coaching, the gap is smaller than Gong's pricing premium suggests.

The pricing dynamic is what makes Chorus interesting. If your organization already uses ZoomInfo for prospecting data, Chorus comes bundled or heavily discounted as an add-on. You might pay $0-$30/user/month incremental cost versus $100+/user/month for Gong. That math changes the evaluation entirely. Buying Gong alongside ZoomInfo means paying for two enterprise contracts. Chorus bundled with ZoomInfo consolidates spend and vendor relationships, even if the CI capabilities are a step behind.

Pros & Cons

  • Bundled with ZoomInfo at significant savingsFor existing ZoomInfo customers, adding Chorus is dramatically cheaper than buying Gong separately. Some ZoomInfo enterprise contracts include Chorus at no additional cost. Even when priced separately, the bundled discount typically makes Chorus 50-70% cheaper than Gong. For budget-conscious teams already in the ZoomInfo ecosystem, this is the strongest argument.
  • Solid core CI capabilitiesCall recording, transcription, keyword tracking, competitor mentions, and conversation analytics cover the standard CI use cases. The transcription quality is good. Topic detection works well for common sales conversation patterns. Search across the call library is fast. For teams stepping up from no CI tool at all, Chorus delivers immediate value.
  • ZoomInfo data enrichment on call participantsChorus can pull ZoomInfo profile data for call participants, giving reps context about who they're speaking with. Job title, company size, industry, and other firmographic data appear alongside the call recording. This integration between data and conversations is unique to the ZoomInfo ecosystem.
  • Improving product velocity under ZoomInfoZoomInfo has invested in Chorus development since the acquisition. The UI has improved, new analytics features ship regularly, and the integration with ZoomInfo's broader platform deepens over time. The product trajectory is positive, even if Gong maintains a feature lead today.
  • Analytics depth trails GongGong's conversation analytics, deal intelligence, and custom reporting capabilities remain ahead. Chorus covers the basics well but lacks the granularity that large sales organizations rely on for data-driven coaching and pipeline management. Teams wanting the deepest possible CI analytics will find Chorus limiting.
  • Coaching workflows are less polishedGong's coaching scorecards, structured review workflows, and clip-sharing features are more mature. Chorus supports coaching through call review and commenting, but the structured coaching tooling feels a generation behind Gong's. Managers who want to build systematic coaching programs may find the tooling frustrating.
  • Tied to ZoomInfo's ecosystem and contractThe value proposition for Chorus is strongest within the ZoomInfo ecosystem. If you ever leave ZoomInfo for another data provider, you'd need to replace Chorus too. This creates vendor lock-in that limits your flexibility. Standalone CI buyers should evaluate Chorus purely on its own merits, where it's harder to justify over Gong.
  • UI and UX lag behind competitorsChorus's interface feels functional but dated compared to Gong's polish or newer entrants like Sybill. Navigation requires more clicks. Dashboard customization is limited. The experience is adequate, but sales reps who use the tool daily notice the difference in usability compared to more modern interfaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Chorus as good as Gong?

For core CI (recording, transcription, basic analytics), Chorus is 80-85% of Gong's capability. Where Gong pulls ahead is deal intelligence, coaching workflows, custom analytics, and UI polish. If CI is a strategic priority and budget isn't constrained, Gong is the better product. If you're already paying for ZoomInfo and want good CI without a second enterprise contract, Chorus delivers strong value.

Can I buy Chorus without ZoomInfo?

Technically yes, but it's uncommon and removes the primary value proposition. Chorus standalone pricing is comparable to other CI tools but without the ZoomInfo data enrichment benefits. If you're evaluating CI tools independently of ZoomInfo, compare Chorus directly against Gong, Clari Copilot, and lighter options like Sybill.

Does Chorus integrate with Salesforce?

Yes. Chorus syncs call data, insights, and conversation analytics to Salesforce opportunity and contact records. The integration surfaces CI data within the CRM so reps and managers don't need to switch between tools. The Salesforce integration has improved significantly since the ZoomInfo acquisition.

How does Chorus handle call recording consent?

Chorus displays a recording notification at the start of each call and can be configured to announce recording verbally. Settings adjust by region to comply with one-party or two-party consent requirements. GDPR-specific configurations handle European call recording rules. These features match what Gong and other CI tools offer.

Will Chorus eventually match Gong's features?

ZoomInfo is investing in Chorus development, and the feature gap has narrowed since the acquisition. New analytics, improved coaching tools, and better integrations ship regularly. Whether Chorus fully catches Gong depends on ZoomInfo's continued investment. The trajectory is positive, but Gong keeps innovating too, so the gap may persist.
