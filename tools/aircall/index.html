The Bottom Line

Aircall is the best choice for companies that need a cloud phone system and a sales dialer in a single platform. The 100+ integrations, reliable call quality, and combined inbound/outbound capabilities eliminate the need for separate phone and dialing tools. For companies where sales is one of several teams that need phone infrastructure, Aircall simplifies the stack.

The weakness is outbound intensity. Aircall's power dialer handles sequential calling well but doesn't compete with Orum or Nooks on parallel dialing, AI detection, or outbound-specific analytics. Dedicated SDR teams that live on the phone 6 hours a day will outgrow Aircall's outbound features quickly. The power dialer is a feature within a phone system, not a purpose-built outbound engine.

Buy Aircall if you need one phone platform for sales, support, and general business calling with broad integration support. Buy Orum or Nooks if outbound SDR productivity is the primary goal. Buy Kixie if you want deeper CRM integration at lower cost for outbound-only use cases. Buy PhoneBurner if flat-rate pricing and simplicity matter most. Aircall wins when the buying decision is about the whole phone system, not just the dialer.

Aircall Overview

Aircall is a cloud phone system first and a sales dialer second. That distinction matters. While Orum, Nooks, and PhoneBurner are built for outbound sales velocity, Aircall is built to be your company's phone system that also happens to have solid outbound capabilities. The result is a tool that handles inbound routing, IVR, call queues, and international calling alongside power dialing and sales workflows.

The integration ecosystem is Aircall's standout advantage: 100+ integrations with CRMs, helpdesks, e-commerce platforms, and productivity tools. Salesforce, HubSpot, Intercom, Shopify, Slack, Zendesk, Gong, and dozens more connect natively. For companies that need their phone system to work with a complex tech stack, Aircall's integration breadth is unmatched among dialers. The IT team will appreciate that every integration uses OAuth and has a documented API.

Call quality and reliability are consistently strong across user reviews. Aircall runs on AWS infrastructure with data centers in multiple regions, delivering clear audio with minimal latency for domestic and international calls. For companies with sales teams across multiple countries, Aircall's international number provisioning and global call quality give it an edge that US-focused dialers can't match.

The trade-off is outbound intensity. Aircall's power dialer (available on the Professional plan at $50/user/month) handles sequential dialing well, but there's no parallel dialing. No AI answer detection. No virtual sales floor. For a dedicated outbound SDR team making 100+ dials per day, Aircall is a phone system with a power dialer bolted on, not a purpose-built outbound machine. The sweet spot is teams that need both inbound and outbound calling in one platform without paying for two separate tools.

  • Clean, modern interfaceAircall's desktop and mobile apps are well-designed and intuitive. The interface is polished without being complex. Reps and managers learn the system quickly. The mobile app is particularly strong, which matters for field sales teams and managers who need to monitor calls on the go.
  • Not purpose-built for high-volume outboundAircall's power dialer handles sequential calling but lacks parallel dialing, AI answer detection, and the outbound-specific features that Orum and Nooks offer. SDR teams focused exclusively on high-volume cold calling will find Aircall's outbound features adequate but not competitive with dedicated outbound dialers.
  • No parallel dialing limits SDR productivitySequential dialing means reps call one number at a time. For teams making 80+ calls per day, the absence of parallel dialing translates to 2-3x fewer live conversations compared to Orum or Nooks. This is the single biggest limitation for outbound-focused teams evaluating Aircall.

There are no published volume discounts for smaller deals, and the per-user model means costs grow proportionally with headcount. Compare this to flat-rate alternatives for budget-sensitive organizations.
  • Advanced outbound analytics are limitedAircall provides call metrics, team performance dashboards, and basic reporting. Conversation intelligence, AI call scoring, and the deep outbound analytics that Orum offers aren't available natively. Teams wanting data-driven outbound optimization will need to pair Aircall with a tool like Gong for conversation analytics.

Pricing Analysis

Aircall publishes three pricing tiers. Essentials at $30/user/month includes inbound and outbound calling, IVR, call recording, and basic integrations (minimum 3 users). Professional at $50/user/month adds the power dialer, advanced analytics, call monitoring, and the full integration library (minimum 3 users). Custom pricing is available for larger deployments needing SLA guarantees, dedicated support, and custom integrations.

All plans include unlimited inbound calls within your country. Outbound calls are charged per-minute based on destination, which is standard for cloud phone systems. International calling rates vary by country. Toll-free numbers and additional number provisioning may carry extra monthly fees.

Compare Aircall against the split alternative: a phone system (Dialpad, RingCentral, or Grasshopper at $15-$30/user/month) plus a dedicated dialer (PhoneBurner at $124/month or Kixie at $35-$65/month). For teams needing both inbound and outbound, Aircall's combined pricing is often cheaper than two separate tools. For teams needing only outbound dialing, dedicated dialers provide more capability at comparable or lower cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Aircall a good sales dialer?

Aircall is a good power dialer within a great cloud phone system. For teams that need both inbound phone capabilities and outbound dialing, it's an excellent single-platform solution. For teams focused exclusively on high-volume outbound calling (80+ dials/day), purpose-built dialers like Orum or PhoneBurner offer more outbound-specific features and higher throughput.

Does Aircall have parallel dialing?

No. Aircall's power dialer (Professional plan, $50/user/month) dials numbers sequentially from a list. There's no parallel or multi-line dialing capability. For sequential power dialing, Aircall performs well. Teams needing parallel dialing should look at Orum, Nooks, or Kixie's Outbound Power Dialer plan.

How does Aircall compare to RingCentral?

Both are cloud phone systems, but Aircall is more sales-oriented while RingCentral focuses on unified communications (video, messaging, phone). Aircall has a stronger sales dialer, better CRM integrations, and a simpler interface. RingCentral has stronger video conferencing, team messaging, and enterprise telephony features. Choose Aircall for sales and customer-facing teams. Choose RingCentral for company-wide communications.

How many integrations does Aircall have?

Aircall offers 100+ native integrations including Salesforce, HubSpot, Intercom, Zendesk, Shopify, Slack, Gong, Monday.com, Pipedrive, and many more. The integration marketplace covers CRMs, helpdesks, e-commerce platforms, productivity tools, and conversation intelligence. The breadth of integrations is the widest among sales dialers.

Can Aircall replace my office phone system?

Yes. Aircall is a full cloud phone system that handles IVR, call routing, voicemail, business hours, ring groups, and multi-location management. Companies use Aircall as their primary phone infrastructure for all departments. The combination of business phone features and sales dialing capabilities makes it suitable as a single phone platform for companies up to several hundred employees.
