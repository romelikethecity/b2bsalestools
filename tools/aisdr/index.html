The Bottom Line

AiSDR is the right tool for teams that want to test AI outbound without betting the budget. At $750/month, it removes the financial barrier that makes 11x and Artisan inaccessible to smaller organizations. Buy AiSDR if you're testing AI outbound for the first time, have a limited budget, or need to supplement a small SDR team without a major investment. Use the 60-90 day period to measure meeting volume and quality. If AI outbound works for your ICP, you can decide whether AiSDR's capabilities are sufficient or whether the results justify upgrading to a more capable platform.

AiSDR Overview

AiSDR offers AI-powered email writing, lead research, and automated follow-up at the most accessible price point in the AI SDR category. Starting at $750 per month, it costs a fraction of 11x ($5K+) or Artisan ($2K+) while covering the core workflow: find prospects, write personalized emails, send them, and follow up based on replies. For teams that want to test AI outbound without a $5,000/month commitment, AiSDR is the obvious starting point.

The platform handles lead sourcing through integrations with data providers and your own uploaded lists. AiSDR researches each prospect using LinkedIn data and company information, then generates personalized emails. The AI writes in a direct style that avoids the over-personalized, trying-too-hard tone that plagues some competitors. Follow-up sequences trigger based on prospect behavior, with the AI adjusting messaging for non-responders versus partial engagers. The workflow is straightforward and doesn't require a dedicated ops person to manage.

Where AiSDR trades off against pricier competitors is depth. The prospect research is thinner than 11x's SEC-filing-reading Alice. The personalization is solid but won't reference a prospect's recent podcast appearance or earnings call comments. The platform handles the 80% use case (well-researched, personalized cold email at scale) without the bells and whistles of premium alternatives. For many teams, that 80% is exactly enough.

The company has grown quickly on the strength of its pricing and simplicity. Setup takes hours, not weeks. The interface is clean with traditional dashboards and settings rather than the AI employee metaphors used by competitors. You can see exactly what's being sent, who's being targeted, and how sequences are performing. This transparency is a meaningful advantage for sales leaders who want to understand and control their outbound, even when AI is doing the work.

Pros & Cons

  • Most affordable entry point for AI SDRAt $750/month, AiSDR costs 85% less than 11x and 60% less than Artisan. For teams testing the AI SDR concept, this price point dramatically reduces risk. If it doesn't work, you've lost less than two months of a ZoomInfo subscription instead of committing $60K/year.
  • Fast setup and transparent interfaceAiSDR is operational within hours. The traditional dashboard interface shows exactly who's being contacted, what's being sent, and how campaigns are performing. No AI persona to chat with, no black box decisions. Sales leaders can audit every email before it goes out during the initial calibration period.
  • Clean, effective email outputThe AI writes concise cold emails that avoid the common AI trap of over-personalization. Emails read like a competent SDR wrote them quickly, not like an AI trying to impress with how much research it did. The tone is professional and direct, which tests well for B2B outreach.
  • Solid follow-up logicAutomated follow-ups adjust based on whether the prospect opened, clicked, or replied. Non-responders get different messaging than prospects who opened but didn't reply. The branching is simpler than Outreach's but covers the use cases that matter most for cold outbound sequences.
  • Shallower prospect research than premium alternativesAiSDR's research pulls from LinkedIn profiles and basic company data. It won't read 10-K filings, earnings call transcripts, or niche industry publications the way 11x does. For enterprise selling where deep research drives response rates, the lighter touch may not be enough.
  • Less sophisticated personalizationEmails are personalized with role, company, and industry context. They won't reference a prospect's recent conference talk or their company's Q3 revenue miss. The personalization is good for volume outbound but lacks the specificity that moves needles on high-value enterprise targets.
  • Smaller company with limited track recordAiSDR is a newer entrant without the funding levels or market presence of 11x or Regie.ai. The product is solid today, but longevity and continued development depend on the company's financial trajectory. Evaluate current capabilities and get comfortable with the team before committing long-term.
  • Data sourcing depends on integrationsUnlike Artisan with its built-in database, AiSDR relies on integrations with data providers or your own uploaded contact lists. You'll still need a data source, which adds cost and complexity. Factor in $200-$1,500/month for a data provider on top of AiSDR's $750/month.

Use Cases

Budget-Conscious Team Piloting AI Outbound

A 15-person sales team at a bootstrapped SaaS company wants to test AI SDR capabilities without major budget commitment. They deploy AiSDR at $750/month targeting their secondary ICP segment (mid-market HR directors) that the human SDR team hasn't had bandwidth to work. The setup takes one afternoon. Within a week, AiSDR is sending personalized emails to 50 new prospects daily. After 60 days, they've booked 23 meetings from the segment. The $1,500 total investment produced enough pipeline to justify either continuing with AiSDR or upgrading to a more capable platform.

Supplementing a Small SDR Team During Busy Periods

A company with 3 SDRs uses AiSDR to handle overflow during their busiest quarters. When inbound volume spikes, the SDRs focus on warm leads while AiSDR maintains outbound activity on cold lists. The AI keeps the outbound pipeline active without the SDRs splitting attention between inbound follow-up and cold prospecting. When inbound slows down, the SDRs shift back to outbound and AiSDR pauses or scales to a different segment. The flexibility is valuable for seasonal businesses.

Founder-Led Sales With Limited Time

A B2B founder spending 80% of time on product uses AiSDR to maintain consistent outbound activity. The founder uploads a target list of 500 companies, defines the ICP in the dashboard, and lets AiSDR run weekly email campaigns with automated follow-ups. The founder checks results every Monday morning, responds to interested prospects, and lets the AI handle everything else. Total weekly time investment: 2-3 hours. Total monthly meetings booked: 8-12. The math works for a founder who can close deals but can't afford the time or money for a human SDR.

AiSDR publishes its starting price at $750 per month, making it the most transparent and affordable option in the AI SDR category. Higher tiers with increased sending volumes, advanced personalization, and priority support are available at custom pricing.

Try it for 60-90 days, measure meetings booked, and make a data-driven decision about continuing, upgrading, or switching to a different platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does AiSDR compare to 11x?

AiSDR is 85% cheaper ($750/month vs $5K+) but offers shallower prospect research and less sophisticated personalization. 11x reads SEC filings and earnings calls. AiSDR pulls from LinkedIn and company pages. For volume outbound to mid-market prospects, AiSDR delivers solid results. For deep-research enterprise outbound, 11x's research advantage matters more.

Do I need a separate data provider with AiSDR?

Yes. Unlike Artisan, AiSDR doesn't include a built-in contact database. You'll need to upload lists or connect a data provider like Apollo, ZoomInfo, or Lusha. Factor in $200-$1,500/month for data on top of AiSDR's pricing. Apollo's free tier can work for initial testing.

How quickly can I start sending with AiSDR?

Setup takes a few hours. Upload or connect your contact data, define your ICP and messaging guidelines, and AiSDR starts generating emails the same day. Plan for 3-5 days of reviewing output quality before letting it send autonomously. Domain warm-up may take 1-2 weeks if you're starting from a cold sending domain.

What email volume can AiSDR handle?

Base plans support meaningful daily volumes suitable for most mid-market outbound programs. Specific volume limits depend on your plan tier and email infrastructure. AiSDR manages sending pace and deliverability, but you're still bound by your email provider's limits and domain reputation. Start conservatively and scale volume as deliverability stabilizes.

Is AiSDR good enough for enterprise outbound?

For enterprise accounts with complex buying committees and high deal values, AiSDR's lighter research may fall short. Enterprise outbound demands deep personalization that references specific business challenges. AiSDR handles mid-market and SMB outbound well. For enterprise targets, consider 11x or supplement AiSDR with manual research on your highest-value accounts.
