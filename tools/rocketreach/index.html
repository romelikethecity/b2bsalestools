The Bottom Line

RocketReach fills a narrow but real niche: simple, affordable email lookups for individuals who don't need a full sales platform. Recruiters, solo sellers, and PR professionals who just need to find someone's email address get solid value from the Essentials plan at $39/month.

The platform struggles to justify itself for sales teams doing systematic outbound. No sequencing, no intent data, no dialer, limited phone accuracy. Apollo provides all of those features plus a larger verified email database at a comparable price point. For teams of 3+, the total cost of RocketReach plus a separate engagement tool almost always exceeds Apollo's all-in-one pricing.

Buy RocketReach if you're an individual user who needs a dedicated email finder with a clean interface and you already have your outreach workflow sorted. Skip it if you're building a sales stack from scratch. Apollo or even Lusha will give you more value per dollar in that scenario.

RocketReach Overview

RocketReach is a focused email and phone finder used heavily by recruiters and individual sellers. The platform indexes over 700M professional profiles and 35M+ companies, making it one of the larger databases available. Their core competency is finding verified email addresses, where accuracy rates run 85-90% across their database.

The product is deliberately simple. You search for a person or company, get their contact info, and move on. There's no built-in sequencing, no intent data, no conversation intelligence. RocketReach does one thing and does it competently. That simplicity makes it popular with recruiters and sales reps who already have their outreach tools and just need reliable contact data.

Pricing starts at $39/month for the Essentials plan, which includes email lookups only. Pro at $99/month adds phone numbers. Ultimate at $249/month adds team features, API access, and higher lookup limits. The per-seat pricing is straightforward, but the credit-based model shares the same scaling problem as Lusha. Teams doing high-volume prospecting will run through credits fast.

RocketReach's biggest weakness is the platform layer around the data. The search interface is basic. Filtering options are limited compared to Apollo or ZoomInfo. Company data (technographics, firmographics, intent signals) is thin. If you need more than an email finder with a Chrome extension, you'll outgrow RocketReach quickly.

Pros & Cons

  • Large email database with solid accuracy700M+ professional profiles give RocketReach broad coverage across industries and geographies. Email accuracy of 85-90% is competitive for the price point. The platform works especially well for finding personal and professional email addresses for executive-level contacts.
  • Simple, focused user experienceRocketReach avoids feature bloat. Search for a contact, get their info, export. The Chrome extension works on LinkedIn, company websites, and other pages. Reps can look up a contact in under 10 seconds. There's almost zero learning curve.
  • Strong for recruiting use casesRocketReach's search by skills, company, and title makes it a natural fit for recruiters who need to find candidate contact info quickly. Many recruiting teams use it alongside LinkedIn Recruiter to get personal email addresses and phone numbers that LinkedIn doesn't expose.
  • Reasonable pricing for individualsAt $39/month for email-only lookups, RocketReach is one of the most affordable dedicated contact finder tools. Solo recruiters and individual sellers get solid value without committing to $15K+ annual contracts.
  • Phone data is limited and less accurateRocketReach's phone data lags behind Lusha, Cognism, and ZoomInfo. Direct dial connect rates run 40-55%, meaning more than half your calls won't reach the right person. Phone numbers are only available on the Pro plan ($99/month) and above.
  • No built-in engagement toolsRocketReach provides data only. No sequencing, no email sending, no dialer, no LinkedIn automation. Every contact you find needs to be exported to a separate tool for outreach. Apollo bundles all of this at a lower total cost.
  • Basic search and filteringThe prospecting interface is functional but shallow. You won't find technographic filters, funding alerts, hiring signals, or intent data. Building complex prospect lists with multiple qualifying criteria requires exporting to another tool and filtering there.
  • Email accuracy below top-tier providers85-90% email accuracy is acceptable but sits below ZoomInfo (95%+) and Apollo (91-93%). For high-volume cold email campaigns, that 5-10% gap means more bounces, which damages sender reputation over time.

Use Cases

Executive Recruiter Sourcing C-Suite Candidates

An executive recruiter uses RocketReach to find personal email addresses for VP and C-level candidates identified on LinkedIn. They search by name and company, then export the personal email to bypass corporate spam filters. The recruiter contacts 20-30 candidates per week and finds that personal email outreach generates 3x the response rate of LinkedIn InMail.

Solo Consultant Building a Client Pipeline

An independent management consultant uses RocketReach Essentials ($39/month) to find email addresses for decision-makers at target companies. They research prospects on LinkedIn, use the Chrome extension to grab verified emails, and send personalized outreach through Gmail. The workflow is basic but effective for someone managing 50-100 prospects at a time.

PR Team Finding Journalist Contacts

A PR agency uses RocketReach to find verified email addresses for journalists and editors at target publications. The search-by-company feature lets them quickly build media lists for specific outlets. They export contacts to their PR platform (Cision or Muck Rack) for outreach tracking. The 700M+ profile database covers most journalists at major publications.

Pricing Analysis

RocketReach offers three published plans. Essentials at $39/month includes email lookups with 80 lookups per month. Pro at $99/month adds phone numbers with 200 lookups per month. Ultimate at $249/month includes 500 lookups per month, API access, and team management features.

Annual billing saves approximately 40% across all plans. Additional lookup credits can be purchased if you exceed your monthly allotment. The API is metered separately on the Ultimate plan.

One thing to watch: the per-lookup cost varies significantly by plan. Essentials works out to $0.49 per lookup, Pro to $0.50 per lookup, and Ultimate to $0.50 per lookup. The per-unit economics don't improve much as you move up tiers. You're paying more for phone data access and higher monthly caps, not better per-contact pricing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is RocketReach?

RocketReach's email accuracy runs 85-90% for verified addresses. Phone number accuracy is lower, around 40-55% for direct dials. Email accuracy varies by industry and seniority level. Executive contacts at large companies tend to have the most reliable data.

Is RocketReach better than Apollo?

For simple email lookups by individuals who already have outreach tools, RocketReach is a clean, focused option. For anything beyond basic lookups, Apollo wins. Apollo bundles data, sequencing, a dialer, and LinkedIn integration starting at $49/month with unlimited emails. The total cost of ownership favors Apollo for any team doing systematic outbound.

Does RocketReach have a free plan?

RocketReach offers a limited free trial with 5 lookups. There is no ongoing free plan like Apollo (10K credits/month) or Lusha (5 credits/month). After the trial, you need a paid plan starting at $39/month to continue using the platform.

Can RocketReach find personal emails?

Yes. RocketReach can surface personal email addresses (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo) in addition to business emails. This is particularly useful for recruiters reaching out to passive candidates who may not check their work email frequently, or for contacting executives who change companies regularly.

Does RocketReach integrate with CRMs?

RocketReach integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Zapier. The integrations allow direct export of contacts to your CRM with field mapping. API access on the Ultimate plan enables custom integrations with other platforms. The Salesforce integration is the most feature-complete.
