The Bottom Line

ZoomInfo is the best B2B data platform available if you can afford it. The database size, accuracy, and breadth of features are unmatched. Intent data, website visitor tracking, and conversation intelligence (via Chorus) make it a legitimate go-to-market operating system, not just a data vendor.

The problem is cost. At $15K+ per year for a small team, ZoomInfo prices out most startups and SMBs. And the platform is complex enough that teams without a dedicated admin or RevOps lead often underuse it. If you're buying ZoomInfo, make sure you have the process maturity and headcount to actually use what you're paying for.

For teams with the budget and the GTM sophistication to exploit the full platform, ZoomInfo delivers clear ROI. For everyone else, Apollo.io provides 80% of the core data functionality at a fraction of the cost. The right choice depends on your team size, budget, and how much you value intent data and phone numbers.

ZoomInfo Overview

ZoomInfo is the 800-pound gorilla of B2B sales data. With over 321 million professional profiles and 104 million company records, it's the largest commercial contact database on the market. The company went public in 2020 and has been on an acquisition spree since, buying Chorus (conversation intelligence), RingLead (data orchestration), and Comparably (employer brand data). Annual revenue crossed $1.2 billion in 2023.

The platform does more than just sell contact info. ZoomInfo's intent data, website visitor tracking, and workflow automation tools make it a full go-to-market operating system. Their SalesOS, MarketingOS, and TalentOS products cover sales prospecting, demand generation, and recruiting use cases from a single data backbone.

The catch is cost. ZoomInfo contracts start around $15,000/year for a small team and can climb past $100,000 for enterprise deployments. Auto-renewal clauses and multi-year commitments are standard. Teams that don't fully adopt the platform often find themselves paying for features they don't use. The ROI is there for organizations with the budget and the process maturity to exploit it.

Data accuracy is ZoomInfo's strongest selling point. Independent tests consistently place their email verification rates above 95% for direct dials and business emails. Phone number accuracy runs around 70-80%, which is the highest in the market but still means 1 in 4 numbers won't connect. European data coverage improved significantly after the Cognism partnership ended, but EMEA accuracy still trails North American data.

Pros & Cons

  • Largest B2B database available321M+ professional profiles and 104M+ company records give ZoomInfo the widest coverage in the market. You'll find contacts at companies competitors simply don't have in their databases. This matters most when prospecting into mid-market or niche verticals.
  • Best-in-class data accuracyEmail accuracy above 95% and the highest direct dial accuracy available (70-80%). ZoomInfo invests heavily in data verification with a combination of AI, manual research, and contributor data from their community.
  • Full go-to-market platformIntent data, website visitor identification, workflow automation, and conversation intelligence (via Chorus) make ZoomInfo more than a data provider. Enterprise teams can run prospecting, engagement, and analytics from one platform.
  • Strong intent data signalsZoomInfo's Streaming Intent tracks buying signals across 6,000+ topics in real time. Combined with website visitor tracking, you can identify accounts actively researching solutions in your category before they fill out a form.
  • Expensive with aggressive contractsStarting at $15K/year for small teams and scaling to $100K+ for enterprise. Multi-year commitments and auto-renewal clauses are standard. Canceling or downsizing mid-contract is difficult. Ask about the cancellation window during negotiation.
  • Complex platform with steep learning curveThe breadth of features means most teams use less than 30% of what they're paying for. Onboarding takes 2-4 weeks minimum. Without a dedicated admin or RevOps lead, adoption often stalls after the initial setup.
  • European data still trails North AmericaEMEA coverage has improved but gaps remain, especially for SMB contacts in Southern and Eastern Europe. If your ICP is primarily European, test accuracy on a sample of your target accounts before committing to a contract.
  • Phone number accuracy is imperfectDirect dials connect about 70-80% of the time. That's the best in the market, but SDR teams making 100+ calls a day will still hit 20-30 wrong numbers daily. Mobile numbers are even less reliable.

Pricing Analysis

ZoomInfo's pricing is opaque by design. Published starting prices show Professional at $14,995/year and Advanced at $24,995/year, but real-world pricing varies significantly based on seat count, credit volume, and add-ons.

Expect to negotiate. First-year discounts of 15-30% are common for new customers. Multi-year deals (2-3 years) typically include better per-seat pricing but lock you into auto-renewal. Ask specifically about the auto-renewal cancellation window, which is usually 60-90 days before renewal.

Add-ons that drive cost up include: Streaming Intent ($5K-15K/year), Chorus licenses ($1,200-1,600/seat/year), and additional data credits beyond your base allotment. Enterprise teams with 50+ seats often negotiate custom packages that bundle everything.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is ZoomInfo data?

ZoomInfo's email accuracy runs above 95% for verified business emails. Direct dial phone numbers connect about 70-80% of the time, the highest rate in the B2B data market. Mobile numbers are less reliable, connecting roughly 50-60% of the time. Accuracy varies by region, with North American data being the strongest.

How much does ZoomInfo cost per year?

ZoomInfo contracts typically start at $14,995/year for a small team (2-3 seats) on the Professional plan. Advanced plans with intent data start around $24,995/year. Enterprise deployments with full platform access often run $50,000-$150,000/year depending on seats, credits, and add-ons. First-year discounts of 15-30% are negotiable.

Can I cancel my ZoomInfo contract early?

ZoomInfo contracts are annual or multi-year with auto-renewal clauses. Early cancellation isn't typically allowed, but you can choose not to renew. The cancellation window is usually 60-90 days before your renewal date. Miss that window and you're locked in for another year automatically.

How does ZoomInfo compare to Apollo?

ZoomInfo has a larger database (321M vs 270M contacts), better data accuracy (especially for phone numbers), and more advanced features like intent data and website visitor tracking. Apollo costs a fraction of the price ($49/mo vs $15K/year) and includes built-in email sequencing. For teams under 20 reps with limited budgets, Apollo delivers 80% of the value at 5% of the cost.

Does ZoomInfo integrate with Salesforce and HubSpot?

Yes. ZoomInfo offers native integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics, and most major CRMs. The Salesforce integration is the most mature, with bi-directional sync, automated enrichment, and the ability to push ZoomInfo data directly into records. HubSpot integration is solid but has fewer automation options.
