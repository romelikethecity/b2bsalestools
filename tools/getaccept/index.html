The Bottom Line

GetAccept Overview

GetAccept bundles three capabilities that most competitors sell separately: deal rooms, e-signatures, and video messaging. The combination creates a single platform where sellers can share content, track engagement, send personalized videos, and close deals with legally binding signatures without switching tools. For teams that want an all-in-one solution and don't want to manage integrations between a deal room, a proposal tool, and a video platform, GetAccept eliminates the stitching.

The deal room functionality covers the standard features: shared workspaces for deal content, document organization, mutual action plans, and buyer engagement tracking. What separates GetAccept is the native video messaging that lets reps record personalized walkthrough videos embedded directly in the deal room. A rep can record a 90-second overview of the proposal, address specific stakeholder concerns, and attach the video to the relevant section. Buyers engage with video at higher rates than text, and GetAccept's data shows that deals with video messages close 26% faster.

The e-signature capability is baked into the deal room rather than requiring a separate tool or integration. Contracts go from the same workspace where the buyer reviewed the proposal, pricing, and case studies. The signature flow is smooth: the buyer reviews the deal room content, reaches the contract section, and signs electronically. No switching to DocuSign, no separate email with a signing link. The reduced friction matters at the critical moment when a deal moves from verbal agreement to executed contract.

Pricing starts at $15/user/month and scales to $39/user/month for the full-featured plan, making GetAccept the most affordable deal room that includes e-signatures and video. The trade-off is that no single capability matches the best-of-breed alternative. The deal room isn't as polished as Dock. The e-signature isn't as feature-rich as DocuSign. The video messaging isn't as capable as Vidyard. But the combined value of all three in one platform, at this price point, makes GetAccept compelling for teams that prioritize efficiency over perfection in any single category.

Pros & Cons

  • Three tools in one platform at a low priceGetAccept combines deal rooms, e-signatures, and video messaging starting at $15/user/month. Buying these capabilities separately (Dock + DocuSign + Vidyard) would cost $80-$120/user/month. The bundled approach saves money and eliminates integration maintenance. For budget-conscious teams, the value proposition is strong.
  • Native video messaging increases buyer engagementReps record personalized videos within the deal room that buyers watch alongside documents and proposals. GetAccept reports that deals with embedded video messages see 41% higher engagement rates. The video feature doesn't require a separate recording tool or embedding workflow. It's point, click, record, and the video appears in the buyer's deal room immediately.
  • E-signature flow reduces closing frictionBuyers review content, watch videos, and sign contracts in the same workspace without switching platforms. The e-signature captures legally binding signatures with audit trails and compliance documentation. Removing the 'now go check your email for the DocuSign link' step keeps momentum at the critical moment when the buyer is ready to commit.
  • Solid engagement analytics across all content typesGetAccept tracks engagement across documents, videos, and the deal room itself. Reps see who viewed which content, how long they watched videos, when they opened the contract, and where they paused. The unified analytics across all content types give a complete picture of buyer activity in one dashboard.
  • No single feature is best-in-classThe deal room is good but trails Dock's design quality. The e-signature works but lacks DocuSign's advanced workflow options. The video messaging is functional but doesn't match Vidyard's editing and hosting capabilities. Teams that need the best possible version of any one capability will find GetAccept competent but not exceptional in each area.
  • Interface can feel cluttered with multiple modulesCombining deal rooms, e-signatures, and video in one platform creates a denser interface than single-purpose tools. New users may find the navigation confusing as they figure out where deal room features end and e-signature or video features begin. The learning curve is 1-2 weeks for full proficiency, longer than simpler tools like Flowla.
  • Video quality depends on rep effort and environmentThe video messaging feature is only as good as the videos reps record. Without coaching on video length, lighting, and messaging, some reps create videos that hurt more than help. The tool makes recording easy, but it doesn't make reps good on camera. Teams need to invest in video coaching alongside tool deployment.
  • E-signature feature may not satisfy heavy compliance needsGetAccept's e-signature handles standard B2B contracts well, but organizations with complex signing workflows (multiple signers in sequence, wet signature requirements, industry-specific compliance) may find the capabilities insufficient. Financial services, healthcare, and government contracts often need DocuSign or Adobe Sign level compliance features.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can GetAccept replace DocuSign?

For standard B2B contracts with 1-3 signers, yes. GetAccept's e-signature handles legally binding signatures with audit trails and compliance documentation. For complex signing workflows with sequential routing, conditional fields, power forms, or industry-specific compliance (healthcare HIPAA, financial SOX), DocuSign's deeper feature set is still necessary. Evaluate based on your specific contract complexity.

How does GetAccept's video messaging work?

Reps record videos directly within the GetAccept platform using their webcam and screen share. Videos can be recorded in advance or captured just before sending. The video embeds in the deal room alongside documents and proposals. Buyers watch without downloading or switching to another platform. Analytics track video views, watch duration, and replay behavior. No separate Loom or Vidyard subscription needed.

What CRM integrations does GetAccept support?

GetAccept integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, and Microsoft Dynamics for CRM sync. It also connects with engagement platforms like Outreach and Salesloft. The integration library is broader than most deal room competitors because GetAccept has been in market longer. Activity logging, deal room engagement, and signature status sync back to CRM records automatically.

How does GetAccept compare to Dock?

Dock has a better deal room UI and buyer experience. GetAccept bundles e-signatures and video messaging that Dock doesn't offer. If you only need a deal room, Dock is the better product. If you need deal rooms plus e-signatures plus video in one platform at a lower total cost, GetAccept wins on value. The decision depends on whether you prefer best-in-class deal rooms or a bundled multi-tool approach.

Is GetAccept's e-signature legally binding?

Yes. GetAccept's e-signatures comply with ESIGN Act (US), eIDAS (EU), and equivalent regulations in most jurisdictions. Each signature includes a full audit trail with timestamps, IP addresses, and authentication records. The signatures are legally valid for standard business contracts. For specialized industries with additional compliance requirements, verify GetAccept's specific certifications against your regulatory needs.
