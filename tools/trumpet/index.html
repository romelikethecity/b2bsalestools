The Bottom Line

Trumpet is the deal room for teams that want their sales materials to look as good as their product. The visual quality of Pods is a step above standard deal rooms, and for companies where brand impression influences buying decisions, that matters. Creative agencies, design-led SaaS companies, and premium service providers will get the most mileage from Trumpet's aesthetic advantage.

The scrolling microsite format works well for narrative-driven selling. Walking a buyer through a structured story from problem to proof to pricing creates a more compelling experience than dropping them into a folder of files. The trade-off is price. At $45-$79/user/month with no free tier, Trumpet costs more than Dock or Aligned while offering fewer deal execution features. If mutual action plans, buyer intent scoring, or a free evaluation period matter to you, look elsewhere. Buy Trumpet if visual impact is a differentiator in your sales process and you're willing to pay the premium for it.

Trumpet Overview

Trumpet is a UK-based deal room platform that calls its workspaces 'Pods.' The branding is distinctive, but the product behind it is what matters: visually appealing, interactive digital spaces where sellers organize deal content, track buyer engagement, and move deals forward. Trumpet's design sensibility leans toward the creative side, producing deal rooms that look more like branded microsites than file-sharing folders. For companies that care about how their sales materials reflect their brand, that aesthetic matters.

Each Pod can contain videos, documents, proposals, pricing tables, mutual action plans, and embedded content from other tools. The layout uses a vertical scrolling format that feels more like browsing a landing page than navigating a portal. Buyers scroll through sections in a guided sequence while still being able to jump to specific areas. The format works well for storytelling (walking a prospect through problem, solution, proof, next steps) and for deals where the visual presentation of information influences buying decisions.

Trumpet's analytics track Pod visits, content engagement, and individual stakeholder activity. Reps see which sections buyers scroll through, where they spend time, and which documents they download. The analytics are solid without being overwhelming. Email notifications alert reps when key stakeholders visit the Pod. For teams transitioning from email-based content sharing, the visibility into buyer behavior is a significant upgrade.

Pricing runs $45-$79/user/month, which puts Trumpet at the higher end of the mid-market deal room category. The product competes with Dock ($49) and Aligned ($29-$49) while offering a distinct visual approach. UK and European sales teams will appreciate the GDPR compliance built into the platform's DNA, and the UK-based support team operates in European time zones. For North American teams, the time zone gap for support is worth considering.

Pros & Cons

  • Visually striking deal rooms that reflect brand qualityTrumpet's Pods look polished and intentional. The design templates produce rooms that feel like branded experiences rather than utilitarian file shares. For companies selling creative services, design tools, or premium products, the visual quality of the deal room reinforces the brand message. First impressions in a deal room matter, and Trumpet makes a strong one.
  • Guided scrolling format works for storytellingThe vertical scroll layout walks buyers through content in a narrative sequence. Sellers can structure the Pod as a story: problem statement, solution overview, case studies, pricing, next steps. This format is more engaging than the folder-and-file approach that most deal rooms use. Buyers consume the content in context instead of jumping between disconnected documents.
  • Strong engagement analytics with stakeholder trackingTrumpet tracks individual stakeholder activity across every section and document in the Pod. Reps see who visited, what they viewed, and how long they engaged. Real-time notifications fire when key contacts open the Pod. The analytics provide enough signal to time follow-ups and personalize outreach without drowning reps in data they won't use.
  • Pricing is high for the feature setAt $45-$79/user/month, Trumpet costs more than Dock ($49 with a free tier) and Aligned ($29-$49) while offering comparable or fewer features. The visual design is the premium you're paying for. Teams that don't place high value on aesthetics will find better ROI with Dock's free plan or Aligned's mutual action plan depth at a lower price point.
  • Smaller ecosystem and integration libraryAs a UK-based company with a smaller customer base than US competitors, Trumpet's integration ecosystem is more limited. The core CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot) work well, but niche integrations, marketplace apps, and third-party extensions are fewer. Teams with complex tech stacks may need workarounds for tools that Dock or GetAccept support natively.
  • Scrolling format can feel long on content-heavy dealsThe vertical scroll works beautifully for deals with 5-8 content sections. Enterprise deals with 20+ documents, multiple appendices, and extensive technical specs can produce Pods that feel like infinitely scrolling pages. For content-heavy deals, a tabbed or folder-based navigation (like Dock's sections) may organize information more efficiently.
  • UK-based support creates time zone friction for US teamsTrumpet's support team operates primarily in UK/European hours. US-based teams on Pacific time may experience 8-10 hour response delays for support tickets. The product works well enough that daily support isn't needed, but during initial setup or troubleshooting, the time zone gap can slow resolution.

Pricing Analysis

Trumpet's pricing starts at $45/user/month for the standard plan and goes up to $79/user/month for the full-featured plan. The higher tier includes advanced analytics, custom branding, CRM integrations, and priority support. Enterprise pricing with SSO and dedicated support is available on request.

Annual billing is standard, and month-to-month options carry a premium. For a 10-person sales team on the mid-tier plan, expect to spend roughly $7,200-$9,500 per year. That's 20-40% more than Dock or Aligned for comparable team sizes.

Trumpet does not offer a free tier, which means teams need to commit to a paid plan to evaluate the product with real deals. A 14-day free trial is available. When comparing total cost, factor in the design quality advantage and whether your brand and sales process benefit from the visual premium that Trumpet delivers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Trumpet Pod?

A Pod is Trumpet's term for a deal room. It's a branded, interactive digital workspace where sellers organize content, track engagement, and guide buyers through the deal process. Pods use a vertical scrolling format with sections for videos, documents, pricing, mutual plans, and embedded content. Each Pod gets a unique URL that sellers share with prospects.

How does Trumpet compare to Dock?

Both offer strong deal room experiences, but the approach differs. Dock uses a section-based layout with a cleaner, more minimal design and offers a free tier. Trumpet uses a scrolling microsite format with stronger visual design but costs more ($45-$79 vs. $49). Choose Dock for simplicity and value. Choose Trumpet for visual impact and brand-forward deals.

Is Trumpet GDPR compliant?

Yes. As a UK-based company, Trumpet builds GDPR compliance into the platform's core architecture rather than treating it as an add-on. Data processing and storage comply with EU and UK data protection regulations. For European sales teams or any team selling to European buyers, this native compliance simplifies procurement and legal review processes.

Can I embed videos in Trumpet Pods?

Yes. Trumpet supports embedded videos from Loom, YouTube, Vimeo, and native video uploads. Videos can be placed anywhere within the Pod's scrolling layout. Engagement analytics track whether buyers watched videos, how far they got, and whether they replayed sections. Video is one of Trumpet's strongest content types given the visual, scrolling format.

Does Trumpet integrate with Salesforce?

Yes. Trumpet integrates with Salesforce and HubSpot for CRM sync. Pod activity, engagement data, and stakeholder interactions sync back to the CRM record. The integration handles activity logging and basic bi-directional sync. The integration library is smaller than US-based competitors, so check compatibility with your specific tech stack before committing.
