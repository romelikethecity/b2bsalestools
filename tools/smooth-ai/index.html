The Bottom Line

smooth.AI occupies an awkward position in the market. The real-time search concept is compelling. Finding fresh contacts that static databases haven't indexed is a genuine advantage for certain prospecting scenarios. But the inconsistent accuracy, aggressive sales practices, and opaque pricing undermine the value proposition.

For most sales teams, Apollo delivers better data accuracy, more features (sequencing, dialer, analytics), and a more transparent pricing model at a lower cost. ZoomInfo provides superior accuracy for teams with larger budgets. Lusha beats smooth.AI on phone number accuracy for targeted lookups. smooth.AI's best use case is high-volume teams that need unlimited lookups and can tolerate 10-15% bounce rates with a secondary verification layer.

Buy smooth.AI if you're doing very high-volume prospecting (100+ contacts per rep daily), you're targeting emerging or fast-moving industries where static databases go stale quickly, and you're comfortable with variable data quality. For everyone else, Apollo or ZoomInfo will serve you better.

Seamless.AI Overview

smooth.AI takes a different approach than most B2B data providers. Instead of maintaining a static database, the platform searches the web in real time to find and verify contact information when you run a query. The idea is that real-time search produces fresher data than databases that update on weekly or monthly cycles. In practice, the results are mixed.

The company claims access to 1.9 billion contact and company records. That number includes unverified records, so the usable database is smaller than it sounds. smooth.AI has raised over $70M in funding and built a user base of 500,000+ sellers. They've added features like a writing assistant (powered by AI) and buyer intent data, pushing toward being a fuller prospecting platform.

Pricing is opaque. The free plan gives you 50 credits to test the platform. Basic starts at $147/month. Pro and Enterprise are custom-priced. Many users report that the sales process for smooth.AI itself is aggressive, with high-pressure tactics, extended trial-to-paid conversion calls, and contracts that are difficult to cancel. The irony of a sales tool company using hard-sell tactics on its own customers isn't lost on the B2B community.

Data quality is where smooth.AI polarizes users. Some reps report finding fresh, accurate contacts that other databases miss. Others see bounce rates north of 15% and phone numbers that go to main switchboards instead of direct lines. The real-time search model means results vary significantly by industry, company size, and geography. Your experience depends heavily on who you're targeting.

Pros & Cons

  • Real-time search surfaces fresh contact dataInstead of pulling from a static database, smooth.AI searches the web at query time to find and verify contacts. This can surface recently changed job titles, new email addresses, and contacts that haven't made it into competitors' databases yet. For fast-moving industries with high turnover, this freshness matters.
  • Unlimited contacts on higher-tier plansPro and Enterprise plans offer unlimited contact searches, which removes the per-credit math that makes tools like Lusha and RocketReach expensive at scale. SDR teams doing 100+ lookups per day get predictable costs regardless of volume.
  • Built-in AI writing assistantsmooth.AI includes an AI-powered writing tool that generates personalized email copy based on prospect data. It's not a replacement for a dedicated copywriting tool, but it saves reps 5-10 minutes per prospect on initial outreach drafts. The writing quality is adequate for first-touch cold emails.
  • Buyer intent data includedHigher-tier plans include buyer intent signals that flag accounts researching topics relevant to your product. Combined with the real-time search model, this lets reps find and contact buying-stage prospects in a single workflow.
  • Inconsistent data accuracyBounce rates vary widely. Some users report 90%+ deliverability, others see 15-20% bounces. The real-time search model means accuracy depends heavily on the quality of public data available for your specific prospects. Enterprise contacts at well-known companies tend to be accurate. SMB contacts in niche industries are less reliable.
  • Aggressive sales tactics and difficult cancellationsmooth.AI's own sales process is a frequent complaint. Extended demo calls, high-pressure closing, and contracts with auto-renewal clauses that are hard to cancel. Multiple review sites flag this as a consistent issue. Read the contract terms carefully before signing.
  • Phone numbers frequently route to main linesMany phone numbers returned by smooth.AI are company switchboard numbers rather than direct dials. Reps expecting direct connections will spend time navigating automated phone trees. Direct dial accuracy sits around 40-50%, below Lusha (75-80%) and ZoomInfo (70-80%).
  • Search results can be slow and inconsistentBecause smooth.AI searches in real time rather than querying a pre-built database, some searches take 10-30 seconds to return results. Batch searches can take minutes. Results for the same query can differ between searches as the system hits different data sources.

Use Cases

High-Volume SDR Team Doing Outbound Blitzes

A 15-person SDR team uses smooth.AI's unlimited plan to run daily prospecting sprints. Each rep searches for 100+ contacts per day across their territory without worrying about credit limits. They accept that 10-15% of emails will bounce and use a verification tool (NeverBounce) as a secondary filter before loading contacts into their sequencing platform.

Startup Seller Prospecting Into Emerging Markets

A seller at a startup targeting a new vertical (climate tech companies) uses smooth.AI to find contacts at companies too new or too small to appear in ZoomInfo or Apollo's databases. The real-time search model surfaces recently created company profiles and contacts that static databases haven't indexed yet.

Marketing Team Building Event Invite Lists

A field marketing team uses smooth.AI to build invite lists for regional events. They search by geography, title, and company size to identify 500+ prospects per event. The unlimited plan lets them build large lists without credit constraints. They export to HubSpot for email invitations and track RSVPs through the CRM.

Pricing Analysis

smooth.AI's pricing is partially published but the details get murky fast. The free plan gives you 50 credits to evaluate the platform. Basic starts at $147/month and includes 250 credits per month. Pro pricing is custom and adds unlimited searches, intent data, and the AI writing assistant. Enterprise includes everything plus dedicated support, advanced integrations, and custom onboarding.

Annual contracts are standard on Pro and Enterprise tiers. Month-to-month billing is available on Basic but at a premium. Multiple users report that cancellation requires calling a retention team and navigating a multi-step process. Get cancellation terms in writing before signing.

The credit system on Basic counts each contact reveal as 1 credit. At 250 credits/month and $147/month, each contact costs $0.59. Pro's unlimited model drops that per-contact cost dramatically for high-volume teams, which is the primary reason to upgrade.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is smooth.AI accurate?

Accuracy varies significantly by target segment. Email deliverability ranges from 80-92% depending on the industry and company size you're targeting. Enterprise contacts at well-known companies tend to be the most accurate. SMB contacts and niche industries have higher bounce rates. Phone numbers frequently route to company switchboards rather than direct lines.

How much does smooth.AI cost?

The free plan includes 50 credits. Basic starts at $147/month with 250 credits. Pro and Enterprise plans are custom-priced and include unlimited searches. Annual contracts are standard for Pro and above. Expect to pay $2,000-$5,000/year for individual use and $10,000-$30,000/year for teams, depending on features and seats.

Is smooth.AI better than Apollo?

For most teams, no. Apollo provides a larger verified database, built-in sequencing, a dialer, and LinkedIn integration starting at $49/month. smooth.AI's real-time search model can surface fresher data in specific scenarios, but the inconsistent accuracy and higher price point make Apollo the better all-around choice. smooth.AI's unlimited plan may make sense for teams doing 100+ lookups per rep daily.

Can I cancel smooth.AI?

Cancellation is possible but widely reported as difficult. Multiple review sites mention aggressive retention calls and complex cancellation processes. The auto-renewal clause in contracts means you need to cancel within a specific window (usually 30-60 days) before renewal. Get the cancellation process documented in writing when you sign up.

Does smooth.AI integrate with Salesforce?

Yes. smooth.AI integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, Salesloft, and other major CRM and engagement platforms. The integrations support direct contact export and basic field mapping. The Salesforce integration works but is less feature-rich than ZoomInfo or Apollo's native integrations.
