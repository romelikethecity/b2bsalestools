The Bottom Line

11x is the premium bet in the AI SDR category. Alice's prospect research is the deepest available, the multi-agent approach with Jordan adds phone coverage, and the full autonomy means your team spends zero time on manual outbound tasks. For companies with the budget and the right ICP, 11x can deliver outbound coverage that would require 2-3 human SDRs.

The risks are real. $5,000+/month is a significant commitment for a category that's less than two years old. Deliverability management requires attention. Output quality varies by ICP clarity and prospect data availability. And you're trusting an AI to represent your brand in first-touch communications with potential customers. That trust needs to be earned during a pilot, not assumed from a demo.

Buy 11x if you're spending $100K+/year on outbound SDRs and want to test whether AI can handle a meaningful portion of that workload. Start with a defined pilot targeting one ICP segment. Measure meetings booked, not emails sent. If Alice delivers at 50%+ of a human SDR's meeting rate at a fraction of the cost, expand. If she doesn't, you've learned something valuable about your market's readiness for AI outreach.

11x Overview

11x builds AI workers that replace human SDRs. Their flagship agent, Alice, handles outbound prospecting autonomously. She researches prospects, writes personalized emails, sends follow-ups, and books meetings on your calendar. A second agent, Jordan, handles AI-powered phone outreach. The company raised $24M in Series A funding in 2024 and has been one of the most visible players in the AI SDR space, marketing aggressively on LinkedIn and at sales conferences.

Alice works by ingesting your ICP definition, connecting to your email infrastructure, and then running the full outbound workflow without human intervention. She pulls prospect data from built-in sources, researches each contact's company and role, crafts personalized emails referencing recent news or job changes, and manages multi-step follow-up sequences. The personalization goes deeper than mail merge tokens. Alice reads 10-K filings, press releases, and LinkedIn activity to find angles that feel hand-written.

The quality of Alice's output varies. On well-defined ICPs with clear pain points, the emails read impressively well. On ambiguous or broad ICPs, the personalization can feel forced or miss the mark entirely. The research is only as good as what's publicly available about each prospect, so targeting executives at companies with thin public footprints produces weaker results. You'll want to review output carefully during the first few weeks to calibrate expectations.

Pricing starts at $5,000 per month, which positions 11x as a premium option in the AI SDR category. The pitch is straightforward: Alice costs less than a human SDR's fully loaded salary ($6,000-$8,000/month), works 24/7, and scales without headcount. Whether that math holds depends entirely on conversion rates, which are still unproven at scale across diverse industries. Early adopters report mixed results, with some seeing meeting volumes comparable to a mid-tier human SDR and others struggling with deliverability and relevance.

Pros & Cons

  • Deepest prospect research in the categoryAlice pulls from SEC filings, press releases, LinkedIn activity, job postings, and company news to write emails that reference specific, timely details. The research depth exceeds what most human SDRs invest per prospect. This matters most for enterprise selling where generic outreach gets ignored.
  • True end-to-end autonomyFrom prospect identification through email send and follow-up, Alice runs the entire workflow without manual steps. Most competitors still require humans to review, approve, or trigger sends. 11x's full autonomy is a differentiator for teams that want a set-and-forget outbound engine.
  • Multi-agent approach with Jordan for phonesJordan adds AI phone outreach alongside Alice's email, making 11x one of the few platforms offering multi-channel AI SDR capabilities. Phone and email can work in coordinated sequences. The combination is still early, but the vision of a complete AI SDR team is compelling.
  • Premium pricing limits accessibilityAt $5,000+ per month, 11x costs more than most competitors in the AI SDR space. AiSDR starts at $750/month. Relevance AI starts at $19/month. You're paying for the research depth and full autonomy, but the ROI is unproven for many ICPs. Startups and SMBs will find the price hard to justify without clear pipeline numbers.
  • Black box decision-makingAlice decides who to contact, what to say, and when to follow up. The autonomy that makes her powerful also makes her hard to debug. When conversion rates drop, diagnosing whether the issue is targeting, messaging, timing, or deliverability requires digging through logs that aren't always intuitive to parse.
  • Deliverability risks from high-volume AI sendsAutonomous email agents sending hundreds of personalized emails daily can trigger spam filters and damage domain reputation if not carefully managed. 11x includes deliverability infrastructure, but the risk is higher than human-paced sending. Domain warm-up and monitoring are critical, and teams have reported deliverability issues during initial ramp-up.
  • Category is too new for proven ROI benchmarksAI SDRs emerged in 2023-2024. There's no five-year track record of consistent performance data. Case studies exist, but most come from the vendor or early adopters with favorable conditions. Buying 11x today requires a willingness to experiment, not just deploy.

Use Cases

Scaling Outbound Without Adding SDR Headcount

A Series B SaaS company selling to mid-market finance teams deploys Alice to run outbound alongside their 4-person SDR team. Alice handles the top-of-funnel prospecting for a new market segment (healthcare) that the company wants to test without committing headcount. She targets VP Finance and CFO personas at 200-500 employee healthcare companies, researching each prospect's recent funding rounds and regulatory filings. Over 90 days, Alice generates 47 qualified meetings in the new segment, giving the team enough data to decide whether to hire dedicated healthcare SDRs or let Alice continue autonomously.

Enterprise Account Research and Outreach

An enterprise software company targeting Fortune 500 accounts uses Alice for deep research-driven outreach. Each target account gets 15-20 minutes of AI research before the first email. Alice reads the company's latest earnings call transcript, identifies pain points mentioned by the CFO, cross-references those with the prospect's LinkedIn activity, and crafts emails that reference specific business challenges. The emails read like they came from a well-prepared AE, not an SDR running a sequence. Response rates on these high-research emails average 12%, well above the team's previous 3-4% on templated outreach.

24/7 Global Outbound Coverage

A company selling to European and Asian markets from a US-based team uses Alice and Jordan to cover time zones that human SDRs can't reach during business hours. Alice sends emails timed for each prospect's local morning. Jordan makes AI phone calls during local business hours in London, Singapore, and Sydney. The global coverage means prospects receive outreach at optimal times regardless of the seller's location. The team sees 30% higher open rates compared to US-hours-only sending, and Jordan's calls connect at rates comparable to human cold calls during prime calling windows.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 11x Alice compare to a human SDR?

Alice excels at research depth and consistency. She'll never skip prospect research or send a lazy template. She works weekends and holidays. Where she falls short is judgment. A good human SDR reads between the lines, adjusts tone mid-conversation, and knows when to go off-script. Alice follows patterns. For high-volume, research-heavy outbound, she's competitive. For nuanced enterprise selling, she's a supplement, not a replacement.

What integrations does 11x support?

11x integrates with major CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), calendar tools (Google Calendar, Outlook), and email infrastructure providers. The platform handles email sending through its own infrastructure with deliverability monitoring. API access is available for custom integrations on enterprise plans.

Is 11x suitable for small businesses?

At $5,000+/month, 11x is priced for companies with established sales teams and meaningful outbound budgets. Startups and small businesses should consider AiSDR ($750/month) or Relevance AI ($19/month) as more affordable entry points. 11x makes sense when you're spending $50K+/year on outbound and need to scale without proportional headcount growth.

How long does it take to set up 11x?

Initial setup takes 1-2 weeks. You'll define your ICP, connect email infrastructure, integrate your CRM, and configure Alice's research parameters. The first week of sending should be monitored closely to review output quality and tune the targeting. Expect 30-45 days before you have reliable data on Alice's performance for your specific ICP.

What happens to my domain reputation with AI-sent emails?

This is the biggest operational risk. AI agents sending high volumes can trigger spam filters if deliverability isn't managed carefully. 11x includes domain warm-up protocols and sending limits that ramp gradually. Use a dedicated subdomain for AI outbound to protect your primary domain. Monitor bounce rates and spam complaints weekly during the first 90 days.
