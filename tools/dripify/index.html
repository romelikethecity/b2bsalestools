The Bottom Line

Dripify is the best entry point for LinkedIn automation. At $39/month, it costs less than most business lunches and provides enough functionality for individual reps and small teams to test whether automated LinkedIn outreach works for their sales motion.

The limitations are real. Safety features lag behind Expandi. Conditional logic is basic. Integrations require workarounds. But for teams that just need to automate connection requests and follow-up messages at moderate volumes, Dripify does the job without overcomplicating the workflow.

Buy Dripify if you're new to LinkedIn automation, budget-conscious, or running simple outreach campaigns. Upgrade to Expandi when your campaigns get more sophisticated, you're managing multiple accounts, or you're running high enough volume that safety becomes a concern. Don't start with Expandi unless you know you need its advanced features.

Dripify Overview

Dripify is the budget-friendly LinkedIn automation tool. Starting at $39/month, it provides basic LinkedIn sequence automation (connection requests, messages, profile views) through a clean, simple interface. For individual reps and small teams who want to automate LinkedIn outreach without the complexity or cost of Expandi, Dripify is a solid entry point.

The platform runs in the cloud and supports multi-step sequences with basic conditions. You can set up campaigns that view a profile, send a connection request, wait 2 days, then follow up with a message if accepted. The workflow builder is drag-and-drop with minimal learning curve. Most users are running their first campaign within 30 minutes.

Dripify works with any LinkedIn account. You don't need Sales Navigator, although the search functionality is more powerful with it. The tool imports prospects from LinkedIn search results or CSV uploads, then runs your sequence against the list with configurable daily limits and timing.

The trade-off is safety. Dripify's safety features aren't as sophisticated as Expandi's. No dedicated IP addresses. Activity patterns are less randomized. For casual LinkedIn outreach at modest volumes (30-50 connection requests per day), the risk is acceptable. Agencies and heavy users running campaigns across multiple accounts face higher risk of LinkedIn restrictions.

Pros & Cons

  • Most affordable LinkedIn automationStarting at $39/month (Basic), Dripify costs 60% less than Expandi. The Pro plan at $59/month adds A/B testing and advanced analytics. For individuals testing LinkedIn automation for the first time, the low entry cost reduces financial risk.
  • Simple, clean interface with minimal learning curveThe drag-and-drop sequence builder takes minutes to learn. Campaign setup is straightforward: upload or search for prospects, build a sequence, set limits, and launch. Users consistently praise the UI as one of the most intuitive in the LinkedIn automation space.
  • Works without Sales NavigatorDripify pulls prospects from standard LinkedIn search results, not just Sales Navigator. This matters for teams that can't justify the $99+/month Sales Navigator cost per rep. You lose advanced search filters but gain LinkedIn automation at a combined lower cost.
  • Team management featuresThe Advanced plan ($79/month) includes team dashboards, shared templates, and performance analytics across reps. Sales managers can see who's running campaigns, acceptance rates, and response rates without logging into individual accounts.
  • Safety features less strong than ExpandiNo dedicated IP addresses. Activity patterns are less sophisticated. At high volumes, Dripify users face a slightly higher risk of LinkedIn account restrictions. The tool is fine for moderate daily activity (30-50 actions), but aggressive campaigns increase risk.
  • Limited conditional logic in sequencesSequences support basic if/then conditions (accepted connection → send message), but lack the complex branching and A/B testing depth of Expandi. Power users building multi-path campaigns will feel constrained.
  • Fewer integrationsDripify integrates with Zapier and Webhooks for connecting to other tools, but lacks native integrations with major CRMs and engagement platforms. Exporting data to Salesforce or HubSpot requires Zapier workflows, adding complexity and cost.
  • Analytics are basicCampaign analytics cover acceptance rates, response rates, and message stats. No deep funnel analytics, attribution modeling, or ROI tracking. Teams wanting to tie LinkedIn outreach to pipeline need to build that reporting externally.

Use Cases

Solo Consultant Building a Client Pipeline

An independent consultant uses Dripify Basic ($39/month) to automate LinkedIn outreach to potential clients. They search for decision-makers by title and industry on LinkedIn, import 200 prospects into a Dripify campaign, and run a 4-step sequence (profile view → connection request → message → follow-up). The consultant books 4-6 discovery calls per month from automated outreach while spending only 30 minutes per week managing campaigns.

Small Sales Team Testing LinkedIn Automation

A 5-person sales team uses Dripify Pro ($59/month per rep) to test LinkedIn as an outreach channel. Each rep runs 2-3 campaigns targeting different ICPs. The A/B testing feature lets them compare different connection request messages. After 60 days, they have data on which messaging and targeting works best. If LinkedIn proves productive, they'll evaluate upgrading to Expandi for better safety features.

Recruiter Automating Candidate Outreach

A recruiter uses Dripify to automate initial outreach to passive candidates. They upload a CSV of 500 target profiles and run a sequence that views profiles, sends connection requests, and follows up with role details. The automation lets them reach 500 candidates in 2 weeks instead of 2 months of manual messaging.

Pricing Analysis

Dripify offers three plans. Basic at $39/month includes core LinkedIn automation, sequence builder, and basic analytics. Pro at $59/month adds A/B testing, advanced analytics, and CSV imports. Advanced at $79/month adds team management, shared templates, and activity controls.

All plans support cloud-based execution (your computer doesn't need to be running). Annual billing saves approximately 25% across all tiers. There's a 7-day free trial on all plans.

The per-seat pricing is per LinkedIn account. Each account you want to automate needs its own Dripify subscription. The costs are still significantly lower than Expandi ($99/seat) for comparable basic functionality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Dripify safe for LinkedIn?

Dripify includes basic safety features (activity limits, delay randomization, working hours scheduling) but isn't as sophisticated as Expandi's dedicated IPs and advanced behavior mimicking. At moderate daily volumes (30-50 connection requests), the risk is acceptable. Heavy users should consider Expandi for better account protection.

Do I need Sales Navigator for Dripify?

No. Dripify works with any LinkedIn account (free, Premium, or Sales Navigator). However, Sales Navigator provides better search filters for building targeted prospect lists. Many users pair Sales Navigator for prospecting with Dripify for automated outreach.

How does Dripify compare to Expandi?

Dripify is simpler and cheaper ($39/mo vs $99/mo). Expandi is more sophisticated with better safety features, A/B testing, conditional logic, and dedicated IPs. Choose Dripify for basic LinkedIn automation on a budget. Choose Expandi if you're running complex campaigns at scale or managing multiple accounts.

Can Dripify send messages to non-connections?

Dripify can send connection requests with messages and follow-up messages to accepted connections. It cannot send InMail to non-connections (that requires a Sales Navigator subscription). The standard workflow is: send connection request → wait for acceptance → send message.

How many connection requests can I send daily?

Dripify lets you configure daily limits, but LinkedIn enforces its own caps (approximately 100 connection requests per week). The recommended safe range is 20-40 per day with Dripify's standard safety settings. Exceeding LinkedIn's limits increases account restriction risk regardless of which tool you use.
