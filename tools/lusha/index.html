The Bottom Line

Lusha is the right tool for individual reps and small teams who need fast, accurate contact data without the complexity of a full sales platform. The Chrome extension is the best quick-lookup experience available, and direct dial accuracy at 75-80% puts it near the top of the market.

The credit-based pricing model is Lusha's Achilles heel. It works fine when you're revealing 5-15 contacts per day, but the economics fall apart at higher volumes. A 10-person SDR team doing 50+ lookups per rep daily will spend more on Lusha credits than a ZoomInfo contract costs. Apollo's unlimited model makes it the obvious choice for high-volume prospecting.

Buy Lusha if you're a small team (1-5 reps) doing targeted, phone-first outreach. It's fast, accurate, and affordable at low volumes. Switch to Apollo or ZoomInfo when your team grows past 5 reps or your daily contact volume exceeds what the credit model can sustain economically.

Lusha Overview

Lusha built its reputation as the fastest way to grab a phone number or email from LinkedIn. The Chrome extension lets reps pull contact data in two clicks without leaving a prospect's profile. It's simple, it's fast, and for individual contributors who just need numbers, it works.

The company has grown well past that initial use case. Lusha now offers a prospecting platform with company search, list building, bulk enrichment, and CRM integrations. Their database covers 100M+ business profiles with a focus on direct dials and verified emails. They went through a significant rebrand in 2023, positioning themselves as a go-to-market intelligence platform rather than just a Chrome extension.

Pricing keeps Lusha accessible. The free plan gives you 5 credits per month. Pro starts at $29/user/month with 480 credits annually. Premium runs $51/user/month with 960 credits. For individual reps and small teams doing targeted outreach, the per-contact cost is reasonable. Where it breaks down is scale. A 20-person SDR team burning through credits on high-volume prospecting will find Apollo's unlimited model far more economical.

Data accuracy is Lusha's strongest card. Their community-contributed data model, where users share business card data in exchange for credits, creates a feedback loop that keeps contact info fresher than purely scraped databases. Direct dial accuracy sits around 75-80%, competitive with ZoomInfo and well above Apollo. Email accuracy runs 90-93%.

Pros & Cons

  • Fast, frictionless Chrome extensionLusha's Chrome extension is the simplest contact lookup tool available. Two clicks on any LinkedIn profile and you've got a phone number and email. No list building, no filters, no complex workflows. For reps who live in LinkedIn, this speed matters.
  • Strong direct dial accuracyLusha's community-sourced data model produces direct dial accuracy around 75-80%. Users contribute business card data to the network, which keeps phone numbers fresher than databases relying purely on web scraping. For phone-first reps, that 15-20% accuracy edge over Apollo translates to more conversations per hour.
  • Affordable entry point for individualsAt $29/user/month for Pro, Lusha costs less than a team lunch. The free plan with 5 monthly credits lets you test the data quality on your actual prospects before committing. For solo sellers or 2-3 person teams, the total cost stays well under $2,000/year.
  • Clean, focused interfaceLusha doesn't try to be everything. The UI is straightforward: search, find contacts, export. There's no sequencing engine, no workflow builder, no intent data dashboard cluttering the experience. For reps who just need data and already have their outreach tools, that simplicity is a feature.
  • Credit-based pricing gets expensive at scaleLusha charges per contact reveal. Pro gives you 480 credits/year. A single SDR prospecting 50 contacts/day burns through that in 2 weeks. At scale, Apollo's unlimited email model or ZoomInfo's bulk access packages deliver far better per-contact economics.
  • Smaller database than major competitors100M+ profiles is solid but substantially smaller than ZoomInfo (321M+) or Apollo (270M+). You'll hit gaps when prospecting into niche industries, smaller companies, or non-English-speaking markets. The database skews toward tech, finance, and larger companies.
  • Limited platform featuresNo built-in sequencing, no intent data, no conversation intelligence. Lusha is a data provider, not a sales platform. Teams need to pair it with Outreach, Salesloft, or another engagement tool, which adds cost and complexity that Apollo's all-in-one approach eliminates.
  • Prospecting features still maturingLusha's list building and company search tools work, but they lack the filter depth of ZoomInfo or Apollo. Technology install data, hiring signals, and funding filters are limited. Power users building complex prospect lists will feel the constraints quickly.

Use Cases

Individual AE Researching Target Accounts

An account executive at a mid-market SaaS company uses Lusha's Chrome extension to pull direct dials for 10-15 key contacts per day while reviewing LinkedIn profiles. They paste the numbers directly into their CRM and call immediately. The workflow takes 30 seconds per contact compared to 5-10 minutes of manual research. At 5-15 contacts per day, the Pro plan's 480 annual credits last the full year.

Small Recruiting Team Sourcing Candidates

A 3-person recruiting team uses Lusha to find personal email addresses and phone numbers for passive candidates on LinkedIn. Each recruiter reveals 8-10 contacts daily. The team upgraded to Premium for the extra credits and uses Lusha's bulk enrichment to process shortlists of 50+ candidates exported from LinkedIn Recruiter.

Sales Manager Building a Target Account List

A sales manager uses Lusha's prospecting platform to build an initial target account list for a new territory. They filter by industry, company size, and location, then export 200 contacts with emails and direct dials directly into HubSpot. The CRM integration maps fields automatically, saving 3-4 hours of manual data entry.

Pricing Analysis

Lusha's pricing is credit-based and transparent. The free plan includes 5 credits per month. Pro costs $29/user/month (billed annually) and includes 480 credits per year. Premium runs $51/user/month with 960 annual credits. Scale pricing is custom and includes unlimited credits for larger teams.

Each contact reveal costs 1 credit for email and 1 credit for a phone number. Bulk enrichment and API access are available on Premium and Scale plans. The credit model works well for low-volume, targeted outreach but becomes expensive for high-volume prospecting.

One credit consideration that's easy to miss: revealing both email and phone for a single contact costs 2 credits, not 1. Teams doing heavy phone prospecting should track credit consumption closely. If you're regularly running out before renewal, you're better off evaluating Apollo or ZoomInfo's unlimited/bulk pricing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is Lusha's data?

Lusha's email accuracy runs 90-93%, competitive with Apollo and slightly below ZoomInfo. Direct dial phone numbers connect about 75-80% of the time, which is among the best in the market. The accuracy advantage comes from their community-sourced data model, where users contribute business card data to keep contact info current.

Is Lusha free?

Lusha offers a free plan with 5 credits per month. That's enough to test the data quality on a handful of real prospects but won't sustain any kind of regular prospecting cadence. Paid plans start at $29/user/month (Pro) with 480 annual credits.

How does Lusha compare to Apollo?

Lusha is better for individual reps doing low-volume, targeted lookups where phone accuracy matters. Apollo is better for teams doing high-volume outbound because it bundles unlimited email credits, sequencing, and a dialer at $49/month. If you're prospecting more than 20 contacts per day, Apollo's economics are significantly better.

Does Lusha work with Salesforce?

Yes. Lusha integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, and other major CRMs. The integration supports automatic contact creation, field mapping, and duplicate detection. The Salesforce integration is available on Pro plans and above.

Is Lusha GDPR compliant?

Lusha is GDPR compliant and ISO 27701 certified. They maintain opt-out lists and process data deletion requests. Their community-sourced model does raise occasional privacy questions, but the company has invested heavily in compliance infrastructure since 2021.
