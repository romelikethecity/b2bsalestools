What is B2B Contact & Company Data?

B2B Contact & Company Data provide verified contact information, including emails, direct dials, mobile numbers, and job titles, alongside company firmographics like revenue, headcount, industry, and tech stack. These databases are the foundation of outbound sales, giving reps the raw material they need to build targeted prospect lists.

B2B data platforms aggregate professional contact records from public filings, web scraping, data partnerships, and contributor networks. The best providers verify records continuously, flagging job changes, bounced emails, and disconnected numbers. Accuracy varies significantly between vendors, especially for phone numbers and European contacts. Choosing the right provider depends on your target market, budget, and how much you value phone data versus email-only coverage.

How to Choose B2B Data Software

Start with your ICP. If you sell to North American mid-market and enterprise companies, most major providers will have strong coverage. If your targets are SMBs, European companies, or niche verticals like healthcare or manufacturing, test accuracy on a sample of 200-300 target accounts before signing a contract. Coverage gaps in your specific market matter more than total database size.

Phone number accuracy is the biggest differentiator between providers. Email verification rates cluster between 90-97% across top vendors, but direct dial accuracy ranges from 40% to 80%. If your sales motion depends on cold calling, prioritize providers with strong phone data and test connection rates yourself. If you run email-only outbound, you can save significantly by choosing a lower-cost provider with solid email verification.

Watch the contract structure. Enterprise providers like ZoomInfo lock you into annual contracts with auto-renewal clauses and credit-based usage limits. Exceeding your credit allotment mid-year means either paying overage fees or losing access until renewal. Newer platforms like Apollo and Lusha offer monthly plans and pay-as-you-go pricing that reduce financial risk. For teams under 10 reps, monthly pricing almost always makes more sense than an annual commitment you might outgrow or underuse.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is B2B contact data in 2026?

Email accuracy from top providers ranges from 90-97%, with verified emails bouncing less than 5% of the time. Phone number accuracy is lower, typically 40-80% for direct dials depending on the provider. Mobile numbers are the least reliable, connecting 30-60% of the time. Always test on a sample of your target accounts before committing.

What is the difference between B2B data providers and data enrichment tools?

B2B data providers sell access to contact and company databases for prospecting. Data enrichment tools take your existing records and fill in missing fields by querying multiple sources. Some platforms like Apollo and ZoomInfo do both, but dedicated enrichment tools like Clay and Clearbit specialize in augmenting data you already have.

How much do B2B data providers cost?

Pricing ranges from free tiers with limited credits (Apollo, Lusha) to $15,000+/year for enterprise platforms like ZoomInfo. Mid-range options like Cognism and RocketReach run $3,000-$10,000/year depending on seats and credit volume. Most providers use credit-based pricing where each contact lookup costs one credit.

Is it legal to use B2B contact data for cold outreach?

In the US and most of the world, using business contact data for B2B outreach is legal under CAN-SPAM and similar regulations. GDPR in Europe requires a legitimate interest basis and an easy opt-out mechanism. Reputable providers maintain opt-out lists and compliance frameworks, but the responsibility for compliant outreach ultimately falls on your team.

How often does B2B contact data go stale?

The average American worker changes jobs every 2.7 years, so roughly 30% of contact records become outdated annually. Direct dials and mobile numbers change even faster. Top providers re-verify records every 30-90 days, but you should still expect 10-15% of any exported list to contain outdated information. Running lists through an email verification service before outreach reduces bounce rates significantly.
