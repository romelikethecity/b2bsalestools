Expandi vs Dripify

Side-by-side comparison for 2026. Which one is right for your team?

Last updated: 2026-05-06

Expandi vs Dripify

Expandi wins on safety and campaign sophistication. Dripify wins on price and simplicity. Agencies need Expandi; individual reps can start with Dripify.

Expandi and Dripify are LinkedIn automation tools designed to help sales teams, recruiters, and marketers automate connection requests, messages, and profile engagement on LinkedIn. Both tools operate in a gray area of LinkedIn's Terms of Service, which prohibits automated activity. Using either tool carries the risk of account restrictions or bans, though both have implemented safety features to reduce that risk.

Expandi is the more established player, launched in 2019 and widely considered the premium LinkedIn automation tool. It runs as a cloud-based platform with a dedicated IP address per user, mimicking human behavior patterns to avoid detection. Dripify launched later and targets a similar market with a simpler interface and lower price point.

Pricing is straightforward for both. Expandi costs $99/month per LinkedIn account. Dripify offers three tiers: Basic at $59/month, Pro at $79/month, and Advanced at $99/month per account. For a 5-person team, Expandi costs $495/month while Dripify ranges from $295-$495/month depending on the plan. The price difference is significant for budget-conscious teams.

The core value proposition is the same for both tools: automate the repetitive parts of LinkedIn outreach (connection requests, follow-up messages, profile visits, endorsements) while maintaining the appearance of human activity. Both tools support drip campaigns with multiple steps, conditional logic, and delay settings.

Both platforms have evolved in the past year. Expandi has improved its smart sequences, added better analytics, and expanded its integration options. Dripify has added a CRM feature, improved its campaign builder, and enhanced its safety controls. The feature gap between the two tools has narrowed, making pricing and usability the primary differentiators.

A critical note on compliance: LinkedIn actively detects and restricts accounts using automation tools. Both Expandi and Dripify implement safety measures (daily limits, human-like delays, dedicated IPs), but no tool can guarantee your account will not be flagged. Use these tools with a dedicated LinkedIn Sales Navigator account rather than your primary LinkedIn profile, and keep daily activity within conservative limits (20-30 connection requests per day, not the maximum the tool allows).

The regulatory landscape around LinkedIn automation deserves serious attention. LinkedIn has increased enforcement actions against automated accounts, including temporary restrictions, feature limitations, and permanent bans in severe cases. The risk is not theoretical. Sales teams that run aggressive LinkedIn automation campaigns regularly report account restrictions. This risk should be factored into the ROI calculation for either tool.

It is also worth considering the broader trend toward LinkedIn native features. LinkedIn has improved Sales Navigator with better search filters, buyer intent signals, and InMail capabilities. For some teams, the native features plus manual outreach may be more effective and safer than automation tools. The value of automation tools is highest for teams that need to reach 100+ new prospects per week on LinkedIn and cannot justify the manual effort.

Detailed Breakdown

Safety and Account Protection

Expandi is generally regarded as safer due to its dedicated IP per user, country-based IP matching, and more sophisticated human behavior simulation. Expandi randomizes actions, varies timing, and mimics natural browsing patterns more convincingly. Dripify also implements safety measures (daily limits, activity randomization), but user reports suggest slightly higher rates of LinkedIn warnings with Dripify. Neither tool is risk-free. Conservative usage limits (20-30 connection requests/day) are recommended regardless of which tool you choose.

Campaign Builder

Expandi's Smart Sequences offer more advanced conditional logic: if a prospect accepts a connection, send message A; if they do not, send an InMail; if they view your profile but do not connect, endorse them. This branching logic creates more natural-feeling outreach flows. Dripify's campaign builder is simpler with linear sequences and basic if/then conditions. For sophisticated multichannel LinkedIn campaigns, Expandi's builder is more capable. For straightforward connect-and-message sequences, Dripify is sufficient.

Ease of Use

Dripify wins on ease of use. The interface is cleaner, the setup process takes 15-20 minutes, and most users can launch their first campaign within an hour. Expandi's interface is more complex, reflecting the additional features and configuration options. New users typically need 2-3 hours to understand the campaign builder and safety settings. For non-technical users or teams that want minimal setup time, Dripify's simplicity is appealing.

Pricing

Dripify's Basic plan ($59/month) covers core LinkedIn automation needs at 40% less than Expandi ($99/month). The Pro plan ($79/month) adds A/B testing and advanced analytics. The Advanced plan ($99/month) matches Expandi's price but includes additional features like team management. For teams managing 5+ LinkedIn accounts, the cost difference adds up: $295-$395/month for Dripify versus $495/month for Expandi. Expandi justifies the premium with better safety features and more advanced campaign logic.

Analytics and Reporting

Both tools track connection acceptance rates, message response rates, and campaign performance metrics. Expandi's analytics are more detailed, with funnel-style reporting that shows conversion rates at each campaign step. Dripify's analytics cover the essentials but lack the step-by-step funnel visibility. For teams that optimize campaigns based on step-level performance data, Expandi's reporting is more useful. For teams that just want to know how many connections and replies they got, Dripify covers it.

Integration Options

Expandi integrates with more tools: CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), Zapier, webhooks, and email platforms for multichannel outreach. The Zapier integration is particularly valuable for building automated workflows (e.g., when a prospect replies on LinkedIn, create a task in Salesforce). Dripify offers Zapier integration and has added a built-in CRM feature. Both tools cover the basics, but Expandi's integration ecosystem is more mature and flexible.

Multichannel Capabilities

Expandi has a stronger multichannel story, combining LinkedIn automation with email outreach within the same campaign. You can create sequences that alternate between LinkedIn messages and emails based on prospect behavior. Dripify is primarily a LinkedIn-only tool with limited email capabilities. For teams running coordinated LinkedIn plus email outreach, Expandi's multichannel sequences reduce the need for a separate email tool. For LinkedIn-only automation, this is not a differentiating factor.

Team Management

Both tools support multi-account management for teams. Dripify's team management features (available on the Advanced plan) include a shared dashboard, team performance analytics, and account assignment. Expandi offers similar team features with agency management capabilities for teams managing client accounts. For agencies or large teams managing 10+ LinkedIn accounts, both tools handle multi-account management, though Expandi's agency features are more developed.

Onboarding Speed

Dripify is faster to set up and launch. Most users have their first campaign running within 1-2 hours. Expandi requires more initial configuration (IP setup, safety settings, browser profile) and typically takes 3-4 hours before the first campaign launches. The extra setup time for Expandi is an investment in safety and campaign sophistication, not unnecessary complexity. For teams that want to start immediately, Dripify's quick onboarding is an advantage.

The Bottom Line

Expandi is the better LinkedIn automation tool for teams that depend on LinkedIn as a primary outbound channel. The safety features (dedicated IP, behavior simulation, country-based IP matching) provide better account protection. The Smart Sequences enable sophisticated multichannel campaigns. The integrations connect LinkedIn activity to your broader sales workflow. At $99/account/month, the premium over Dripify is justified by reduced account risk and more capable campaign logic.

Dripify is the better choice for teams using LinkedIn as a supplementary outbound channel or operating on a tight budget. The $59/month Basic plan covers core automation needs (connection requests, follow-up messages, basic analytics) at a price that makes LinkedIn outbound accessible. The simpler interface means less setup time and a faster path to value.

For a solo founder or single SDR running LinkedIn outbound, Dripify at $59/month is the pragmatic choice. You need basic automation, not advanced campaign logic. Keep your daily limits conservative and focus your time on writing good connection request messages rather than building complex sequences.

For a 5-10 person SDR team where LinkedIn generates 20%+ of pipeline, Expandi is worth the investment. The campaign sophistication and safety features matter when you are managing multiple accounts and your outbound volume creates more visibility with LinkedIn's detection systems.

For enterprise teams, proceed with caution. Have your compliance team review LinkedIn's Terms of Service and your organization's risk tolerance before deploying any automation tool. If approved, Expandi's team management and safety features are the appropriate choice. Use dedicated SDR accounts only.

The bottom line: Expandi for serious LinkedIn outbound teams willing to pay for safety and sophistication. Dripify for budget-conscious teams that want basic LinkedIn automation without the premium price. Both tools carry account risk, so diversify your outbound channels regardless of which you choose.

A critical perspective that automation tool vendors will not share: the best LinkedIn outbound is still manual. A thoughtful, personalized connection request from a real person who has clearly read the prospect\'s profile outperforms any automated message. Automation tools are for scaling outreach to prospects where that level of personalization is not economically justified. Use automation for volume, not for your most important accounts.

For teams considering LinkedIn automation for the first time, start with Dripify\'s Basic plan ($59/month) on a single dedicated LinkedIn account. Run a 90-day test with conservative limits (15-20 connection requests per day). If the pipeline generated exceeds the cost, scale to additional accounts. If not, the $177 investment was a cheap learning experiment. Do not commit to annual contracts with either vendor until you have proven the channel works for your specific ICP.

Which Is Right for Your Stage?

Startups & SMBs

Start with Dripify at $59/month per account. At this stage, you need basic LinkedIn automation: send connection requests, follow up with messages, and track acceptance rates. Dripify's simplicity and lower cost are ideal for founder-led sales or a single SDR running LinkedIn outbound. Keep daily connection requests under 25 and use a LinkedIn Sales Navigator account (not your personal profile). Save the premium for Expandi if LinkedIn becomes your primary outbound channel and you need more sophisticated campaign logic. One practical alternative to both tools at the startup stage: use LinkedIn Sales Navigator\'s native features combined with manual outreach. Spend 30-45 minutes per day sending 15-20 personalized connection requests and follow-up messages. At this volume, the automation benefit is marginal, and the account risk is zero. Once LinkedIn becomes a proven pipeline source and you need to scale beyond what manual effort supports (typically 50+ connection requests per day), then evaluate Dripify or Expandi.

Growth Stage

If LinkedIn generates meaningful pipeline for your team, upgrade to Expandi for the safety features and Smart Sequences. At $99/account/month for a 5-10 person team ($495-$990/month), the investment is justified by better account protection and more sophisticated campaign flows. Build multichannel campaigns that combine LinkedIn touches with email follow-ups. Use Expandi's Zapier integration to push LinkedIn engagement data to your CRM. If LinkedIn is a secondary channel and email is primary, Dripify at $59-$79/account/month remains the cost-effective choice. At the growth stage, implement a clear account tiering strategy for LinkedIn automation. Tier 1 accounts (your most important prospects) should receive manual, thoughtful LinkedIn outreach from your best reps. Tier 2-3 accounts can be handled by automation tools with well-crafted templates. This tiering protects your brand on the accounts that matter most while still scaling outreach volume. Both Expandi and Dripify support this model, but Expandi\'s campaign segmentation makes it easier to manage different outreach strategies simultaneously.

Enterprise

At enterprise scale, evaluate whether LinkedIn automation aligns with your compliance and brand risk policies. Many enterprise organizations prohibit LinkedIn automation due to Terms of Service concerns. If your compliance team approves it, Expandi's dedicated IPs, safety features, and team management tools are the appropriate choice. Budget $99/account/month and establish strict usage policies: conservative daily limits, approved messaging templates, and regular account health monitoring. Consider using these tools only for SDR accounts, not for senior executives whose LinkedIn presence is too valuable to risk. Enterprise compliance teams often flag LinkedIn automation as a vendor risk issue. Prepare a risk assessment document before the compliance review that addresses: LinkedIn\'s Terms of Service position, the safety measures implemented by the tool, account risk mitigation strategies (dedicated accounts, conservative limits), and the business case for automation versus manual outreach. Having this document ready can shorten the compliance review from weeks to days. If compliance rejects automation tools entirely, invest in LinkedIn Sales Navigator\'s premium features as the alternative.

Questions to Ask Before Choosing

  1. Is LinkedIn a primary or secondary outbound channel for our sales team?
  2. How many LinkedIn accounts will we be automating?
  3. Has our compliance team reviewed and approved the use of LinkedIn automation tools?
  4. Do we need multichannel campaigns (LinkedIn plus email) in a single tool?
  5. What is our risk tolerance for LinkedIn account restrictions?
  6. Are we using LinkedIn Sales Navigator, and do we have dedicated accounts for outbound?
  7. How important is conditional campaign logic versus simple linear sequences?
  8. Do we need Zapier or CRM integrations to connect LinkedIn activity to our sales workflow?
  9. What is our budget per LinkedIn account per month for automation?
  10. Are we managing client accounts (agency model) that require white-label or multi-account management?

Frequently Asked Questions

Will LinkedIn ban my account for using these tools?

There is a risk with any LinkedIn automation tool. LinkedIn's Terms of Service prohibit automated activity, and their detection systems are improving. Both Expandi and Dripify implement safety measures to reduce this risk, but no tool can guarantee account safety. Best practices: use a dedicated LinkedIn account (not your primary profile), keep daily connection requests under 25-30, vary your activity patterns, and stop automation immediately if you receive a LinkedIn warning. Expandi's dedicated IP and behavior simulation offer marginally better protection.

Is Expandi worth the premium over Dripify?

For teams where LinkedIn is a primary outbound channel generating significant pipeline, yes. The better safety features, advanced campaign logic, and multichannel capabilities justify the $20-$40/month premium per account. For teams using LinkedIn as a supplementary channel alongside email, Dripify's lower cost and simpler interface deliver sufficient value. The decision comes down to how strategically important LinkedIn is to your outbound motion.

Can I automate LinkedIn InMails with these tools?

Both tools support InMail automation for LinkedIn Sales Navigator users. InMails bypass the connection request requirement and go directly to the prospect's inbox. The response rate for automated InMails is typically lower than for connection-request-based outreach because InMails lack the social proof of a shared connection. Use InMails as a fallback step in campaigns when connection requests go unanswered, not as the primary outreach method.

How many connection requests can I safely send per day?

LinkedIn's official weekly limit for connection requests is approximately 100 per week (varies by account standing). Most safety experts recommend 20-30 connection requests per day as a conservative target. Both Expandi and Dripify allow you to set daily limits. Exceeding 40+ requests per day significantly increases the risk of account restrictions. Start at 15-20 per day for new accounts and gradually increase based on acceptance rates and account health indicators.

Do these tools work with LinkedIn Sales Navigator?

Yes, both tools integrate with LinkedIn Sales Navigator. Sales Navigator is recommended (and arguably required) for effective LinkedIn automation. It provides advanced search filters, higher InMail allocations, and lead list management. The combination of Sales Navigator ($99-$149/month) plus an automation tool ($59-$99/month) is the standard setup for LinkedIn-based outbound. Using automation tools without Sales Navigator limits your targeting precision significantly.

Can I use these tools for recruiting, not just sales?

Both Expandi and Dripify are popular with recruiters for automating outreach to candidates. The workflow is similar: search for candidates matching specific criteria, send connection requests with personalized notes, and follow up with messages. Expandi's Smart Sequences work well for recruiting workflows where different follow-up paths are needed based on candidate response. Dripify's simpler sequences cover basic recruiting outreach needs. The safety considerations are the same for recruiting as for sales.

How do these compare to LinkedIn's native Sales Navigator features?

Sales Navigator provides search filters, lead lists, and InMail but does not automate connection requests, follow-up messages, or drip sequences. Expandi and Dripify automate the execution layer that Sales Navigator lacks. They are complementary, not competing. Most users need Sales Navigator for targeting and one of these tools for automated execution. LinkedIn is unlikely to build native automation features that violate its own Terms of Service, so third-party tools fill this gap.

What happens to my campaigns if LinkedIn changes its detection algorithms?

Both Expandi and Dripify update their safety measures in response to LinkedIn's algorithm changes. Expandi has historically been faster to adapt, likely due to a larger engineering team focused on the product. If LinkedIn significantly tightens detection, some campaigns may need to pause while the tool updates. This is a structural risk of LinkedIn automation. Maintain a diversified outbound strategy (email, phone, LinkedIn) so that LinkedIn automation disruptions do not shut down your entire pipeline.

How do I create a dedicated LinkedIn account for automation?

Create a new LinkedIn profile with a real team member\'s name and photo (with their permission). Complete the profile thoroughly: headline, summary, experience, education, and a professional photo. Connect with 200-300 colleagues and industry contacts to build the network organically before starting automation. Wait 2-4 weeks after creating the account before connecting any automation tool. LinkedIn flags new accounts running automation more aggressively than established accounts. The warmup period is essential for account safety.

Can I use both Expandi and Dripify on different LinkedIn accounts?

Yes, some teams use Expandi for their primary SDR accounts (better safety) and Dripify for secondary or test accounts (lower cost). Each tool connects to specific LinkedIn accounts independently. The main consideration is management overhead: running two automation platforms means two dashboards, two sets of campaign data, and two subscription costs. Unless you have a specific reason to split (e.g., testing one tool against the other), standardizing on a single platform is simpler.

What is the difference between LinkedIn automation and LinkedIn ads for prospecting?

LinkedIn automation sends connection requests and direct messages to specific individuals. LinkedIn ads display sponsored content to targeted audiences in their feed. Automation is direct outreach with higher engagement per contact but manual effort and account risk. Ads are scalable brand awareness with lower engagement per impression but no account risk. For direct pipeline generation, automation typically produces faster results. For brand building and long-term demand generation, LinkedIn ads are the appropriate channel. Many teams run both simultaneously for different objectives.
