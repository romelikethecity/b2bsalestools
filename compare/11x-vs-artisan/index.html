11x vs Artisan

Side-by-side comparison for 2026. Which one is right for your team?

Last updated: 2026-05-06

11x vs Artisan

11x wins on AI autonomy. Artisan wins on pricing and their built-in contact database. Both are early-stage bets. Neither has proven at scale.

11x and Artisan represent the newest category in sales tech: AI SDRs. Both companies promise to automate outbound prospecting using AI agents that research prospects, write personalized emails, and manage follow-up sequences with minimal human oversight. The pitch is compelling: replace or augment your SDR team with AI that works 24/7 at a fraction of the cost.

The reality is more nuanced. Both products are early-stage, with capabilities that are impressive in demos but inconsistent in production. The AI-generated emails are better than generic templates but rarely match a skilled human SDR writing for high-value prospects. The research quality varies depending on data availability for each prospect. And the autonomy comes with risk: AI agents can send embarrassing or off-target messages if guardrails are not properly configured.

11x positions its AI SDR (named Alice) as an enterprise-grade solution that integrates into existing sales workflows. The company has raised significant funding and targets mid-market to enterprise companies with established sales teams. Artisan positions its AI SDR (named Ava) as a more accessible, plug-and-play option for teams of all sizes. Their go-to-market has been more aggressive on social media and targets earlier-stage companies.

Pricing for both tools is opaque and typically requires a demo call. Based on available information, 11x pricing starts around $5,000-$10,000/month for their base package. Artisan pricing starts lower, around $2,000-$5,000/month, though both vendors adjust pricing based on volume and use case. For context, a human SDR in the US costs $5,000-$8,000/month fully loaded (salary, benefits, tools, management overhead).

The honest assessment: both products show what AI SDRs will become, but neither is a reliable replacement for a competent human SDR team today. They are best deployed as supplements that handle initial outreach on lower-priority accounts, freeing human SDRs to focus on strategic, high-value prospects.

This market is moving fast. Features and capabilities change monthly. What is written here reflects the state of both products as of early 2026. Check recent user reviews and request a current demo before making any purchasing decision.

The AI SDR category is attracting significant venture capital, which means both companies are growing fast and burning cash. This creates an important consideration for buyers: will these companies be around in 3 years? 11x has raised $50M+ in funding with a valuation that assumes significant future growth. Artisan has also raised meaningful capital. Both companies need to grow into their valuations, which means aggressive sales tactics and feature promises that may outpace delivery.

Buyers should also consider the talent implications. If AI SDRs become highly effective, the demand for human SDRs will shift. But today, the most successful implementations treat AI SDRs as tools that make human SDRs more productive, not replacements. The organizations getting the best results pair AI SDRs with strong human oversight and clear rules about which accounts get human versus AI treatment.

Detailed Breakdown

Email Quality

Both AI SDRs generate emails that are better than generic mass templates but fall short of what a skilled human SDR can write for strategic accounts. 11x's Alice tends to produce more structured, professional emails that read well but can feel formulaic. Artisan's Ava leans toward a more conversational tone. In testing, reply rates from AI-generated emails typically land 30-50% lower than well-crafted human emails for the same prospect list. The gap narrows significantly on high-volume, lower-ACV outreach where personalization depth matters less.

Research Quality

The quality of AI-generated research depends heavily on the data available for each prospect. For C-suite executives at well-known companies with active LinkedIn profiles and press coverage, both tools produce solid research summaries. For mid-level contacts at smaller companies with limited online presence, the research is thin and sometimes inaccurate. 11x generally produces more detailed research briefs, likely due to more data source integrations. Artisan's research is faster but shallower.

Autonomy and Guardrails

Both tools offer configurable autonomy levels. You can set the AI to draft emails for human review before sending, or let it send autonomously within defined parameters. The risk with full autonomy is real: AI agents occasionally reference incorrect company information, misattribute roles, or produce tone-deaf messages. 11x has more granular guardrail settings and approval workflows. Artisan offers simpler controls that work for smaller teams but lack the compliance features larger organizations need.

Pricing

11x pricing starts around $5,000-$10,000/month for base packages, with enterprise pricing going higher based on volume and features. Artisan starts around $2,000-$5,000/month. Both are significantly cheaper than a human SDR ($5,000-$8,000/month fully loaded), but the comparison is misleading because neither tool fully replaces a human SDR. The fairer comparison is cost per qualified meeting generated. Early data suggests AI SDRs generate meetings at $300-$800 each, while human SDRs average $200-$500 per meeting. The cost advantage is not as clear as the marketing suggests.

Integration Depth

11x has invested more in enterprise integrations, with connectors for Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, and Slack. The data flows bi-directionally, meaning CRM updates can trigger AI actions and AI-generated activities log back to the CRM automatically. Artisan's integrations are growing but more limited, with basic CRM sync and email platform connections. For teams that need the AI SDR to fit into an existing multi-tool sales stack, 11x's integration layer is more mature.

Ease of Setup

Artisan is faster to get running. The onboarding process takes 1-2 weeks, and the interface is designed for non-technical users. 11x requires more setup time (2-4 weeks), including detailed configuration of research parameters, email templates, approval workflows, and CRM mappings. The tradeoff is that 11x's more complex setup produces more tailored output. Artisan's simpler setup means faster time-to-value but potentially less precise targeting.

Scalability

11x is designed for mid-market and enterprise teams that need to scale AI outreach across multiple products, segments, and territories. The platform supports multiple AI agents running different campaigns simultaneously with centralized management. Artisan works well for smaller teams and single-product companies but may require workarounds for complex, multi-segment outbound strategies. If you plan to scale AI-assisted outbound across a large sales org, 11x's architecture is better prepared.

Customer Support

Both companies are early-stage and offer high-touch support. 11x provides dedicated customer success managers for enterprise accounts and regular strategy sessions. Artisan offers responsive support through chat and email. The quality of support at early-stage companies can fluctuate as they scale. Both vendors are generally responsive, but expect the support experience to evolve as the companies grow and their customer bases expand.

Data Security

11x has invested more in enterprise security certifications, including SOC 2 Type II compliance. Their data handling policies are designed for organizations with strict security requirements. Artisan is working toward similar certifications but may not yet meet the security standards required by larger enterprises. If your organization has strict vendor security review processes, verify current certifications before proceeding with either vendor.

The Bottom Line

Neither 11x nor Artisan is a mature, reliable replacement for human SDRs today. Both tools show impressive potential and can generate meetings, but the quality and consistency are not yet at the level where you can fully replace headcount.

That said, both tools are useful as supplements to an existing SDR team. The best deployment model is tiered: human SDRs handle Tier 1 strategic accounts where message quality and relationship building matter. AI SDRs handle Tier 2-3 accounts where volume and coverage matter more than personalization depth.

11x is the better choice for mid-market and enterprise teams. The integration depth, security certifications, guardrail controls, and multi-agent architecture are designed for organizations with 10+ SDRs and complex sales motions. Budget $5,000-$10,000/month and treat it as a 6-month pilot with clear success metrics.

Artisan is the better choice for smaller teams and startups. The lower price point, faster setup, and simpler interface make it accessible for companies that want to experiment with AI-assisted outbound without a large commitment. Budget $2,000-$5,000/month and start with a limited campaign on one account segment.

If you are a 5-person startup SDR team, be honest about what an AI SDR can do for you. At this stage, the $2,000-$5,000/month is likely better spent on a tool like Instantly ($80/month) plus better data ($200-$500/month) and investing the difference in SDR training.

The bottom line: AI SDRs are a real category with real value, but the marketing outpaces the reality. Start small, measure carefully, and scale based on data, not demos.

The honest recommendation is this: watch this category closely, but invest cautiously. AI SDR technology will improve significantly over the next 12-24 months. Early adopters will have a learning advantage, but they will also absorb the costs of immature technology. A small pilot ($2,000-$5,000/month) gives you exposure to the category without bet-the-farm risk.

Also evaluate the competitive alternatives. Some teams achieve AI SDR-like results by combining ChatGPT (for research and writing), Clay (for data enrichment), and Instantly (for sending) at a fraction of the cost. The workflow is more manual, but the control over messaging is greater. This DIY approach works particularly well for teams with an ops-minded person who enjoys building automations.

Which Is Right for Your Stage?

Startups & SMBs

Be cautious with AI SDRs at the startup stage. If you have fewer than 5 reps, you need human SDRs who can adapt messaging in real time based on prospect feedback, iterate on ICP definitions, and handle the ambiguity of early-stage outbound. An AI SDR cannot tell you that your value proposition is wrong. A human SDR can. If budget is extremely tight, Artisan's lower price point ($2,000-$5,000/month) could supplement a 1-2 person SDR team by handling initial outreach on lower-priority account tiers. Do not rely on it as your sole outbound channel. If you are a solo founder or very early-stage team, consider this alternative approach before investing in an AI SDR: use ChatGPT to help write email copy, Clay or Apollo for prospect research, and Instantly for automated sending. This DIY stack costs under $200/month and gives you more control over messaging than a black-box AI SDR. The output quality can be comparable, and you build institutional knowledge about what messaging works that an AI SDR obscures.

Growth Stage

This is where AI SDRs start making sense as a supplement. If you have 10-20 human SDRs and want to expand outbound coverage without hiring more headcount, an AI SDR can handle Tier 2 and Tier 3 accounts while your human team focuses on strategic prospects. 11x is the better fit at this stage because the integration depth and guardrail controls matter more with a larger team. Budget $5,000-$10,000/month for 11x and set up a structured A/B test comparing AI-generated outreach against human SDR outreach on comparable prospect segments. At the growth stage, the right deployment model is a structured test. Take 1,000 accounts from your Tier 2-3 segments and split them: 500 go to the AI SDR, 500 go to your human SDRs using the same messaging framework. Run the test for 90 days and compare cost per meeting, meeting quality (conversion to opportunity), and pipeline generated. This data-driven approach eliminates the guesswork and gives you a clear decision framework for scaling AI SDR usage.

Enterprise

Enterprise adoption of AI SDRs is still experimental. The best use case is augmenting your existing SDR team with AI-handled outreach on lower-priority segments, geographic territories without dedicated headcount, or re-engagement campaigns for dormant prospects. 11x's enterprise features (SOC 2, advanced integrations, multi-agent management) make it the only viable option at this scale. Budget $10,000-$25,000/month and treat it as a pilot program with clear success metrics. Do not commit to replacing headcount until you have 6+ months of performance data. For enterprise teams considering AI SDRs, the legal and compliance review may be the longest part of the evaluation. Questions to resolve: who owns the AI-generated content? What happens if the AI sends a message with incorrect claims about your product? How does AI outreach comply with your industry\'s communication regulations? Legal review for AI SDR tools typically takes 4-8 weeks for enterprise organizations. Start the legal process in parallel with the product evaluation to avoid timeline delays.

Questions to Ask Before Choosing

  1. What percentage of our outbound pipeline comes from accounts that an AI SDR can effectively target?
  2. Do we have human SDRs who can handle the high-value accounts that AI cannot?
  3. What is our current cost per qualified meeting, and what would the AI SDR need to achieve to justify the investment?
  4. How important is message quality versus volume for our outbound strategy?
  5. Do we have the internal resources to review AI-generated messages before they send, or do we need full autonomy?
  6. What is our security review process for new vendors, and do either tool's certifications meet our requirements?
  7. How complex is our product offering, and can an AI agent accurately represent it in outbound messaging?
  8. Are we comfortable with the reputational risk of AI-generated emails occasionally missing the mark?
  9. What CRM and sales tool integrations are required for the AI SDR to fit into our existing workflow?
  10. Do we have a clear Tier 1/Tier 2/Tier 3 account segmentation that would let us deploy AI SDRs on appropriate accounts?

Frequently Asked Questions

Can an AI SDR actually replace a human SDR?

Not reliably, at least not in 2026. AI SDRs can handle initial outreach, follow-up sequences, and basic personalization at scale. They cannot read between the lines in a prospect's response, pivot messaging based on competitive intelligence gathered in a conversation, or build the rapport that converts a cold lead into a warm opportunity. The best deployment model is AI SDR for initial outreach on Tier 2-3 accounts, with human SDRs handling strategic accounts and all live conversations.

How do reply rates compare between AI SDRs and human SDRs?

In head-to-head tests, human SDRs consistently outperform AI SDRs on reply rates by 30-50% for the same prospect lists. The gap is largest for enterprise prospects (C-suite at Fortune 1000) and smallest for SMB prospects where a well-structured template is sufficient. AI SDRs compensate with higher volume: while a human SDR might send 100 personalized emails per week, an AI SDR sends 500-1,000. The net meeting output can be comparable when volume compensates for lower conversion.

What happens when an AI SDR sends a bad email?

It happens. AI SDRs occasionally reference incorrect company information, cite outdated job titles, or produce messages with awkward phrasing. The brand risk is real, especially for enterprise-focused companies where a poorly written email to a VP can damage the relationship permanently. Mitigate this by starting with human-in-the-loop approval for the first 4-8 weeks, then gradually increasing autonomy as you calibrate the guardrails. Both 11x and Artisan allow you to set up approval workflows for specific account tiers.

Is 11x worth the premium over Artisan?

For mid-market and enterprise teams, yes. The deeper integrations, SOC 2 compliance, multi-agent management, and granular guardrails justify the price difference. For smaller teams (under 10 reps) or companies with less complex security and integration requirements, Artisan delivers sufficient functionality at a lower cost. The decision parallels the broader build-vs-buy tradeoff: 11x is the enterprise option, Artisan is the SMB option.

How long does it take to see results from an AI SDR?

Expect 4-8 weeks before meaningful results. The first 2 weeks are setup and configuration. Weeks 3-4 are initial sends with higher human oversight. Weeks 5-8 are optimization based on initial reply data. Most teams see stable, predictable meeting generation by month 3. If you are not seeing results by month 4, the issue is likely targeting or messaging, not the tool itself.

Should I wait for AI SDR technology to mature before investing?

The technology is improving rapidly, but waiting has its own cost. Teams that start experimenting now will have 6-12 months of data, optimized configurations, and institutional knowledge about what works when the technology reaches its next level. Starting with a small pilot ($2,000-$5,000/month on Artisan or a limited 11x deployment) lets you build this capability without a large commitment. The worst approach is waiting until AI SDRs are perfect, then scrambling to implement while competitors are already optimized.

Do AI SDRs work for non-English outreach?

Both tools support multiple languages, but quality varies. English-language email generation is the most polished. German, French, and Spanish are functional but noticeably less natural. Other languages are hit-or-miss. If your outbound includes significant non-English outreach, test the specific languages you need during the evaluation process. Do not assume multilingual support means equal quality across languages.

How do these compare to using ChatGPT for email writing?

ChatGPT (or other general-purpose LLMs) can write individual emails, but it lacks the prospecting infrastructure: automated research, sequence management, deliverability tools, CRM integration, and follow-up automation. You can build a DIY AI SDR using ChatGPT plus a tool like Instantly for sending, but the workflow is manual and breaks at scale. 11x and Artisan package the AI writing with the infrastructure needed to actually run outbound campaigns end-to-end.

How do AI SDRs handle objections in email replies?

Both 11x and Artisan can generate follow-up responses to common objections (not interested, bad timing, already have a solution). The quality varies: straightforward objections get reasonable responses, but nuanced replies or complex questions often trigger a handoff to a human rep. Neither tool handles live objection handling in calls or chat. The safest approach is to configure the AI to hand off all substantive replies to a human SDR for response, using the AI only for initial outreach and follow-up sequences.

What data sources do these AI SDRs use for prospect research?

Both tools scrape publicly available information: LinkedIn profiles, company websites, press releases, job postings, funding announcements, and social media. 11x integrates with additional data providers for firmographic and technographic data. Artisan relies more on publicly available sources. The research quality directly correlates with how much public information exists about each prospect. For well-known companies and senior executives, the research is solid. For smaller companies or mid-level contacts, expect thinner personalization.

Can I use an AI SDR for inbound lead follow-up, not just outbound?

Both tools are primarily designed for outbound prospecting, but some teams use them for fast inbound lead follow-up. The AI can send a personalized response within minutes of a form submission, including relevant information based on the lead\'s company and role. This use case has lower risk because inbound leads have already expressed interest, making the AI\'s message more likely to be well-received. If inbound lead response time is a problem (the industry average is over 5 hours), an AI SDR can provide sub-5-minute response times.
