Clari vs Gong Forecast

Side-by-side comparison for 2026. Which one is right for your team?

Last updated: 2026-04-23

Clari vs Gong Forecast

Clari wins on pure forecasting depth. Gong wins when you want forecasting informed by actual conversation data. If forecasting accuracy is job #1, pick Clari.

Clari and Gong both promise to improve revenue outcomes, but they attack the problem from different angles. Gong is a conversation intelligence platform that records, transcribes, and analyzes sales calls to extract coaching insights and deal signals. Clari is a revenue operations platform that aggregates pipeline data across your CRM, email, calendar, and calls to forecast revenue and flag deal risk.

The confusion between these two tools exists because their feature sets have expanded toward each other. Gong now offers deal boards and forecasting. Clari now offers conversation intelligence. But the core DNA of each product still reflects its origin, and that matters when you are choosing where to invest.

Gong's strength is in the conversation layer. The AI analysis of sales calls is the most mature in the market, with accurate transcription, topic tracking, talk-to-listen ratios, and competitor mention detection. Sales managers use it daily to coach reps and understand what is actually happening on calls.

Clari's strength is in the revenue layer. It pulls data from your CRM, email engagement, meeting activity, and pipeline changes to create a unified view of deal health and revenue forecast accuracy. RevOps leaders and CROs use it to run forecast calls and hold teams accountable to pipeline targets.

Pricing reflects the different value propositions. Gong typically runs $100-$150/user/month for conversation intelligence, with add-ons for forecasting and deal management. Clari pricing is custom and typically structured around the number of revenue team members, landing in the $80-$120/user/month range for the core platform.

The market has shifted in the last year. Gong's expansion into deal management and forecasting has been aggressive, with new features for Gong Forecast and Deal Board. Clari has deepened its analytics capabilities and added more AI-powered insights. Both vendors are positioning as complete revenue platforms, but the execution quality still skews toward their respective strengths.

One factor that often gets overlooked is organizational alignment. Gong is a tool that frontline sales managers champion because it directly helps them coach reps. Clari is a tool that CROs and VP Finance champion because it directly helps them forecast accurately. The internal champion determines adoption success. If you buy Gong without manager buy-in, calls go unreviewed. If you buy Clari without executive sponsorship, forecast submissions become a checkbox exercise.

The integration story also matters. Both tools play well with Salesforce, but Gong\'s ecosystem of integrations (Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, Dialpad, RingCentral) is broader on the communication platform side. Clari\'s integration strength is on the data aggregation side, pulling from CRM, email, calendar, and marketing automation platforms. Your existing tech stack should influence which tool integrates more naturally.

One more practical consideration: the procurement process differs. Gong typically requires a 12-month commitment with pricing that scales by user count and features. Clari\'s contracts are similarly annual but structured around revenue team size rather than individual user seats. Both vendors offer pilot programs, but Gong\'s pilot is easier to execute because you can deploy to a single team and see results quickly. Clari\'s pilot needs a full forecasting hierarchy to generate meaningful data, which makes the evaluation period longer and more involved. Plan for 30-60 days with Gong and 60-90 days with Clari to run a meaningful evaluation.

Detailed Breakdown

Conversation Intelligence

Gong is the category leader in conversation intelligence, and the gap is significant. Transcription accuracy, speaker identification, topic detection, and coaching scorecards are all best-in-class. Clari acquired conversation intelligence capabilities but they remain secondary to the core platform. If your primary goal is improving how reps sell on calls, Gong is the clear winner. Clari's conversation features are functional but feel like an add-on rather than a core product.

Revenue Forecasting

Clari was built around forecasting accuracy, and it shows. The platform aggregates signals from CRM fields, email engagement, meeting frequency, and pipeline movement to generate AI-driven forecasts that consistently beat CRM-based forecasts by 15-25% in accuracy. Gong's forecasting module is newer and relies more heavily on conversation signals. For CROs who live and die by forecast accuracy, Clari has the deeper product.

Deal Visibility

Both tools offer deal boards, but the data sources differ. Gong's deal insights are powered by what happens on calls: stakeholder mentions, competitor references, next step commitments, and sentiment analysis. Clari's deal insights come from engagement patterns: email response rates, meeting cadence, CRM field changes, and historical win/loss patterns. The most complete picture comes from combining both data sources, which is why some larger orgs run both tools.

Ease of Use

Gong has a more intuitive interface for frontline managers and reps. The call library, snippets, and coaching tools are easy to navigate without training. Clari's interface is more dashboard-heavy and designed for RevOps and leadership. Reps interact with Clari primarily through CRM integrations and weekly forecast submissions rather than the platform itself. If you want a tool reps will open daily, Gong has the edge. If you want a tool leadership will open daily, that is Clari.

Pricing

Gong pricing starts around $100-$150/user/month for conversation intelligence, with additional costs for Gong Forecast and Engage modules. Full-platform Gong deployments for a 100-rep team can reach $180K-$250K annually. Clari pricing is custom-quoted and structured around team size, typically landing between $80-$120/user/month. A 100-rep Clari deployment runs $120K-$200K annually. Both vendors offer discounts on multi-year commitments and are willing to negotiate at scale.

CRM Integration

Both tools integrate deeply with Salesforce, and both offer HubSpot integrations. Clari's CRM integration is more comprehensive because the platform depends on CRM data as a primary input. Clari reads and writes forecast data, pipeline changes, and activity metrics directly to Salesforce fields. Gong's CRM integration focuses on logging call data, surfacing insights on deal records, and syncing contact information. If CRM data quality is a priority, Clari's bi-directional sync is more impactful.

Onboarding Speed

Gong can deliver value within days of connecting your phone system or video platform. The call recording starts immediately, and insights begin populating after 10-15 recorded calls. Clari requires more upfront configuration: mapping your pipeline stages, defining forecast categories, setting up team hierarchies, and calibrating the AI model against your historical data. Plan 4-8 weeks before Clari's forecasts become reliable. Gong's faster time-to-value is a real advantage for teams that want immediate impact.

AI Features

Both platforms are investing heavily in AI, but in different directions. Gong's AI focuses on conversation analysis: summarizing calls, identifying action items, flagging risk signals in buyer language, and generating coaching suggestions. Clari's AI focuses on predictive analytics: forecasting deal outcomes, identifying pipeline gaps, and recommending actions to close revenue gaps. Gong's AI is more visible to reps. Clari's AI is more valuable to leadership.

Data Security

Call recording introduces data privacy considerations that pipeline analytics do not. Gong records and stores audio and video files, which triggers compliance requirements around consent, data residency, and retention policies. Both tools are SOC 2 Type II certified, but Gong requires more careful configuration around recording consent (especially in two-party consent states and GDPR jurisdictions). Clari's data footprint is lighter because it primarily processes metadata and CRM fields rather than raw audio.

The Bottom Line

Gong and Clari are not direct competitors, despite the overlapping feature sets. Gong is a conversation intelligence platform that has added forecasting. Clari is a revenue forecasting platform that has added conversation intelligence. The core product quality still reflects those origins.

If your primary goal is improving how reps sell on calls, coaching new hires faster, and understanding what top performers do differently, Gong is the right choice. The conversation analytics are best-in-class, the interface is intuitive, and the time-to-value is measured in days, not months.

If your primary goal is improving forecast accuracy, catching at-risk deals earlier, and giving your CRO confidence in the revenue number, Clari is the right choice. The forecasting engine is the most accurate in the market, and the pipeline analytics drive real operational improvements.

For a 10-person startup, choose Gong. You need rep coaching and call recording more than you need AI-powered forecasting. Your pipeline is small enough to forecast in a spreadsheet.

For a 50-person growth-stage team, choose based on your biggest pain point. If your CRO is frustrated by forecast misses, start with Clari. If your VP of Sales is frustrated by inconsistent rep performance, start with Gong. Plan to add the second tool within 12-18 months.

For a 200+ person enterprise team, run both. The $400K-$600K annual investment pays for itself if it prevents one bad forecast quarter or improves win rates by even 3-5%. The tools complement each other, and the data from Gong's conversation analysis makes Clari's forecasting even more accurate when both are feeding into your CRM.

For teams evaluating alternatives, consider Chorus (ZoomInfo) as a Gong alternative and Aviso or BoostUp as Clari alternatives. None match the category leaders on product quality, but they can be 20-40% cheaper and may offer bundled pricing with other tools in their ecosystem.

The organizational change management aspect deserves attention. Gong requires sales managers to actually review calls and deliver coaching, which demands behavioral change. Clari requires sales leaders to trust AI-driven forecasts over gut instinct, which demands cultural change. Both tools fail when the organizational readiness is not there. Before buying either tool, honestly assess whether your leadership team will commit to using it consistently. A $200K tool that gets used once a week is worse than a free tool used daily.

For teams that currently have neither tool, here is a practical starting framework: if your sales managers spend more than 3 hours per week listening to recorded calls and coaching reps, Gong automates and amplifies that workflow immediately. If your CRO spends more than 2 hours per week in spreadsheets trying to build an accurate forecast, Clari eliminates that manual effort. Lead with whatever pain point costs the most time and attention today.

Which Is Right for Your Stage?

Startups & SMBs

If you have fewer than 10 reps, start with Gong. The conversation intelligence will immediately improve call quality and shorten your ramp time for new hires. At this stage, your pipeline is small enough to forecast manually or with simple CRM reports. You do not need Clari's forecasting engine until you have enough pipeline volume to make forecasting statistically meaningful. Budget $100-$150/user/month for Gong and plan to add forecasting tools later when you cross 30+ reps. At this stage, you can also consider Chorus (now part of ZoomInfo) as a Gong alternative at a lower price point. The conversation intelligence is not as polished as Gong\'s, but it costs 20-30% less and is bundled with ZoomInfo data. For budget-conscious startups already using ZoomInfo, this combination can be more cost-effective than Gong standalone.

Growth Stage

This is where the decision gets interesting. If your biggest problem is forecast accuracy and pipeline visibility, choose Clari. If your biggest problem is rep performance and call quality, choose Gong. Many growth-stage companies (50-150 reps) start with one and add the other within 12-18 months. If forced to choose one, pick based on your most urgent pain point. Budget $100-$150/user/month for either platform, and negotiate for a pilot period with 20-30 users before committing to a full rollout. One effective deployment strategy at this stage: start with Gong for the entire team, then add Clari for leadership and RevOps only (not per-rep licensing). This gives you conversation intelligence across the org and forecasting tools where they matter most, at a lower total cost than full-org deployment of both tools. Budget $120K-$180K annually for this hybrid approach with a 75-person team.

Enterprise

Most enterprise revenue teams (200+ reps) benefit from running both Clari and Gong. Gong handles conversation intelligence and rep coaching at the frontline manager level. Clari handles forecasting, pipeline analytics, and executive reporting at the CRO level. The combined cost for a 200-rep team runs $400K-$600K annually, which is significant but justified if forecast accuracy improvements prevent even one bad quarter. If budget forces a single choice, Clari is typically the priority for enterprise teams because forecast accuracy has higher executive visibility. At enterprise scale, insist on a joint integration plan with both vendors. Gong and Clari can feed data into each other through your CRM, creating a feedback loop where conversation signals improve forecast accuracy. The implementation should be sequenced: deploy Gong first (faster time-to-value), then deploy Clari once you have 3-4 months of conversation data enriching your CRM records. This sequencing gives Clari better input data from day one.

Questions to Ask Before Choosing

  1. Is our primary pain point forecast accuracy or rep coaching quality?
  2. How many recorded calls per week do we generate across the sales team?
  3. Who will be the primary daily user of this tool: frontline managers, RevOps, or the CRO?
  4. What is our current forecast accuracy, and what would a 15% improvement be worth in planning confidence?
  5. Do we have two-party consent requirements that complicate call recording?
  6. Are we willing to run both tools eventually, or do we need a single platform?
  7. How integrated does this tool need to be with our CRM for data writeback?
  8. What is our budget per user per month, and does that include add-on modules?
  9. Do we need conversation intelligence for customer success calls as well, or only sales?
  10. How many pipeline deals are active at any given time, and is that volume enough for AI-driven forecasting?

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Gong replace Clari for forecasting?

Gong Forecast is improving rapidly but is not yet at Clari's level for forecast accuracy. Gong's forecasting relies heavily on conversation signals and CRM data, while Clari incorporates a broader set of engagement signals (email, calendar, historical patterns). For teams that primarily need directional forecasting and pipeline visibility, Gong Forecast may be sufficient. For CROs who need high-accuracy forecasts to commit numbers to the board, Clari remains the stronger option.

Can Clari replace Gong for conversation intelligence?

Clari offers conversation intelligence, but it is not a direct replacement for Gong. The transcription quality, coaching tools, snippet sharing, and call analytics are less mature. If conversation intelligence is a nice-to-have complement to forecasting, Clari's built-in capabilities may suffice. If coaching and call analysis are primary use cases, Gong is the better product.

Do we need both Clari and Gong?

Many enterprise teams run both and find the combination more valuable than either alone. Gong provides the frontline coaching layer. Clari provides the executive forecasting layer. If budget is constrained, choose based on your highest-priority use case. If you can afford both, the combination gives you conversation-level and pipeline-level insights that neither tool delivers alone.

How long does it take to see ROI from each tool?

Gong delivers visible value within 2-4 weeks as managers start reviewing calls and sharing coaching clips. The ROI is harder to quantify but shows up in improved win rates and shorter ramp times. Clari takes 6-12 weeks to calibrate its forecasting models against your historical data. Once calibrated, the ROI shows up in more accurate forecasts, earlier identification of at-risk deals, and better pipeline coverage ratios.

Which tool works better with HubSpot?

Gong's HubSpot integration is more mature and widely deployed. Call recordings, transcripts, and insights sync to HubSpot deal and contact records cleanly. Clari's HubSpot integration exists but was built primarily for Salesforce environments. If you are a HubSpot shop, Gong is the safer choice. Clari will work, but you may encounter limitations in data sync depth and reporting flexibility.

What about privacy concerns with call recording?

Gong requires careful setup around recording consent. In two-party consent states (California, Illinois, etc.) and GDPR regions, you must configure disclosure prompts and opt-out mechanisms. Gong provides tools for this, but the legal and compliance setup is on your team. Clari's data collection is less sensitive since it primarily analyzes metadata, not recorded conversations. If privacy regulations are a significant concern in your sales territories, factor in the compliance overhead for Gong.

Can either tool work for customer success, not just sales?

Both tools are expanding into customer success use cases. Gong can record and analyze CS calls to identify churn risk signals, expansion opportunities, and product feedback themes. Clari can track renewal pipeline and forecast net revenue retention. Gong's CS use case is more mature and widely adopted. If you want a single platform for both sales and CS conversation analysis, Gong handles that better.

How do the tools handle team hierarchies and permissions?

Both platforms support team hierarchies for managers to view their direct reports' data. Clari's hierarchy management is more sophisticated because forecasting requires accurate org structure mapping for roll-up calculations. Gong's permissions are simpler: managers see their team's calls, reps see their own. For large, complex org structures with matrix reporting, Clari's hierarchy tools are more flexible.

How do Gong and Clari handle data from customer success calls?

Gong can record and analyze CS calls to identify churn risk, upsell opportunities, and product feedback trends. The analysis uses the same AI engine as sales calls but with different signal detection tuned for retention conversations. Clari can incorporate renewal pipeline and expansion revenue into its forecasting models, giving leadership visibility into net revenue retention alongside new business. For organizations that want a single view of revenue (new plus expansion plus renewal), Clari\'s forecasting scope is broader.

What is the typical ROI timeline for each tool?

Gong customers typically report measurable improvements in win rates (5-10% increase) and ramp time (20-30% faster for new hires) within 3-6 months. The ROI is driven by behavior change, so it depends on manager adoption of coaching workflows. Clari customers report forecast accuracy improvements of 15-25% within 2-3 quarters. The ROI is driven by better resource allocation and earlier identification of pipeline gaps. Both tools can demonstrate positive ROI within the first year if properly adopted.

Can I start with a limited pilot before full deployment?

Both vendors support pilot deployments. Gong pilots typically involve 1-2 sales teams (20-30 users) for 60-90 days. Clari pilots work best with an entire forecasting hierarchy (one VP and their managers and reps) to generate meaningful forecast accuracy data. Negotiate pilot pricing: both vendors will offer discounted rates for the pilot period, and you should secure pricing guarantees for full deployment before the pilot begins so you are not renegotiating after you are already invested.
