ZoomInfo vs Cognism

Side-by-side comparison for 2026. Which one is right for your team?

Last updated: 2026-04-23

ZoomInfo vs Cognism

ZoomInfo wins for North American data. Cognism wins for European data and GDPR compliance. If you sell into EMEA, Cognism. NA, ZoomInfo.

ZoomInfo and Cognism are both B2B data providers, but they serve different markets and operate under different philosophies. ZoomInfo is the dominant player in North America with the largest contact database (260M+ professional profiles) and a platform that has expanded well beyond data into orchestration, intent signals, and engagement tools. Cognism leads in European data quality and has built its brand around GDPR compliance and phone-verified mobile numbers.

The pricing gap between these two tools is significant. ZoomInfo's entry point is around $15,000/year for their Professional plan with limited credits, and most mid-market teams end up spending $25,000-$60,000/year for usable credit volumes. Cognism pricing starts lower, typically $15,000-$35,000/year for comparable team sizes, and includes unlimited access to their database in most plans rather than credit-based gating.

Data quality is the defining battleground. ZoomInfo has more records, period. But more records does not always mean better results. Cognism's Diamond Data (phone-verified mobile numbers) has a connect rate that is 2-3x higher than ZoomInfo's standard mobile numbers for European contacts. For North American contacts, ZoomInfo's data is generally more comprehensive and current.

The choice between these two often comes down to geography. If your sales team primarily targets North American companies, ZoomInfo is the default choice with deeper firmographic data, more technographic signals, and better coverage of the US mid-market. If you sell into Europe or have a global sales team that needs GDPR-compliant data, Cognism is the stronger option.

Both platforms have evolved in the past year. ZoomInfo launched Copilot, an AI assistant for prospecting workflows, and has continued expanding its intent data partnerships. Cognism has improved its North American data coverage and added more integration options. The competitive landscape is shifting, but the geographic strength differences remain.

For buyers evaluating both, the most productive approach is to run a data quality test. Export your target account list, request sample data from both vendors, and measure match rates, email accuracy, and phone connect rates against your specific ICP. Generic database size claims are meaningless without this validation step.

The data provider market is also increasingly fragmented, which changes the competitive dynamics. Clay\'s enrichment waterfall approach lets you query both ZoomInfo and Cognism (along with dozens of other providers) and take the best result for each contact. This means the choice between ZoomInfo and Cognism is no longer necessarily exclusive. Some teams use both through a data orchestration layer, optimizing for the best data per region and contact type.

Another factor worth considering is the sales process of each vendor. ZoomInfo\'s sales team is notoriously aggressive, with tactics that include long-term contract pressure, auto-renewal clauses, and upsell campaigns. Cognism\'s sales process is generally described as more straightforward and less high-pressure. While this should not be a primary decision factor, it does affect the ongoing vendor relationship and contract management experience.

Detailed Breakdown

Data Coverage (North America)

ZoomInfo has the deepest North American database, with 260M+ professional profiles, 100M+ company records, and extensive firmographic and technographic data. Coverage of US mid-market and enterprise companies is unmatched. Cognism's North American data has improved but still has gaps in smaller companies (under 200 employees) and niche industries. If 80%+ of your targets are North American, ZoomInfo provides better coverage.

Data Coverage (Europe)

Cognism leads in European data quality, particularly for phone-verified mobile numbers. Their Diamond Data offering delivers 2-3x higher connect rates on European contacts compared to ZoomInfo. GDPR compliance is built into Cognism's data collection methodology, which matters if your legal team is strict about data provenance. ZoomInfo has European data, but accuracy and freshness lag behind Cognism for most EU markets.

Data Accuracy

Cognism's phone-verified Diamond Data has measurably higher accuracy for mobile numbers. Their verification team manually calls and confirms numbers, which drives connect rates significantly higher than database-sourced numbers. ZoomInfo's email accuracy is strong (95%+ claimed), but mobile number accuracy varies widely by segment. For email-first outbound, both platforms are comparable. For phone-first outbound, Cognism's verified numbers are the differentiator.

Pricing and Credit Model

ZoomInfo uses a credit-based model where each contact export costs credits, and credit packages determine your effective cost per lead. Teams frequently run into credit limits mid-quarter. Cognism offers unlimited access plans for most tiers, meaning you can export as many contacts as needed without counting credits. For high-volume outbound teams (1,000+ contacts/month), Cognism's unlimited model is significantly more cost-effective. ZoomInfo's minimum spend starts around $15K/year; Cognism starts around $15K/year but delivers more contacts per dollar.

Intent Data

ZoomInfo's intent data offering (powered by Bombora partnership and their own signals) is more established and covers a broader set of intent topics. The platform can trigger workflows based on companies researching specific topics, which is valuable for ABM strategies. Cognism has intent data through a Bombora partnership as well, but ZoomInfo's integration of intent signals into prospecting workflows is more mature. If intent data is central to your go-to-market strategy, ZoomInfo has the edge.

Platform and Workflow Tools

ZoomInfo has expanded into a full go-to-market platform with engagement tools (Engage), website visitor tracking (WebSights), chat (Chat), and orchestration workflows. It is trying to be an all-in-one prospecting and engagement solution. Cognism remains focused on data, with a cleaner, simpler interface for finding and exporting contacts. If you want a single platform for data plus engagement, ZoomInfo offers that. If you already have engagement tools and just need high-quality data, Cognism's focused approach is preferable.

Compliance and GDPR

Cognism was built with GDPR compliance as a foundational principle. Their data collection includes consent tracking, DNC list checking, and compliant sourcing methodologies. ZoomInfo has invested in compliance features (including a privacy center and opt-out mechanisms), but the data collection approach is broader and has faced more regulatory scrutiny. For companies with strict compliance requirements, particularly those selling into EU markets, Cognism's approach provides more defensible data sourcing.

CRM Integration

Both tools integrate with Salesforce, HubSpot, and other major CRMs. ZoomInfo's integrations are deeper, with bi-directional sync, automated data enrichment, and workflow triggers. Cognism's integrations cover the core export-to-CRM workflow well, but lack some of the advanced automation options. For teams that want automated data enrichment running in the background, ZoomInfo's integration layer is more sophisticated.

Customer Community

ZoomInfo has a larger customer base and consequently a larger community of users sharing best practices, templates, and workflows. Third-party training resources, consultants, and agencies that specialize in ZoomInfo are widely available. Cognism's community is smaller but growing, particularly in the European market. For teams that want peer learning and external support resources, ZoomInfo's ecosystem is more developed.

The Bottom Line

ZoomInfo is the default choice for North American-focused B2B sales teams. The database is the largest, the platform tools are the most mature, and the ecosystem of integrations and third-party support is unmatched. If 80%+ of your pipeline comes from the US and Canada, and your budget supports $25K-$60K/year, ZoomInfo delivers the most comprehensive data and workflow solution.

Cognism wins for teams selling into Europe or running global outbound operations. The GDPR-compliant data sourcing, phone-verified mobile numbers, and unlimited access pricing model make it the smarter investment for these use cases. The 2-3x higher connect rate on Diamond Data numbers is not a marketing claim; it is a measurable difference that directly impacts SDR productivity.

For a startup with fewer than 5 SDRs, skip both and use Apollo.io until you have validated your outbound motion. The $15K+ annual minimum for either tool is too much when you are still figuring out your ICP and messaging.

For a growth-stage team with 10-30 SDRs, pick based on geography and run a data quality test before signing. Request sample data for 200-300 accounts from your actual target list and measure match rates, email accuracy, and phone connect rates. The vendor whose data matches your specific ICP better will deliver better results, regardless of total database size.

For enterprise teams with global operations, consider running both tools with geographic segmentation. The combined $50K-$100K annual investment is justified if your outbound motion generates enough pipeline to support it. Set clear data governance rules so your CRM does not become a mess of conflicting records.

The bottom line: ZoomInfo for North America, Cognism for Europe, and a data test for everyone. Do not sign an annual contract based on a demo and a database size claim. Validate against your actual target accounts first.

One emerging trend worth watching: both ZoomInfo and Cognism are investing in AI-powered prospecting features that go beyond static data. ZoomInfo\'s Copilot and Cognism\'s AI recommendations are early attempts at using AI to prioritize accounts and suggest outreach strategies. These features are not mature enough to influence a purchase decision today, but they signal where the market is heading.

For organizations with strict data governance requirements, also evaluate how each vendor handles data deletion requests, opt-out compliance, and data lineage documentation. Cognism\'s GDPR-first approach makes this simpler for European operations. ZoomInfo provides compliance tools but requires more proactive management to maintain compliance across all data usage scenarios.

One practical tip regardless of which vendor you choose: negotiate a data quality guarantee into your contract. Request a minimum match rate for your ICP and a mechanism for credits or refunds if accuracy falls below the agreed threshold. Both vendors will resist this, but it sets the right expectations and gives you recourse if the data underperforms.

Which Is Right for Your Stage?

Startups & SMBs

If you are pre-revenue or under $2M ARR, neither ZoomInfo nor Cognism is the right investment. Use Apollo.io ($49-$99/user/month) or LinkedIn Sales Navigator ($99/month) for prospecting. The data quality is lower, but the cost is 90% less. Once you have validated your ICP and have a repeatable outbound motion generating pipeline, then evaluate ZoomInfo or Cognism. Most startups reach this point around 5-10 SDRs and $3M+ ARR. Before that, the annual minimums ($15K+) are hard to justify. If you do decide to invest in a data provider at the startup stage, Apollo.io\'s free tier (600 email credits/year) is the true starting point. Once you exhaust that, Apollo\'s paid plans ($49-$119/user/month) provide adequate data for early outbound. LinkedIn Sales Navigator ($99/month) is another cost-effective option for manual prospecting. The jump to ZoomInfo or Cognism should happen only when you have a proven outbound motion and need data at a scale that justifies the $15K+ annual commitment.

Growth Stage

This is the sweet spot for both tools. Choose based on your target geography. If 80%+ of your pipeline comes from North America, ZoomInfo's data coverage and platform tools justify the higher price. Budget $25K-$50K/year and negotiate hard on credit volumes. If you sell globally or have significant European pipeline, Cognism's data quality and unlimited access model deliver better ROI. Budget $15K-$30K/year. Run a data test with both vendors before committing, using 200-300 target accounts from your actual ICP. At the growth stage, also consider the team workflow impact. ZoomInfo\'s broader platform (engagement tools, intent signals, website visitor tracking) means fewer tools in your stack. Cognism\'s focused approach means you will need separate tools for engagement and intent. Calculate the total stack cost, not just the data cost. A $30K/year ZoomInfo subscription that replaces $10K/year in other tools has a real net cost of $20K. Similarly, Cognism at $20K/year plus $10K in additional tools equals the same total spend with potentially better data quality.

Enterprise

Enterprise teams often run both tools to maximize coverage. ZoomInfo for North American prospecting and intent data, Cognism for European outreach and phone-verified numbers. The combined spend ($50K-$100K/year) is justified if your TAM spans both regions. If budget requires one tool, choose based on where 70%+ of your revenue comes from. At enterprise scale, negotiate aggressively on multi-year commitments and ask for custom credit pools or unlimited access tiers that are not listed on the standard pricing page. Enterprise negotiations should include a data quality SLA. Ask both vendors to guarantee minimum match rates and accuracy levels for your specific ICP, with credit or refund provisions if the data underperforms. ZoomInfo is more likely to agree to performance guarantees for large contracts. Cognism\'s Diamond Data already has implicit quality guarantees through the phone verification process. Also negotiate for a dedicated customer success manager and quarterly business reviews to monitor data quality trends over time.

Questions to Ask Before Choosing

  1. What percentage of our target accounts are in North America versus Europe versus other regions?
  2. Is our outbound strategy phone-first or email-first?
  3. How many contacts per month does our SDR team need to source?
  4. Do we have GDPR compliance requirements that restrict how we source contact data?
  5. What is our budget for sales intelligence data, and does that include engagement tools or just data?
  6. Have we validated our ICP well enough to justify a $15K+ annual data investment?
  7. Do we need intent data signals to prioritize accounts, or is our targeting criteria sufficient?
  8. How important is phone-verified mobile number accuracy for our connect rates?
  9. Do we already have engagement tools (Outreach, Salesloft), or do we need the data platform to include outbound sequencing?
  10. What is our current email deliverability rate, and would higher-accuracy data meaningfully improve it?

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ZoomInfo worth the price premium over Cognism?

For North American-focused teams, typically yes. ZoomInfo's data coverage and platform depth justify the premium if your pipeline is primarily US and Canada. The intent data, technographic signals, and workflow automation tools add value beyond raw contact data. For European-focused teams, no. Cognism's data quality in EU markets is demonstrably better, and the pricing is more accessible. For global teams, the answer depends on the revenue split between regions.

How accurate are ZoomInfo's phone numbers compared to Cognism's?

Cognism's Diamond Data (phone-verified mobile numbers) connects at 2-3x the rate of ZoomInfo's standard mobile numbers for European contacts. For North American contacts, the gap narrows. ZoomInfo's phone accuracy in the US is reasonable (40-60% connect rate on mobile numbers), while Cognism's Diamond Data achieves 70-80% connect rates on verified numbers. The catch is that Cognism's Diamond Data coverage is smaller; not every contact has a verified number.

Can we use both ZoomInfo and Cognism at the same time?

Yes, and many global sales teams do exactly this. Use ZoomInfo as your primary source for North American contacts, intent data, and technographic signals. Use Cognism for European contacts and phone-verified mobile numbers. The main challenge is deduplication and data hygiene in your CRM. Set clear rules for which tool is the source of truth for which regions to avoid conflicting data.

How do ZoomInfo credits work, and do we always run out?

ZoomInfo credits are consumed when you export contacts or reveal specific data points. The credit allocation depends on your plan tier. Most teams underestimate their credit needs and run out mid-quarter, which either means paying for additional credits or pausing outbound until the next quarter. When negotiating, push for 30-50% more credits than ZoomInfo's sales team recommends. Alternatively, ask about unlimited export tiers, which are available but not always offered upfront.

Which tool has better Salesforce integration?

ZoomInfo's Salesforce integration is more comprehensive, with automated data enrichment, lead routing, and workflow triggers built in. Cognism integrates with Salesforce for contact export and basic enrichment but lacks the advanced automation layer. If you want a set-it-and-forget-it enrichment engine that keeps your CRM data current automatically, ZoomInfo is the better choice. If you primarily need a tool for SDRs to search and export contacts manually, both integrations are sufficient.

Is Cognism's North American data good enough to skip ZoomInfo?

Cognism's North American data has improved significantly in the past two years, but it still trails ZoomInfo in coverage for smaller companies, niche industries, and technographic data. If your ICP is Fortune 500 or large mid-market companies in well-covered industries (SaaS, financial services, healthcare), Cognism's NA data may be sufficient. If you target SMBs or niche verticals, ZoomInfo's broader database is more likely to have the contacts you need.

How long do annual contracts last, and can we cancel early?

Both ZoomInfo and Cognism typically require annual commitments. ZoomInfo contracts are notoriously difficult to cancel early, and auto-renewal clauses are standard. Read the fine print carefully and calendar your renewal date 90 days in advance to avoid auto-renewal. Cognism's contracts are generally more flexible, with some monthly options available for smaller teams. For both vendors, negotiate a 90-day cancellation notice clause and avoid multi-year commitments unless the discount is substantial (20%+).

What alternatives should we consider besides ZoomInfo and Cognism?

Apollo.io is the leading budget alternative with a free tier and paid plans starting at $49/user/month. Data quality is lower than both ZoomInfo and Cognism, but adequate for many teams. Lusha is another mid-market option with strong European data. RocketReach and smooth.AI compete on pricing but have weaker data accuracy. For intent data specifically, Bombora can be purchased independently. The right alternative depends on your budget, data volume needs, and accuracy requirements.

How often does each vendor refresh their data?

ZoomInfo claims to reverify data continuously using a combination of automated systems and their contributor network (professionals who install a browser extension that shares anonymized contact data). In practice, data freshness varies by segment. Well-known companies and senior executives are updated frequently. Smaller companies and mid-level contacts can go months between updates. Cognism\'s Diamond Data is refreshed through manual phone verification, which means verified numbers are accurate at the time of verification but may not be re-verified for 6-12 months.

Can I use these tools for account-based marketing, not just outbound sales?

Both tools support ABM strategies. ZoomInfo\'s intent data and company-level signals are particularly valuable for ABM, letting marketing teams identify accounts showing buying intent and target them with specific campaigns. Cognism\'s contact-level data supports ABM by providing verified contacts within target accounts for direct outreach. ZoomInfo is the stronger ABM platform due to its intent data depth and integration with marketing automation tools like Marketo and HubSpot.

What is the typical contract structure, and can I get monthly billing?

Both vendors prefer annual contracts. ZoomInfo\'s minimum commitment is typically annual with limited monthly options. Cognism offers more flexibility, with some monthly plans available for smaller teams. For annual contracts, both vendors offer 10-20% discounts for multi-year commitments. The key negotiation point is auto-renewal: both vendors include auto-renewal clauses by default. Insist on a 90-day cancellation notice window and calendar your renewal date immediately upon signing.
